# What it is
VR therapy platform for autism and other neurodivergent users. 175+ immersive lessons covering social communication, behavior, daily life skills, joint attention, calming sensory spaces. Runs on Meta Quest headsets or smartphones. Designed to be paired with a coach (parent, RBT, BCBA, SLP) on a tablet who controls the lesson.

# Pricing
- Individual — USD 50 CAD/month (no annual option).
- School pricing — tiered by enrollment, choose 6-month or academic-year.
- Clinic pricing — tiered, monthly or annual.

# Features
- 175+ VR lessons.
- Coach-companion device for parent/clinician to guide.
- Two sensory calm spaces (aquarium, crystal cave).
- AI personalization for lesson recommendation.
- NIMH SBIR funding pedigree.
- Targets joint attention, social skills, life skills (crossing the street, ordering food, etc.).

# Strengths
- The serious VR autism platform. No real competitors at this scope in immersive therapy.
- Coach-driven model is the right design — VR alone for autistic kids is risky; coached VR is plausible therapy.
- Clinician adoption growing; ABS Kids and other large ABA providers piloting.
- Some published feasibility study on joint attention module (Floreo + JA module).

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Hardware barrier: family or clinic must own a VR headset (~USD 300+).
- Not all autistic users tolerate VR — sensory and proprioceptive differences make a meaningful subset unable to use it.
- US/Canada only.
- USD 50/month per user is steep for individual use.
- Limited Spanish content; English-first.
- Library is curated; can't author own lessons.
- VR session duration constraints (15-20 minutes max) — hard to integrate into a daily routine.

# Evidence base
- Joint Attention Module pilot study published 2019 (Halabi et al., PMC6792024). Feasibility, not efficacy.
- NIMH SBIR Phase II funding.

# Geographic / language coverage
- English.
- US/Canada distribution.
