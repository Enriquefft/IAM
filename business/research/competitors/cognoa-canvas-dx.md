# What it is
First FDA-authorized autism diagnosis aid (Software as a Medical Device). Caregiver answers questions and uploads two videos via a mobile app; clinician fills a separate questionnaire in a web portal; an ML model produces a positive/negative/indeterminate prediction for ASD in kids 18-72 months. Used by primary-care pediatricians as a screening accelerator.

# Pricing
- USD 2,280 average wholesale price per evaluation (per NY State Medicaid analysis).
- In-network with Highmark and other US payors.
- Not direct-to-consumer; clinicians prescribe.

# Features
- Caregiver-facing iOS/Android app for video and questionnaire submission.
- Clinician-facing web portal.
- Diagnosis aid (not standalone diagnostic) for ASD in 18-72 month old children at risk for developmental delay.
- ML-based prediction with three possible outputs: positive, negative, indeterminate.

# Strengths
- Only FDA-authorized autism dx aid (De Novo, June 2021).
- Insurance-reimbursable in US — a real revenue path that wellness apps don't have.
- Reduces dx wait time from 1-3 years (typical US specialist queue) to weeks.
- Validated in a pivotal trial.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Only US — FDA authorization is jurisdictional. No equivalent regulatory path executed in EU, LATAM.
- Indeterminate output rate is high (substantial percentage of evaluations don't yield a positive or negative).
- USD 2,280 price excludes uninsured families and most LATAM markets entirely.
- Not used by parents on their own — pediatrician must order and submit.
- Narrow scope (dx aid only); no follow-on intervention product.
- Only English in the US release; Spanish caregiver app version is in development per public statements but not GA.

# Evidence base
