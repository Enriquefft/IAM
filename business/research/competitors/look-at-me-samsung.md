# What it is
Free Android-only app from Samsung's design team and Korean clinical psychologists / psychiatrists. Camera-and-game format trains kids to make eye contact and interpret facial expressions. Seven scientifically-derived missions (the first four with 18 levels each). Pilot trial of 20 kids over 8 weeks reported eye-contact and emotion-decoding improvements.

# Pricing
- Free. Android only. No ads, no IAPs.

# Features
- Camera-based interactive games.
- Facial recognition.
- Train eye contact, recognize emotions, remember faces.
- 7 missions, 4 of which have 18 progressive levels.
- Self-portrait emotion practice.

# Strengths
- Free.
- Backed by Samsung's design and CSR resources.
- Pilot trial showed positive directional results: 60% eye contact improvement, 40% emotion expression improvement, 20% emotion decoding improvement.
- Genuinely innovative use of the smartphone camera.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Android only. Iso users locked out.
- Last meaningful update years ago; effectively a finished CSR project.
- Pilot trial was small (n=20) and uncontrolled.
- "Eye contact" as a learning target is contested in autism acceptance literature — overemphasis can be harmful per some autistic self-advocates.
- Korean-design choices show in UI.
- No data product, no parent dashboard, no clinician access.
- Limited language localization; Korean and English primary.

# Evidence base
- Internal Samsung pilot study at Seoul National University and Yonsei University, n=20, 8 weeks.

# Geographic / language coverage
- Korean and English; some other languages.
- Free worldwide via Google Play.
