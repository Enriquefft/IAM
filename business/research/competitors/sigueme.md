# What it is
Visual attention training app for low-functioning autism, built by University of Granada with Fundación Orange and the Purísima Concepción Foundation. Six progressive phases (Attention, Video, Image, Drawing, Pictogram, Games) move the kid from raw visual stimulation toward symbolic understanding.

# Pricing
- Free. iOS, Android, Windows, Linux.

# Features
- Six progressive phases of visual stimulus complexity.
- Phase 1 (Attention): animated geometric stimuli (spirals, lines, circles) with sound.
- Customizable: replace built-in images, videos, sounds with caregiver content.
- Runs on tablets, phones, PCs, interactive whiteboards.

# Strengths
- Spanish-first.
- Designed explicitly for severe-end autism (low cognitive level), a market most apps ignore.
- Pilot study with 85 students across 18 Spanish centers showed measurable improvement in visual attention and cause-effect understanding — so there is real, if small, evidence.
- Cross-platform including desktop OS.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Last meaningful update years ago; design is visibly dated.
- Single-purpose; doesn't span beyond visual attention.
- Not in active marketing; discovery only via Spanish autism associations.
- No data export, no progress tracking — clinicians cannot use it as an assessment tool.
- No professional dashboard.
- Pilot study is not a rigorous RCT.
- Limited language localization beyond Spanish.

# Evidence base
- Internal pilot study (Granada) on 85 students; not peer-reviewed RCT.

# Geographic / language coverage
- Spanish.
- Free worldwide.
