# What it is
AAC app from PRC-Saltillo built on the Language Acquisition through Motor Planning (LAMP) approach. Distinctive design choice: every word lives in a fixed motor location across all three vocabulary levels (Transition Level 1, 2, 3), so the user builds muscle memory on the same gesture as language grows. Specifically marketed at autistic non-verbal kids.

# Pricing
- LAMP Words for Life — USD 299.99 one-time (App Store).
- 50% volume discount for purchases of 20+ via Apple School Manager.
- LAMP WFL Discover — free 30-day trial app (reverts to lite-tech board after).
- No subscription tier, no add-ons.

# Features
- 4,000+ core words organized in Unity vocabulary system.
- Three developmentally progressive vocabulary pages with motor consistency across levels.
- Vocabulary Builder: lets a clinician focus practice on selected target words by graying others.
- Symbol library: SymbolStix paired with text.
- Switch scanning support.
- Support for keyguards and TouchGuide overlays.

# Strengths
- The fixed-motor philosophy is a genuine differentiator — kids who progress through levels do not have to relearn locations.
- Strong autism-specific positioning; the LAMP approach has its own training certification through The Center for AAC & Autism.
- Solid SLP advocacy in the autism community.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- iOS/iPadOS only.
- Vocabulary is English-first; no Spanish equivalent of the Unity vocabulary. A "Spanish UI" does exist for the app shell but the page set is built for English semantic and morphological structure.
- USD 299.99 is steep one-time hit with no trial of the full app.
- No tracking, no schedule, no emotion content, no parent dashboard. Pure speech output.
- Single approach (LAMP) — if it doesn't fit the kid, the app doesn't pivot.
- Practically zero presence in LATAM markets; LAMP certification courses run almost exclusively in English.

# Evidence base
- LAMP approach is supported by single-subject design literature; no large RCTs.
- Endorsed by The Center for AAC & Autism (a PRC-Saltillo-affiliated org).

# Geographic / language coverage
- English only for the vocabulary content. App UI translated but vocabulary not localized.
- US-centric distribution.
