# What it is
Tracking and journaling app for parents of autistic / special-needs kids. Logs mood, sleep, food, medications, behaviors, therapies, appointments. Dashboard surfaces patterns and triggers. Designed specifically for the "I have to remember everything" load on parents.

# Pricing
- Free tier exists.
- Premium — USD 19/month.
- Multi-collaborator (parents, teachers, aides, providers) supported.

# Status note
- Verify current operational status before relying on this product. Multiple references in 2024-2025 indicate Birdhouse was being wound down due to platform issues. Current sites still serve the marketing pages but signups should be reverified.

# Features
- Daily log: sleep, mood, meltdowns, appointments, food, medications, supplements, therapies.
- Pattern surfacing across days/weeks.
- Skill-progress tracking with shareable / printable reports.
- Multi-user collaboration: invite caregivers, teachers, aides.
- Read/write permissions per collaborator.

# Strengths
- The strongest parent-side journaling product in the autism category. Few real competitors at this scope.
- Multi-collaborator design recognizes that information about a kid lives across many adults.
- Pattern surfacing is actionable for medical visits.
- Skill tracking turns the diary into a quasi-clinical tool.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Operational status uncertain (see above).
- English only.
- Manual data entry — no passive sensors, no integration with the kid's actual schedule app.
- USD 19/month is steep for a journal-only product.
- Not designed as a clinician tool — exports are PDFs, not structured data.
- No AAC, no schedule, no regulation features.

# Evidence base
- Marketing case studies, no published trials.

# Geographic / language coverage
- English.
