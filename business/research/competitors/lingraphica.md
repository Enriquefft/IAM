# What it is
Long-running US AAC company. Sells dedicated speech-generating devices ("AAC devices") for people with aphasia, autism, and other complex communication needs. Devices come with the company's proprietary software (Hub: Talk, Type, Draw, Media). Reimbursement-funded model: Medicare, Medicaid, VA, private insurance.

# Pricing
- Devices typically USD 4,000-7,000 — covered by insurance for qualifying US users.
- No published direct-pay price point.

# Features
- Proprietary "Hub" software with four expression modes: Talk (cards), Type (sentences), Draw (visual), Media (memories).
- Real photos and realistic drawings (rather than abstract pictograms).
- Custom social stories.
- Video modeling integration.
- Free downloadable companion apps for assessment, screening.
- Dedicated hardware tablets pre-loaded.

# Strengths
- Long-standing reputation in adult AAC (originally aphasia-focused, expanded to autism).
- Insurance funding pipeline — Lingraphica handles benefits investigation, reduces friction for US families.
- Photo-realistic icon style is more accessible than abstract pictograms for some autistic users.
- Dedicated hardware sidesteps consumer-tablet distractions.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- US/Canada only; insurance-funded model does not transfer to LATAM private-pay markets.
- High device cost outside of insurance.
- English only.
- Hub UI is enterprise-grade; not parent-friendly out of the box.
- Heavier on adult / aphasia than child / autism, though autism is a growth segment.
- No data product around outcomes.

# Evidence base
- Several aphasia-intervention studies; smaller autism evidence.

# Geographic / language coverage
- English; US/CA distribution.
