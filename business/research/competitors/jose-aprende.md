# What it is
Free interactive picture-book app from Aprendices Visuales (a Spanish non-profit). 15 short illustrated stories featuring a character, José, used to teach autonomy routines: bathing, dressing, eating, brushing teeth, using the toilet, riding the bus, going to a birthday party, etc. Stories are pictogram-supported and tactile — illustrations animate when touched. Distributed under Creative Commons.

# Pricing
- Free, both iOS and Android. No ads, no IAPs.

# Features
- 15 pictogram-supported stories.
- Tactile illustrations: pictures animate when tapped.
- Auto-read mode and self-paced mode.
- Backed by Fundación Orange (distribution and funding).
- Stories also available as printable PDFs at aprendicesvisuales.org.

# Strengths
- Spanish-first content created from scratch (not translated).
- Story-driven format teaches social/autonomy concepts that grids alone cannot — narrative + repetition.
- Creative Commons licensing, so therapists and schools may reuse and adapt freely.
- Strong community recognition in Spanish-speaking autism world; UNICEF Emprende 2013 award.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Catalog is fixed at 15 stories; no new content cadence.
- Stories are aimed at preschool / early elementary; little for older kids.
- Read-only experience — no AAC, no schedule, no tracking.
- No measurement of whether the kid understood or applied the routine.
- Standalone app; no integration with any other tool.
- UI is functional but dated.

# Evidence base
- Anecdotal case studies on Aprendices Visuales site; no published trial.

# Geographic / language coverage
- Castilian Spanish text and audio. No Latin American Spanish localization (vocabulary differences exist).
- Some stories also in English and Portuguese.
- Free worldwide via App Store and Google Play.
