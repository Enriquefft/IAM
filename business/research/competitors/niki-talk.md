# What it is
Family of AAC apps from Italian developer Alessandro La Rocca. Three concurrent products: Niki Talk (legacy free with IAPs), Niki Talk 2 (free with IAPs), Niki Talk 2 Pro (paid). Symbol-based grid AAC plus a small ecosystem of related apps (Niki Diary, Niki Words, Niki Music, Niki Agenda, Niki First Then).

# Pricing
- Niki Talk (original) — free with IAPs.
- Niki Talk 2 — free with IAPs: Voice USD 59.99, Les Pictogrammes USD 19.99, Multi-User USD 11.99.
- Niki Talk 2 Pro — USD 89.99 one-time.

# Features
- Multi-user pages.
- Customizable boards with multiple page levels.
- Buttons can link to other pages, video, or web URLs.
- Symbol libraries: [ARASAAC](arasaac.md), Les Pictogrammes (paid).
- TTS in 31 languages, 70 voices.
- Verb conjugation.
- Voice recording per cell.
- Companion apps for diary, schedule, music.

# Strengths
- Cross-platform: iOS and Android.
- Cheaper than the US incumbents.
- Multi-user mode means one family device can serve siblings or one school iPad can serve a class.
- Italian dev, so natively localized for Romance languages including Spanish, French, Italian, Portuguese.
- Companion app suite (diary, music, agenda) extends beyond pure AAC.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- App ecosystem is fragmented and confusing — three "Niki Talk" apps in stores plus the companion apps, with overlapping but not identical features.
- IAP model on the free tier obscures total cost.
- UX is dated; visuals feel circa-2014.
- Single-developer product; cadence of updates is slow and roadmap is opaque.
- No data collection, no parent dashboard, no progress tracking.
- No clinical evidence base.
- No Spanish-language onboarding documentation, despite supporting Spanish.

# Evidence base
- None published.

# Geographic / language coverage
