# What it is
Long-running US publisher of video modeling content for autistic kids. Several free apps (Model Me Going Places, Model Me Faces & Emotions) for iPad/iPhone covering social skills via short video clips. Plus a paid online subscription with a broader social skills curriculum.

# Pricing
- Free apps (Going Places, Faces & Emotions) — free, App Store.
- Online subscription — 7-day free trial; pricing on modelmekids.com/prices/. `Estimate:` USD 99-200/year individual.
- DVD products from earlier era still sold.

# Features
- Short video clips of typically-developing peers in social situations: store, doctor, playground, hairstylist.
- Faces & Emotions: photos of expressions paired with naming exercises.
- Video modeling pedagogy (peer-modeled behavior).

# Strengths
- Free apps are a low-friction starting point.
- Video modeling has solid evidence base in autism intervention.
- Long track record (since 2002) — predates most of the category.
- Schools and SLPs frequently use Model Me content as supplement.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Apps are static video catalogs from years ago — no cadence of new content.
- No interactivity.
- English only.
- US-only narrative content (American settings, accents).
- Apps don't generate data; passive viewing only.
- Online subscription pricing not transparent on the public landing page.
- Production values dated.

# Evidence base
- Video modeling itself has substantial evidence base in autism intervention.
- No RCT specific to Model Me Kids materials.

# Geographic / language coverage
- English.
- App Store worldwide.
