# What it is
Visual personal diary / calendar app for autistic users. Funded and distributed by Fundación Orange (Spain). Each day is a sequence of activities, each activity gets a pictogram or photo, the time, the people involved, and an optional voice note. Visualization toggles between year, month, week, day, list.

# Pricing
- Free, no ads, no IAPs.
- Funded by Fundación Orange CSR program.

# Features
- Visual schedule that doubles as a personal diary.
- Pictogram library bundled ([ARASAAC](arasaac.md)).
- Photo and audio note attachments per activity.
- Multi-view calendar (year / month / week / day / list).
- Multi-user device support.
- Offline.

# Strengths
- Spanish-first by origin and authoring.
- Free.
- Dual-use as agenda (forward-looking) and journal (back-looking) is unusual and useful — gives a parent or SLP a chronological log without setup.
- Backed by a foundation (no churn risk from VC pressure).

# Weaknesses / gaps
- iOS only (despite older Android version, current focus is iOS — verify before relying on Android).
- Development cadence is slow; visibly older UI.
- No data analytics: the diary is for human review, not aggregated.
- No structured behavioral or emotion tracking (it is freeform text/photo per slot).
- No sync between devices; backups are manual.
- Not designed for professional use; no clinician dashboard.
- No integrations with AAC, no link from a schedule cell into a communication board.

# Evidence base
- Used in Spanish autism associations; no clinical RCT.

# Geographic / language coverage
- Spanish UI and pictograms.
- Available across LATAM via App Store.
