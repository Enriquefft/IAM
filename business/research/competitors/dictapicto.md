# What it is
Free voice-to-pictogram translator app from Fundación Orange (developed by BJ-Adaptaciones and Doble Equipo). Speak (or type) a sentence; the app converts it to ARASAAC pictograms instantly. Used to communicate with autistic users in real time when the speaking partner doesn't know AAC.

# Pricing
- Free. iOS and Android. No ads, no IAPs.

# Features
- Voice input → speech-to-text → pictogram sequence.
- ARASAAC vocabulary corpus.
- Configurable display: pictogram only, pictogram + small text, pictogram + large text.
- Backup/restore of personalized vocabulary; portable to other devices.
- Personalized vocabulary additions.

# Strengths
- A genuinely novel use case — the speaking adult uses the app, not (only) the autistic person. Fills the receptive-language gap.
- Spanish-first, free, well-funded by Orange.
- Pictograms come from ARASAAC, native Spanish association.
- Cross-platform.
- Does one thing very well.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Single-purpose; not an AAC, not a schedule, not a tracker.
- Speech recognition quality depends on OS engine; LATAM Spanish accents can be inconsistent.
- No analytics, no team mode.
- Slow update cadence.
- No equivalent for English speakers in the same form.

# Evidence base
- None published.

# Geographic / language coverage
- Spanish (ES + LatAm dialects depending on phone).
- Free worldwide.
