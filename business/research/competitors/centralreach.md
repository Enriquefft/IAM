# What it is
The market-leading practice-management EHR for ABA and IDD providers in the US. Bundles scheduling, ABA data collection (DTT, task analysis, frequency, rate, etc.), clinical notes, billing, claims, RBT training, parent portal, analytics. Used by 4,000+ orgs and 200,000+ clinicians.

# Pricing
- USD 59/user/month, billed when first client added.
- ABLLS-R / AFLS Starter — USD 120/learner/year.
- Modular pricing; total bill scales with add-ons.

# Features
- AI-powered scheduling (ScheduleAI).
- AI-drafted notes (NoteDraftAI).
- Discrete trial, task analysis, frequency, rate, duration, ABC behavioral data collection.
- Parent portal for session info.
- Billing + claims with payor integrations.
- 1,500+ pre-built program templates.
- RBT certification training.
- HIPAA-compliant.

# Strengths
- Market dominance — the default ABA EHR a US BCBA learns on.
- Breadth of integration (clinical + billing + training in one system).
- Aggressive AI investment (scheduling, notes).
- Acquired Behavior Science Technology, GoTab, etc. — consolidation play.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- US-only; no Spanish UI; no LATAM customers.
- Per-user pricing is a barrier for solo or small clinics outside US private-insurance market.
- Designed for ABA workflow specifically; SLPs and OTs find it heavy.
- No parent-facing tools beyond a portal — kid does not use the product directly.
- Reputation in the autism community: ABA-aligned tooling, which subsets of the autistic community oppose; CentralReach is not engaging with neurodiversity critique publicly.
- Implementation complexity is high — typical onboarding is weeks.
- Wellness/education-first competitors won't displace it as the EHR; the wedge is at the parent/kid end.

# Evidence base
- Tooling is regulatory-compliant infrastructure, not clinical claim.
