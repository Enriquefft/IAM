# What it is
Visual schedule app made in Málaga, Spain, by independent developer Lorenzo Moreno. Drag pictograms ([ARASAAC](arasaac.md) + 15,000+ symbols + own photos) into a sequence; the app shows progression through the schedule with a marker for current activity.

# Pricing
- Android — free on Google Play.
- iOS — paid one-time (price varies by region; published as low-cost: ~USD 2.99 in App Store).

# Features
- Drag-to-sequence visual schedules.
- 15,000+ pictogram library plus photo upload.
- Up to 48 pictograms per agenda.
- Progress marker through the sequence.
- Multilingual UI: Spanish, English, French, Portuguese, Italian, German.

# Strengths
- Lightweight, simple UI suitable for parents without training.
- Spanish-first content with multiple-language UI.
- Cross-platform (Android free, iOS paid but cheap).
- Established in the Spanish-speaking autism community.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Single-developer product; release cadence is slow.
- Only does visual schedules — no AAC, no diary, no analytics.
- No multi-user / family sharing.
- No backup or sync between devices.
- No clinician access mode.
- iOS pricing model is unpublished on the official site.

# Evidence base
- None published.

# Geographic / language coverage
- 6 UI languages with native-quality Spanish.
- Available via App Store and Google Play.

# Usage signal
- ~1.1K installs/month on Google Play (AppBrain, 2026-05-02). Tiny absolute volume even after 11 years on the market. Mixed reviews citing functional bugs.
- Single-developer cadence; releases driven by Lorenzo Moreno alone.
