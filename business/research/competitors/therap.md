# What it is
Long-running EHR specifically for IDD (intellectual and developmental disabilities) and human-services providers. Used by group homes, day programs, school health services, autism programs, early intervention, and home-care agencies. Comprehensive: ISP/IPP plans, EVV (electronic visit verification), Medication administration, T-Logs, billing.

# Pricing
- Contact for pricing; opaque.
- Quoted per-business-need.

# Features
- ISP / Person-Centered Plan documentation.
- Goal and outcome tracking.
- EVV (Electronic Visit Verification) — required for Medicaid HCBS billing.
- Medication administration records.
- T-Logs (daily progress notes).
- Behavior support data.
- Incident reporting.
- Workplace training tracking.
- Billing.

# Strengths
- The default IDD-services EHR in the US for group homes and day programs (broader IDD market than autism specifically).
- Multi-country presence (US, India, Canada, Ireland, Australia).
- EVV compliance built-in — solves a regulatory-headache feature.
- Scales from small agency to multi-state organization.
- 20+ year track record.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Pricing opacity is severe.
- UI is unmistakably enterprise-1990s.
- Heavy implementation overhead.
- ABA-specific data collection is weaker than CentralReach.
- No parent or kid-facing product — purely staff-facing.
- English only in user-facing modules.

# Evidence base
- Compliance tool.

# Geographic / language coverage
- English; US/IN/CA/IE/AU.
