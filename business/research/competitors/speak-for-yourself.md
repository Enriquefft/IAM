# What it is
iPad AAC app developed by SLPs Renee Collender and Heidi Lostracco. Single-page motor-planning vocabulary: every word is in one fixed motor location, never paginates between sub-pages. Marketed as the lowest-key-press-count AAC app. Two-keystroke max for any of 13,000+ words.

# Pricing
- Regular price USD 299.99 one-time.
- Annual Autism Acceptance sale (March/April) discounts to ~USD 149.99.

# Features
- 13,000+ word core vocabulary.
- 2-keystroke max for any word.
- Motor-planning consistent layout across users.
- Babble feature — child can explore without consequence.
- Customizable.
- Symbol library.
- Switch access via iOS.

# Strengths
- The motor-planning approach matters for kids who plateau on grid-paginated apps.
- One-time price (no subscription).
- Designed by practicing SLPs.
- Strong autism-community endorsement, especially in US east-coast SLP networks.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- iPad only.
- English only.
- USD 299.99 entry price is a barrier; sale is once a year.
- UI looks dated.
- No data tracking.
- Not in LATAM / no Spanish version.
- Past legal disputes (with Prentke Romich) created brand confusion.

# Evidence base
- SLP-authored and used in clinical practice; no RCT published.

# Geographic / language coverage
- English.
- App Store worldwide.
