# What it is
Cognitive-language training app from ImagiRation (a Boston-area research outfit). Game-like exercises designed to develop mental imagery and language integration in young autistic children. Based on Pivotal Response Treatment (PRT) and built around the developer's own published research. FDA Breakthrough Device designation.

# Pricing
- Free base app on App Store, Google Play, Amazon.
- MITA Premium — subscription via app store (price varies by country; ~USD 9.99/month).

# Features
- Progressive exercises: simple vocabulary → adjectives → verbs → pronouns → syntax.
- Multiple-cue exercises (Pivotal Response Treatment foundation).
- Cloud sync of progress.
- Add multiple kids per parent.
- Request-new-activity feature in Premium.

# Strengths
- Genuine clinical evidence — 3-year study of 6,454 kids; MITA users showed 2.2× greater language improvement, p<0.0001. Rare in this space.
- FDA Breakthrough Device designation (Q210093) — regulatory standing few competitors have.
- Reportedly 2M+ users.
- Multi-language: English, Spanish, Portuguese, Russian, German, French, Italian, Arabic, Farsi, Korean, Chinese.
- Free base app removes adoption friction.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Single mechanism (mental imagery / multi-cue exercises) — the app is a narrow drill, not a broad curriculum.
- Premium upsell is fragmented across app stores; parents in some countries can't get it.
- No AAC, no schedule, no emotion regulation.
- No clinician portal in the app — clinicians interested in MITA must use it as a take-home assignment.
- Not a substitute for therapy and not marketed as such, but parents may treat it as one.
- Visual style is engineering-team-grade; competing in app stores against polished games.

# Evidence base
- Vyshedskiy et al., 3-year study published 2017-2020; multiple peer-reviewed papers. See `clinical/topics/mita-evidence.md` when populated.
- FDA Breakthrough Device Designation Q210093.

# Geographic / language coverage
- 11 languages including Spanish, Portuguese.
- Free worldwide via app stores.
