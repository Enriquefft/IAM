# What it is
Hardware-plus-software product: a locked-down Android tablet ("Goally tablet") with no browser, no social, no ads, plus a software suite (visual schedules, video lessons, AAC, reward charts, parent app). Targets neurodivergent kids ages 5-13 with executive-function challenges.

# Pricing
- Goally tablet — USD 199-249 one-time.
- Subscription — ~USD 15-20/month for ongoing software access and updates.
- 90-day breakage replacement.
- Software-only subscriptions also available without buying the tablet.

# Features
- Locked tablet (no browser, no social, no app store).
- Visual schedules / routines.
- Video lessons (life skills: brushing teeth, social greetings, etc.).
- Reward charts.
- AAC ("Word Vault" simple AAC).
- Behavioral data collection.
- Parent companion app.
- Bundles Khan Academy Kids and PBS Kids.

# Strengths
- Hardware lock-down is a wedge: no other player ships an autism-specific tablet that solves the "kid keeps opening YouTube" problem.
- Subscription bundles a wide set of features (schedule + video + AAC + chores) instead of fragmenting them across apps.
- Strong US parent-community marketing.
- VC-backed; capable of fast feature iteration.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- US/Canada only; no LATAM availability.
- English UI; no Spanish version of the curriculum.
- Hardware adds CapEx the family doesn't need if they already own a tablet — locks customer into the bundle.
- AAC is rudimentary vs Proloquo2Go / TouchChat — sufficient for early learners, not for advanced communicators.
- Subscription continues forever; total 5-year cost is north of USD 1,000 plus tablet.
- No professional / clinician dashboard.

# Evidence base
- Marketing claims behavioral outcomes; no peer-reviewed trials cited.

# Geographic / language coverage
- English.
- Ships to US and Canada.
