# What it is
Non-profit founded by Miriam Reyes Oliva (UNICEF Emprende 2013 awardee) after creating a pictogram story for her cousin José. Now publishes two free pictogram-story collections (Aprende: 15+ autonomy stories; Disfruta: 5 reading-initiation tales) plus Escuelas Visuales (a curated platform for educators). Distinct from the [José Aprende](jose-aprende.md) app, which is a single-app subset.

# Pricing
- Free. Stories downloadable as PDF and accessible via web platform; some app distribution via Fundación Orange.

# Features
- Aprende collection: pictogram stories on autonomy (handwashing, bath, food, dressing, brushing teeth, hairdresser, park, supermarket, crossing the street, birthday).
- Disfruta collection: 5 narrative pictogram stories.
- Escuelas Visuales platform: educator-targeted resources.
- Stories include basic emotion stories: happy, sad, scared, angry, surprised.
- Creative Commons licensing — free reuse and adaptation.

# Strengths
- Spanish-first, free, foundation-supported.
- Story-based pedagogy is rare and valuable.
- CC license makes therapists and schools first-class users.
- Strong brand recognition in Spanish-speaking autism community.
- Multi-channel: web, app, PDF downloads.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Catalog growth is slow (small non-profit team).
- Reading-only experience; no interaction beyond turning pages.
- No data, no progress tracking.
- Stories aimed at preschool and early elementary; little for older kids.
- App fragmentation: stories distributed via several touch points (own app, Fundación Orange app, web, PDF) — inconsistent UX.
- Castilian Spanish; LATAM regional vocabulary not adapted.

# Evidence base
- Educator anecdotes; no RCT.

# Geographic / language coverage
- Spanish (Castilian); some content in English and Portuguese.
- Free worldwide.
