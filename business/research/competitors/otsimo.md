# What it is
Turkish-origin special education app suite: Otsimo Special Education (game-based learning for autism/Down syndrome/ADHD), Otsimo Speech Therapy (articulation), Otsimo AAC. Game library covers literacy, math, sorting, emotions, sounds, words. No-ads policy. Multi-app ecosystem under one brand.

# Pricing
- 7-day free trial.
- Monthly — USD 9.99/month.
- Annual — USD 119.99/year.
- Lifetime — USD 229.99 one-time.

# Features
- 80+ activities across 13 areas: letters, numbers, tracing, memory, sorting/matching, emotions, sounds, words, reading.
- Personalized learning path: difficulty adapts.
- Detailed progress reports per activity.
- No ads, no third-party content.
- Multilingual.
- Companion Otsimo AAC and Otsimo Speech Therapy in same family.

# Strengths
- Game format is highly engaging for kids in the early-elementary band.
- Curriculum breadth in one subscription.
- Mom's Choice, Parent's Pick, HundredED Global Collection awards — has institutional credibility.
- Multi-language including Spanish.
- Reasonable lifetime price relative to a multi-app stack.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- "AAC" is a separate app and is rudimentary vs dedicated AAC products.
- Personalized path is not transparent; parent can't see how the model picks.
- Reports are surface-level (which games played, not skill mastery vs curriculum).
- Subscription churn is real — annual cost adds up over years a kid actually uses the app.
- No clinician dashboard or shareable progress for an SLP.
- Game-first design can crowd out unstructured learning the kid would actually generalize from.
- "Special education" branding lumps autism, Down syndrome, ADHD together — leaves parents unsure what's autism-specific.

# Evidence base
- Awards-based credibility; no peer-reviewed clinical trial.

# Geographic / language coverage
- 24+ languages; Spanish supported.
- Sold worldwide via App Store / Google Play.
