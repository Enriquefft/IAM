# What it is
Routine and time-management app aimed at neurodivergent kids and adults (ADHD, autism). Visual timer + step-by-step routine player + reward points. Two flavors: Brili Routines for kids, Brili Adult for grown-ups. Parent monitors progress live from a separate device.

# Pricing
- 30-day free trial.
- Monthly — USD 7.99/month.
- 6-month — USD 34.99.
- Annual — USD 49.99/year.

# Features
- Visual timer per activity.
- Audible prompts.
- Dynamic timing: parent sets total window, app re-balances activity durations.
- Star/point reward system; parent-defined rewards.
- Parent companion app for live observation from a different device.
- Multi-child under one parent account.
- Visual analytics dashboard.

# Strengths
- The dynamic timing is a real design choice — most schedule apps treat each activity as fixed duration.
- Cross-platform (iOS, Android).
- Live parent observation is unusual and useful.
- Analytics for the parent — most schedule apps have none.
- Markets to ADHD too, broadening adoption in mixed-need families.

# Weaknesses / gaps
- Subscription pricing is on the higher end for a routine app.
- English UI primary; Spanish localization quality is inconsistent.
- "Routines" framing is morning/bedtime-centric; less useful for school day or a TEA kid's full schedule.
- No AAC, no emotion regulation, no social stories.
- No clinician dashboard; not designed for SLP/OT use.
- Reward-system framing draws ABA-aligned criticism in some autism-acceptance circles.

# Evidence base
- Marketing claims "science-based" without citations.

# Geographic / language coverage
- Multiple UI languages including Spanish.
- Sold worldwide.
