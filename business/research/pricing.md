# Scope

Pricing benchmarks for the three archetypes in [market.md](./market.md):
- **B2C parent-pay**: subscription, lifetime, in-app purchase, freemium conversion.
- **B2B professional**: per-seat / per-clinician.
- **B2B school/clinic**: per-classroom, per-student, district licensing.
- **Public procurement**: government contract sizes for ed-tech and digital health in LATAM.

Bottom line up front: **LATAM Spanish-language consumer health WTP is materially below US.** Median LATAM mobile app revenue per install is **USD 0.06–0.09 vs USD 0.44 14-day ARPU for global health & fitness apps** ([RevenueCat State of Subscription Apps 2025](https://www.revenuecat.com/state-of-subscription-apps-2025/), retrieved 2026-05-02). Do not import US benchmarks uncritically.

## Pricing pattern observations

- **The "annual at USD ~5/mo equivalent" anchor is the dominant industry price point** for child-focused educational subscriptions. LeeLoo, Speech Blubs, ABCmouse all converge on USD 4.99–5.00/mo annualized.
- Monthly is **2.5–3× annual-equivalent**, designed to push annual conversion. LATAM monthly conversion is rare; **weekly is the dominant LATAM cadence** ([RevenueCat 2025](https://www.revenuecat.com/state-of-subscription-apps-2025/), retrieved 2026-05-02).
- **Lifetime exists on three of the four serious competitors** (LeeLoo, Speech Blubs, AssistiveWare's portfolio is lifetime-only). Lifetime price is **2.5–4× annual price**. For LeeLoo: USD 149.99 lifetime / USD 59.99 annual = 2.5×. For Speech Blubs: USD 199 / USD 60 = 3.3×.
- **AAC apps are exclusively lifetime** at USD 100–300. AAC is treated more like assistive equipment than a subscription. This is a notable departure from the wellness/education subscription orthodoxy.

## Freemium conversion benchmarks

- **Median freemium-to-paid conversion: 2.18%** (vs hard paywall apps at 12.11% median) ([RevenueCat 2025](https://www.revenuecat.com/state-of-subscription-apps-2025/), retrieved 2026-05-02).
- 3–5% considered good; 6–8% excellent ([Userpilot](https://userpilot.com/blog/freemium-conversion-rate/), retrieved 2026-05-02).
- LATAM specifically: median conversion rates **<0.2%** at install-to-paid level ([RevenueCat 2025](https://www.revenuecat.com/state-of-subscription-apps-2025/), retrieved 2026-05-02). Two orders of magnitude below US/EU.
- LATAM compensating factor: **highest median MRR growth of any tracked geography (+17.2%)** in 2024.

`View:` Plan for LATAM at **0.5–1.0% install-to-paying-subscriber** in year 1 baseline; **2–3% after 18 months of paywall optimization**. Anchor consumer pricing at **USD 4.99/month annualized** (USD 59.88/year). Monthly tier exists at **USD 11.99–14.99** mostly as a price anchor, not a real revenue line.

## Per-seat structure recommendation (for our product roadmap)

- **Solo professional**: tier-1 USD 12/mo or local equivalent. Includes content library, parent-share links, basic progress tracking.
- **Multi-seat clinic (5–25 seats)**: tier-2 USD 8/seat/mo, minimum USD 60/mo/clinic.
- **Enterprise / large clinic (25+ seats)**: tier-3 contact sales; benchmark USD 5–7/seat at scale.
- **Free academic seat for university faculty teaching SLT/psychology**: pipeline strategy, zero price. Modeled in SOM as 0.

## LATAM-realistic per-classroom pricing

`Estimate:` Anchored to national budget per pupil for special-ed support. PE Plan TEA was modest; CO SGP destined COP 114,338M (~USD 28M) for **all** disability students 2024 = ~USD 130 per disabled student per year of total non-personnel resource. A SaaS license at **USD 30–60 per autism-impacted student per year** is the realistic ceiling for public schools.

# Government procurement deal sizes (LATAM ed-tech / digital health)

`View:` This is the most opaque part of the market. Public procurement deals exist but are slow and politically fragile.

- **Peru MINEDU PerúEduca**: existing platform; third-party vendor inclusion via convocatoria, deals typically PEN 200,000–2,000,000 per cohort (USD 50k–530k). Cycle 12–18 months via SEACE ([trade.gov Peru education](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/peru-education), retrieved 2026-05-02).
- **Colombia SGP discapacidad**: COP 114,338M / ~USD 28M annual budget for disability students nationally ([Mineducación 2024](https://www.mineducacion.gov.co/portal/salaprensa/Comunicados/427413:Mas-de-217-mil-estudiantes-con-discapacidad-hoy-aprenden-en-las-aulas-del-pais), retrieved 2026-05-02). Distributed via departamentos; vendor share unrealistic at >USD 300k/year.
- **Mexico SEP CAM/USAER**: federal SEP procurement is essentially closed; state secretarías de educación occasionally contract. `Estimate:` USD 20k–200k per state pilot.
- **Argentina ANDIS / SUR / obras sociales**: prestaciones reimbursement to centros, not to digital platforms. Exception: pilot grants from CONICET / agencias provinciales, USD 5k–30k.
- **Chile SENADIS / Mineduc**: pilot programs around Ley TEA implementation 2024–2026. `Estimate:` USD 50k–250k per pilot.

`View:` **Do not budget public procurement as a year-1 or year-2 revenue line.** Treat it as optional upside. The reliable revenue is parent-pay and professional SaaS; public deals are reputation-building and capability-validation.

# Pricing structure recommendation (for our product)

1. **B2C**: Freemium → annual at **USD 4.99/mo equivalent (USD 59.88/yr)** with **monthly anchor at USD 14.99**. Lifetime tier at **USD 149** for high-intent parents wanting offline + one-time payment (high LATAM cultural fit). Localize price by geo via storefront (Apple/Google handle this; manual override for AR FX).
2. **B2B per-seat**: **USD 12/mo solo, USD 8/seat clinic (5–25), enterprise quote**. Bill annually with 15% discount.
3. **B2B school/clinic**: **per-classroom annual contract USD 420 (PE/CO/EC) / USD 540 (MX/CL) / USD 600 (AR localized)**. Multi-classroom discount to USD 300/classroom at 5+. Parent-app companion seats free.
4. **Public**: react-only. Do not staff a procurement function before USD 1M ARR.
