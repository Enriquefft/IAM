# Question
Comparing PECS to a speech-generating device (ProxTalker), which produces stronger gains in social-communicative behavior and natural speech for children with severe autism?

# Method
- Multiple-baseline with alternating-treatment design.
- 3 children, severe autism.
- Two conditions: PECS (low-tech) vs. SGD (ProxTalker).
- Outcomes: social-communicative behavior, natural speech production.

# Findings
- Mixed social-communicative results across all 3 participants in both conditions.
- Little observable difference between PECS and SGD conditions.
- No increases in natural speech in either condition.
- Independent requesting was generally easier to acquire under PECS than under the ProxTalker SGD.

# Limitations
- n=3, severe-autism population not representative.
- Specific SGD (ProxTalker) had a 3D-token interface — not representative of modern tablet apps.
- Short intervention period.

# Quotes
> "Data for speech outcomes did not reveal any increases across participants, and no differences between treatment conditions were observed." — abstract

# Builder takeaways
- For severe-presentation children, neither modality magically produces speech — set expectations accordingly.
- Independent requesting was easier under PECS — the *physical exchange* of a card may be more concrete than tapping. UX design lesson: a tablet AAC may need a "drag the card to recipient" gesture rather than a tap, especially for users transitioning from PECS.
