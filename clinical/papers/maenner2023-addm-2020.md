# Question
What is the prevalence and characteristics of autism spectrum disorder among 8-year-old children in the United States in 2020, by sex, race/ethnicity, and ADDM site?

# Method
- Active surveillance program, ADDM Network 2020 cycle (data published 2023).
- Record review across 11 US sites (Arizona, Arkansas, California, Georgia, Maryland, Minnesota, Missouri, New Jersey, Tennessee, Utah, Wisconsin).
- DSM-5 case ascertainment.

# Findings
- Overall ASD prevalence: 27.6 per 1,000 (1 in 36; 2.76%) — up from 23.0 per 1,000 (1 in 44) in 2018 cycle.
- Boys: 43.0/1,000; girls: 11.4/1,000; M:F ratio 3.8:1.
- By race/ethnicity (per 1,000): Asian/Pacific Islander 33.4; Hispanic 31.6; Black 29.3; American Indian/Alaska Native 26.5; Two-or-more 22.9; White 24.3.
- First ADDM cycle in which White prevalence is lower than other racial/ethnic groups — reversal of historical pattern.
- By site: 23.1/1,000 (Maryland) to 44.9/1,000 (California) — ~2x range.
- Intellectual disability among children with cognitive data (66.7% of cohort): 37.9% overall.
- ID by race/ethnicity: Black 50.8%; Asian/Pacific Islander 41.5%; Two-or-more 37.8%; Hispanic 34.9%; AI/AN 34.8%; White 31.8%.
- Median age at earliest known ASD diagnosis: 49 months overall (range 36 months California → 59 months Minnesota).
- Children with ID had earlier median diagnosis (43 months) vs without ID (53 months).

# Limitations
- Site coverage limited to 11 states; not nationally representative.
- 33.3% of cases lack cognitive data — ID estimate is partial.
- Surveillance dependent on documented behaviors in records; underdetection in regions with weaker school/clinical record systems.

# Builder takeaways
- Penultimate CDC ADDM data point before the 2025 update; useful for trend analysis (1 in 44 → 1 in 36 → 1 in 31 across three cycles).
- White-not-highest reversal is the single most important equity finding — historically, the cited "Hispanic underdiagnosis" in pediatric autism is now structurally reversed in US ADDM data.
- ID-associated earlier diagnosis (43 vs 53 months) confirms severe presentations get triaged faster — mild presentations and girls remain late-diagnosed.
