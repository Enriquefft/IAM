# Question
What is the aggregate effect of Early Start Denver Model (ESDM) on cognitive, language, social, adaptive, and autism-symptom outcomes in young children with ASD?

# Method
- Meta-analysis of 12 studies (640 children, 44 unique effect sizes).
- Studies included RCTs and non-randomized designs (~half non-randomized).
- Random-effects model, Hedges' g.

# Findings
- Overall pooled effect g = 0.357, p = 0.024 (moderate, statistically significant).
- Cognition g = 0.412, p = 0.038.
- Language g = 0.408, p = 0.011.
- Autism symptoms g = 0.070, p = 0.616 (NS).
- Social communication g = 0.209, p = 0.285 (NS).
- Adaptive behavior g = 0.121, p = 0.458 (NS).
- Repetitive behaviors g = -0.016, p = 0.876 (NS).
- Heterogeneity I² = 64.84% (moderate).

# Limitations
- Half of studies non-randomized; design risk weakens pooled estimate.
- ~23% of measures had correlated measurement error risk.
- Heterogeneity moderate; treatment dose, age, and fidelity varied widely.
- Author Rogers is co-developer of ESDM.

# Quotes
> "the overall positive effect size should be taken cautiously" — Authors' caveat

# Builder takeaways
- ESDM "works" for cognition + language but does not change autism symptom severity, adaptive behavior, social communication, or stereotypies in pooled data.
- Headline pitch should be skill-acquisition (cognitive, language), not "reducing autism".
- Software augmenting language-rich routines and play-based learning has the strongest evidence anchor.
