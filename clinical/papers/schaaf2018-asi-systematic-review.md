# Question
What is the efficacy of occupational therapy using Ayres Sensory Integration (ASI) for children with autism?

# Method
- Systematic review, 5 studies meeting AOTA inclusion (2007-2015).
- 3 RCTs, 1 retrospective analysis, 1 single-subject ABA design.
- ASI fidelity required.

# Findings
- Strong evidence: ASI yields positive outcomes for individually-set functional goals (Goal Attainment Scaling).
- Moderate evidence: improvements in autistic mannerisms and reduced caregiver support during self-care.
- Emerging evidence: gains in play, sensorimotor, language, social participation.
- Effect sizes not formally reported in abstract.

# Limitations
- All reviewed studies focused on autism — narrow population.
- Mix of designs; only 3 RCTs.
- No pooled meta-analysis.

# Quotes
> "Strong evidence supports use of ASI for autism on individually-established goals."

# Builder takeaways
- AOTA-published — official OT-profession endorsement of ASI for autism.
- Effect framed as goal-attainment, not symptom-reduction or universal outcomes.
- Important context for product positioning in LATAM where OT/ASI is widely sought by parents.
