# Question
What is the prevalence and nature of motor impairments in children with autism spectrum disorder, and to what extent are they recognized as developmental coordination disorder (DCD)?

# Method
- PRISMA-aligned systematic review and meta-analysis.
- Search of MEDLINE, EMBASE, CINAHL, PsycINFO (2010–December 2021).
- 27 studies meeting inclusion criteria (children with ASD ages 5–12, no ID).

# Findings
- 92.5% of articles indicated 50–88% of children with ASD have significant motor impairment on standardized assessments or functional questionnaires.
- Patterns of motor and functional difficulty are consistent with DCD diagnostic criteria.
- Only 15% of papers from 2014 onward described motor problems as DCD.
- Only ~15.1% of children with ASD with motor impairment carry a co-occurring DCD diagnosis — substantial under-recognition.
- Domains affected: gross motor (bilateral coordination, balance, gait), fine motor, visuomotor coordination.

# Limitations
- Restricted to children without ID; ID further increases motor impairment.
- Heterogeneity in motor assessments used.
- Cross-sectional; no developmental trajectories.

# Builder takeaways
- Plan UI for ~70% of users having some motor coordination difficulty — touch targets, dwell times, gesture sensitivity all need DCD-friendly defaults.
- Avoid drag-and-drop, multi-finger gestures, and time-pressured precision interactions for autistic-targeted product surfaces.
- The 15% formal-DCD-diagnosis vs ~70% functional-impairment gap means autistic users often have "undocumented" motor needs — accessibility design must not depend on a separate DCD label.
