# Question
What is the nationwide prevalence of autism spectrum disorder among Colombian children aged 4–14 years in 2022, and how does it vary across geography and health-coverage regime?

# Method
- Descriptive epidemiological analysis of Colombia's SISPRO (Sistema Integral de Información de la Protección Social) health registry.
- ASD case ascertainment by ICD code in the RIPS (Registro Individual de Prestaciones de Salud) within SISPRO.
- ~95% national healthcare coverage between contributory (68.28%) and subsidized (25.36%) regimes.
- Year of analysis: 2022.

# Findings
- Overall ASD prevalence (children 4–14, 2022): 13.788 per 10,000 (~0.138%).
- High-GDP regions (Antioquia, Atlántico, Bogotá, Cundinamarca, Valle del Cauca): mean 17.90 per 10,000 (SD 14.3).
- Low-GDP regions (Amazonas, Guainía, Vaupés, Vichada, Guaviare): mean 2.6 per 10,000 (SD 2.5).
- Earlier 2019 SISPRO analysis reported 18.73 per 10,000 in children 0–14.
- Regional ratio between highest and lowest department: ~7×, paralleling Chile's school-vs-clinical undercount pattern.

# Limitations
- Administrative-data prevalence: counts only children whose ICD-coded ASD diagnosis was registered in SISPRO; substantial undercount expected.
- ~95% coverage but uninsured + non-attending populations excluded.
- ICD-code ascertainment subject to coding variability across providers.
- Cross-sectional snapshot; no individual-level case validation.
- Regional variation conflates true incidence with diagnostic infrastructure differences.
- 0.138% rate is roughly 1/10 of Chile's Bayesian projection (1.31%) — the gap is administrative undercount, not biology.

# Builder takeaways
- Colombia's only national-scale ASD prevalence estimate is administrative (~0.14%) — order of magnitude lower than Chile's clinically-projected 1.31%.
- 7× urban-rural gradient confirms LATAM pattern: diagnostic infrastructure tracks GDP, not biology.
- Bogotá-area rate (~18 per 10,000) is the high watermark for Colombian health-system case-finding; still <1/5 of population-based estimates from Mexico.
- Confirms the LATAM-wide pattern: governmental registry counts capture only the diagnosed minority; software products do not need to wait for clinical case identification to reach users.
