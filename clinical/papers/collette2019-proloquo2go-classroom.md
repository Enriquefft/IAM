# Question
Does Proloquo2Go on an iPad reduce the support a child with ASD needs to perform classroom academic tasks, compared to no technology, PECS cards, or a SMARTBoard?

# Method
- Within-subject comparison across 4 children.
- 4 conditions counterbalanced: no technology, PECS, SMARTBoard, iPad+Proloquo2Go.
- Outcome: amount of adult support required to complete classroom academic opportunities.

# Findings
- Children required less support with Proloquo2Go than with no technology.
- Support needed with Proloquo2Go was equivalent to PECS or SMARTBoard.
- iPad+P2G did not significantly outperform PECS in this very small sample.

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

# Limitations
- n=4; not generalizable.
- Single classroom, single observer.
- Technology novelty effects not controlled.
- "Support" measured qualitatively.

# Quotes
> "Children required less support [with Proloquo2Go] than when using no technology, and equal support to when using a PECS or SMARTBoard." — abstract

# Builder takeaways
- Even rigorous tech-vs-tech AAC comparisons consistently show *no clear winner* among well-designed AAC modalities. The deciding factors are non-efficacy variables: cost, language coverage, device durability, family fit.
- For a wellness/education product, claim "as good as" rather than "better than" PECS — the evidence supports the former, not the latter.
