# Question
What is the prevalence of autism spectrum disorders among regular-school pupils in Quito, Ecuador — pilot prevalence estimate for Ecuador?

# Method
- Cross-sectional school survey.
- 161 regular schools surveyed in metropolitan Quito.
- Total pupils: 51,453.

# Findings
- ASD prevalence with formal diagnosis: 0.11% (~11.07 per 10,000).
- Additional 0.21% suspected but undiagnosed.
- Sex ratio: 4.7:1 (male:female) among diagnosed cases.
- Diagnostic breakdown of identified cases (n=57): classical autism 13; Asperger syndrome 13; PDD-NOS 30; childhood disintegrative disorder 1; Rett 0.
- Authors interpret the rate as reflecting access-to-diagnosis bottleneck, not true biological prevalence.
- Limited diagnostic capacity (only 2–3 neuro-pediatricians available locally), low public awareness, cultural stigma.
- "Very low inclusion tolerance" in regular education.

# Limitations
- Capital city only — rural Ecuadorian rates likely even lower (per author note).
- School-based sampling excludes children outside education system.
- Rate explicitly reported as "incidence indicator of access" by authors.
- Ecuadorian Ministry of Public Health independent estimate: 0.28% (0.18–0.41%) in children ≤5 years — twice the school-based rate but still well below true prevalence.

# Builder takeaways
- Ecuador's only published prevalence number is explicitly an under-detection floor — true rate likely >1%.
- 2–3 specialized diagnosticians for an entire capital city defines the bottleneck — products that empower non-specialist screening (with referral) attack the visible constraint.
- Close in profile to Peru (no own prevalence study, similar service scarcity) — Ecuador can serve as a Peru-analog for product market sizing.
