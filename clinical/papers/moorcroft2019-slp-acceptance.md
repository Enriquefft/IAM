# Question
From the speech-language pathologist's perspective, what factors discriminate parents who accept vs. those who reject/abandon AAC systems for their children?

# Method
- Qualitative semi-structured interviews with 16 SLPs.
- Inclusion: SLPs who had introduced ≥1 accepted system and ≥1 rejected/abandoned system.
- Thematic analysis.

# Findings
- 6 themes influencing parent acceptance:
  1. The way parents view their child.
  2. Parents' views on AAC intervention.
  3. Support networks around parents.
  4. Capacity of and demands on parents.
  5. Services provided by SLPs.
  6. Specific features of AAC systems.
- Novel construct surfaced: parent grief and loss as a driver of abandonment.

# Limitations
- 16 SLPs; Australia.
- SLP perspective may diverge from family/child reality.
- Small qualitative sample.
- No outcome data linked to specific themes.

# Quotes
> "SLPs raised the novel concept of parent grief and loss as a potential contributor to AAC abandonment." — discussion

# Builder takeaways
- Parent grief is an under-addressed product surface area. Features that frame AAC as additive (not "giving up on speech") may reduce grief-driven abandonment.
- Support networks: a peer-family community function (moderated, evidence-aligned) directly addresses theme #3.
- "How parents view their child" implies positioning matters — branding AAC as augmenting, not replacing, capability is materially different from default vendor framing.
