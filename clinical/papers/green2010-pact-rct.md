# Question
Does a parent-mediated communication-focused intervention (PACT) added to treatment-as-usual reduce autism symptom severity and improve parent-child communication compared to TAU alone?

# Method
- Multisite (London, Manchester, Newcastle) parallel-group RCT.
- 152 children (77 PACT + TAU, 75 TAU).
- Ages 2y to 4y 11m, core autism diagnosis.
- PACT: speech-language therapist-delivered parent training. Biweekly 2-hour clinic sessions × 6 months + monthly boosters × 6 months (18 sessions). Daily 30-min home practice.
- Primary outcome: ADOS-G social communication algorithm score at 13 months (blinded).
- Secondary: parent-child interaction (synchrony, child initiations), parent-reported language, adaptive functioning.

# Findings
- Primary (ADOS-G): PACT -3.9 vs TAU -2.9 reduction; effect size -0.24 (95% CI -0.59 to 0.11), NS.
- Parent synchrony: large effect d = 1.22 favoring PACT.
- Child initiations during dyadic interaction: d = 0.41 favoring PACT.
- Parent-reported language gains favored PACT; assessor-rated language and teacher-rated adaptive functioning showed minimal between-group differences.
- Authors did not recommend PACT for autism-symptom reduction at primary endpoint.

# Limitations
- Primary outcome NS — initial publication framed as null trial.
- Effects strongest in dyadic context; generalization to other contexts limited.
- ADOS-G may not be sensitive to intervention-induced change.
- Long-term follow-up (Pickles 2016) later showed sustained symptom-severity benefit.

# Quotes
> "On the basis of our findings, we cannot recommend the addition of this PACT intervention to treatment as usual for the purpose of reduction in autism symptoms."

# Builder takeaways
- Initial trial: parent-mediated communication therapy did NOT reduce autism symptoms at 13 months on the primary endpoint.
- BUT it produced large changes in parent behavior (synchrony d=1.22) and meaningful changes in child initiation — these are the proximal targets software can credibly aim at.
- Parent coaching products should declare goals as "improving parent responsiveness and dyadic communication," not "reducing autism severity."
- Contrasts with later Pickles 2016 long-term follow-up showing eventual symptom benefit — illustrates how early intervention effects may take years to manifest.
