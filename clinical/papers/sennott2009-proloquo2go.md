# Question
Does Proloquo2Go (iPhone/iPod-touch AAC app) align with established best-practices for AAC use in children with ASD?

# Method
- Position / overview paper.
- Maps Proloquo2Go's features against accepted AAC best-practice criteria for ASD: symbol use, visual supports, voice output, inclusion.

# Findings
- Authors conclude Proloquo2Go meets the four mapped best-practice areas.
- Notes mobile-device platform makes AAC less stigmatizing and more readily portable than dedicated SGDs.
- Argues lower price point (vs. ~$8000 dedicated SGDs of the era) democratizes access.

# Limitations
- Sennott is a co-developer of Proloquo2Go — significant conflict of interest.
- No empirical efficacy data in this paper.
- Best-practice "alignment" is the authors' framework, not independent evaluation.

# Quotes
> Proloquo2Go aligns with "best practices in AAC for individuals with ASD such as symbols, visual supports, voice output, and inclusion." — abstract

# Builder takeaways
- Demonstrates the *category-creating* argument: tablet-based AAC at ~$200 vs dedicated SGDs at ~$8000 was a 40× price collapse. The same dynamic is repeatable in LATAM where ARASAAC + free tablet apps can be 100× cheaper than imported PCS+Boardmaker+Tobii devices.
- Author/developer pitch papers are common in AAC literature — when surveying the field, separate vendor self-promotion from independent efficacy work.
