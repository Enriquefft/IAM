# Question
What is the global, regional, and national prevalence and health burden (DALYs, YLDs) of autism spectrum disorder in 2021, and how has it changed since 1990?

# Method
- Systematic literature review across PubMed, Embase, PsycINFO of epidemiological studies.
- Bayesian meta-regression (DisMod-MR 2.1) used to model prevalence and burden.
- Health burden expressed as DALYs and YLDs (no deaths attributed to autism as underlying cause in GBD 2021).
- Autistic individuals involved in research design and interpretation.
- Passive case-finding (counting only pre-existing diagnoses) excluded — major methodological shift from GBD 2019.

# Findings
- Global autism prevalence in 2021: 1 in 127 persons (~0.79%).
- Estimated 61.8 million autistic individuals globally (95% UI 52.1–72.7M).
- Age-standardized prevalence: 788.3 per 100,000 (95% UI 663.8–927.2).
- Male prevalence: 1,064.7 per 100,000; female prevalence: 508.1 per 100,000.
- Global male-to-female ratio: 2.1:1 (lower than DSM-5 clinical samples).
- Highest age-standardized regional prevalence: High-income Asia Pacific (1,559.5/100,000), Australasia (1,191.0/100,000), High-income super-region (1,090.2/100,000).
- Tropical Latin America age-standardized rate: 614.5/100,000.
- Prevalence highest at birth, declining with age (children <5: 169.2/100,000; ages ≥20: 137.7/100,000); decline accelerates after age 60.
- DALYs in 2021: 11.5 million globally (95% UI 7.8–16.3M); rate 147.6/100,000.
- All DALYs were YLDs (no autism-attributable deaths).
- Autism ranked 7th leading cause of YLDs for children <5 years; 8th for ages 5–14; 10th for ages 15–19.
- Excess (all-cause) mortality among autistic people: 564.8/100,000 males; 740.1/100,000 females annually.
- Age-standardized prevalence stable 1990–2021 (773.2 → 788.3/100,000); DALY increase driven by population growth.
- GBD 2019 estimated 1 in 271; jump to 1 in 127 in GBD 2021 driven by methodological change excluding passive case-finding, not actual prevalence rise.

# Limitations
- Modeled estimates depend heavily on data availability — LMIC estimates are extrapolations with wide UI.
- Methodological shift creates discontinuity vs prior GBD reports.
- "Tropical Latin America" is a GBD region aggregation; country-level estimates have high uncertainty.
- All-cause excess mortality reported but causal attribution to autism vs comorbidities is not established.

# Builder takeaways
- Use 1 in 127 (~0.79%) as the global denominator; explicitly note it is GBD-modeled and not a count.
- Tropical LATAM rate of ~615/100,000 (~0.6%) is the best available aggregate for the Andean/Brazilian region — insufficient for country-level Peru sizing.
- Autism is among the top 10 causes of non-fatal disease burden in children under 20 globally — quantitatively comparable to many funded conditions, supporting market case.
- Excess female mortality > male is a counter-intuitive finding worth flagging for any wellness product optimizing safety/risk pages.
