# Question
Does expert PECS training and consultancy delivered to teachers improve communication outcomes in autistic children in mainstream and special schools?

# Method
- Pragmatic, group RCT across 3 arms: immediate treatment, delayed treatment, no treatment.
- Intervention: 2-day PECS workshop for teachers + 6 half-day school-based consulting sessions over 5 months.
- Outcomes assessed via classroom observation, ADOS-G ratings, formal language tests, parent report.
- Follow-up at 10 months.

# Findings
- PECS use and rate of communicative initiations rose significantly post-intervention in immediate vs. no-treatment arms.
- No significant gain in spoken language frequency.
- No improvement on ADOS-G or standardized language measures.
- Treatment effects did not maintain after the active consultancy phase ended.

# Limitations
- Intervention was teacher-mediated, not direct child intervention; dose to child indirect.
- 10-month follow-up showed loss of gains — implementation fidelity decay likely.
- ADOS-G and language batteries may be insensitive to PECS-specific gains.
- Self-selected schools, not population-representative.

# Builder takeaways
- Periodic in-app coaching boosts adoption but adoption decays without sustained scaffolding — argues for ongoing, in-product nudges (modeling reminders, fidelity prompts), not one-off training.
- Speech is not the right success metric for a symbol-exchange product. Picture initiations and number of communicative acts are.
- Without professional support layer, teacher-only PECS implementations regress. A consumer product should provide that layer (video micro-lessons, fidelity self-checks).
