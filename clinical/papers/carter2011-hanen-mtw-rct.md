# Question
Does Hanen's More Than Words (HMTW) parent-training program improve parental responsivity and child communication in toddlers with early ASD symptoms?

# Method
- Multisite RCT, 62 children (mean age 20 months) with ASD diagnosis.
- HMTW arm: 8 weekly group sessions + 3 individual family sessions over 3.5 months, delivered by Hanen-certified speech-language therapist.
- Control arm: business-as-usual.
- Mean group size 3.57 (Hanen recommends ≥8 — fidelity concern).
- Outcomes assessed at 5 and 9 months from enrollment.

# Findings
- No significant main effect on child communication outcomes.
- Parental responsivity gains: Glass's Δ = 0.71 at 5 months, 0.50 at 9 months.
- Child object interest moderated effects: lower-baseline-object-interest children improved more in HMTW arm; higher-object-interest children showed growth attenuation in HMTW arm.

# Limitations
- Small group sizes compromised intended Hanen group dynamic (mean 3.57 vs recommended ≥8).
- Differential effects suggest HMTW may not be appropriate for all toddlers — needs pre-screening.
- 11% attrition.

# Quotes
> "The HMTW intervention showed differential effects on child communication depending on a baseline child factor."

> "Children with lower levels of Time 1 object interest exhibited facilitated growth in communication; children with higher levels of object interest exhibited growth attenuation."

# Builder takeaways
- Most-cited RCT of Hanen MTW — null on primary child outcome.
- Strong on parent behavior (Δ ~0.7), aligning with PACT pattern: parent-mediated interventions reliably change parents but child outcomes contingent on child profile.
- Software targeting parent responsivity has stronger evidence than targeting child communication directly.
- Implication: pre-screen children by baseline object interest before recommending HMTW-style approach.
