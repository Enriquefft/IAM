# Question
Do participant characteristics (presence of intellectual disability, age) moderate which AAC modality (PECS, SGD, other picture-based) produces best outcomes for individuals with ASD?

# Method
- Meta-analysis comparing 3 aided AAC categories: PECS, speech-generating devices, other picture-based.
- Stratification by participant characteristics: with vs. without comorbid intellectual disability; preschool vs. older.
- Effect sizes computed for communication and other outcomes.

# Findings
- ASD without comorbid IDD: SGDs most effective across outcomes.
- ASD with comorbid IDD: PECS most effective across outcomes.
- Preschoolers: SGD and PECS both highly effective.
- Older children/elementary: no significant difference between AAC modalities.
- Speech outcomes: small to moderate across all modalities.

# Limitations
- Single-subject pooling; effect sizes not directly comparable to RCT effect sizes.
- "PECS more effective for ID+ASD" finding is small-sample and needs replication.
- Operational definitions of "comorbid IDD" varied across studies.

# Quotes
> "SGDs appear to be most effective ... with ASD individuals without intellectual/developmental disorders [while] PECS appears to be most effective ... with individuals with ASD and IDD." — abstract

# Builder takeaways
- Stratify product onboarding by cognitive profile. Children with comorbid IDD may benefit from a more scaffolded, exchange-based interaction (PECS pattern); children with autism only may benefit from full SGD interface earlier.
- Don't ship one rigid path — provide interaction modes that map to the two evidence-supported clusters.
- Preschool age is universally the highest-yield window across modalities.
