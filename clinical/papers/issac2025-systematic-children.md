# Question
What is the global prevalence of autism spectrum disorder in children, by region and country income level, based on the most recent (2008–2024) screening studies?

# Method
- PRISMA-aligned systematic review and meta-analysis (PROSPERO CRD42023445469).
- Inclusion: studies of children screened for ASD published January 2008–June 2024.
- 66 studies; n=21,313,061 children.

# Findings
- Global pooled ASD prevalence in children: 0.77%; among boys: 1.14%.
- By region (effect size scale, larger = higher prevalence): Australia 2.18 > Africa 1.51 > Americas 1.10 > Europe 0.71 > Asia 0.28.
- By country income level: high-income 0.86%; middle-income 0.30%; low-income 1.51%.
- Geographic distribution of underlying studies: 25 European, 22 Asian, 13 American, 3 African, 3 Australian.
- I² values frequently >97% — substantial heterogeneity.

# Limitations
- Counter-intuitive low-income > high-income > middle-income pattern likely reflects sample bias (low-income N is small, dominated by selective-recruitment African studies) rather than true epidemiology.
- Effect-size scale not directly translatable to per-1,000 prevalence.
- Heterogeneity I² >97% means pooled mean has very limited interpretability.
- Mixed diagnostic criteria across DSM-IV / DSM-5 / ICD-10 / ICD-11 era studies.
