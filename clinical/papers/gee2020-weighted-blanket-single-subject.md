# Question
Does a weighted blanket (10% body weight, SensaCalm) improve sleep quality, latency, duration, or morning mood in young children with ASD via single-subject ABA design?

# Method
- Single-subject ABA design with 14-night intervention.
- SensaCalm blankets, 3-7 lb sized to 10% body weight.
- Outcomes: caregiver survey + Sense Sleep App (objective tracking).

# Findings
- Only 3 of all measured outcomes reached "effective" status (Percentage of Non-Overlapping Data ≥70%): one child's morning mood, the other's sleep latency, and the other's night wakings.
- Authors concluded improvements were "not strong enough to recommend for clinical use."

# Limitations
- n=2.
- Sense Sleep App lacks published validity/reliability data.
- No control phase.

- Reinforces Gringras 2014: even a permissive single-subject design can't muster clinical-recommendation-grade results from weighted blankets.
