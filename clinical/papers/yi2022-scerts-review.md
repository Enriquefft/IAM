# Question
What is the effectiveness of SCERTS Model-based interventions for autistic children, and what does the current evidence base support?

# Method
- Systematic review of 6 published studies (2014–2021), 455 children.
- 2 of the 6 studies retrieved from gray literature (not peer-reviewed).
- Outcomes: social communication, emotional regulation, transactional supports, language, restricted/repetitive behaviors, adaptive behavior, play, cognition, academics, motor.
- Risk of bias: detection bias rated high in 54.7% of outcomes.

# Findings
- Most studies less vulnerable to bias suggested SCERTS effective for promoting social communication.
- Implementers achieved adequate fidelity through training.
- Inconsistent or insufficient evidence for other outcomes.
- No effect sizes pooled — narrative synthesis only.

# Limitations
- Only 6 studies after >15 years since SCERTS publication; 2 from gray literature.
- Many included studies lacked control groups; small samples; high selection/detection bias.
- No meta-analysis possible.

# Quotes
> "Although SCERTS is a promising intervention with emerging evidence, more methodologically rigorous studies are needed to progress the research base of SCERTS and draw firm conclusions about its effectiveness in improving a wide range of skills for children and implementers."

# Builder takeaways
- SCERTS' transactional-supports framework (visual aids, modified language, predictable routines) is a teacher- and parent-facing structure — software-friendly.
- Despite low evidence-base rigor, SCERTS is widely adopted in school systems (US, UK, Spain) — distribution channel exists.
- Caution when invoking efficacy; 6 studies in 15+ years signals slow research progress.
