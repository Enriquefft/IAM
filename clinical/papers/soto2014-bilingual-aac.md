# Question
What sociocultural framework should guide AAC service delivery for bilingual children with complex communication needs?

# Method
- Theoretical / framework paper.
- Synthesis of existing bilingual education and AAC literature.
- No primary data.

# Findings
- Argues for sociocultural model of AAC service delivery: child's linguistic ecology (home, school, community) must shape AAC system.
- Both/all home languages should be supported, not English-only.
- AAC system must allow for code-switching.
- Vocabulary selection should reflect both/all cultural and linguistic communities the child participates in.
- Identifies major research gaps: very few RCTs, few intervention studies in non-English-dominant contexts.

# Builder takeaways
- Anchor citation for any LATAM AAC product: bilingual support is *the* clinical best practice, not a feature flag.
- Spanish-Quechua, Spanish-English, Portuguese-Spanish (border regions), and Spanish + Indigenous languages are all in scope; an AAC product must architect for multilingual from the data layer up.
- Code-switching (mixing Spanish and English in one utterance) is normal bilingual behavior — system must allow buttons from different languages to combine into single sentence strips.
