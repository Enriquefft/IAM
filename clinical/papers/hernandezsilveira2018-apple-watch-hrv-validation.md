# Question
Does Apple Watch produce HRV measurements valid against a Polar H7 chest-strap reference during rest and induced mental stress?

# Method
- 20 healthy adults; concurrent Apple Watch + Polar H7 ECG.
- Mental stress induced via Stroop and arithmetic tasks.
- HRV indices computed (RMSSD, HF power) for both devices.

# Findings
- Apple Watch HRV indices showed >0.9 reliability vs reference in laboratory.
- HRV decreased significantly under stress vs rest on Apple Watch (HF power, RMSSD; p<0.05).
- Significant agreement supports Apple Watch HRV for stress monitoring under controlled conditions.

# Limitations
- Healthy adult sample, not autistic.
- Laboratory only — does not validate real-world wear.
- No validation against perceived stress in ambulatory follow-ups (separate study found null association).

# Quotes
> "very good reliability and agreement (>0.9)" between Apple Watch and Polar H7

# Builder takeaways
- Apple Watch HRV is good enough for rest-condition trending and laboratory tasks.
- Real-world ambulatory autism stress detection is unproven — caveats: motion artifact, compliance, no EDA signal.
- Consumer-grade wearable enables crude HRV trending across ages 12+; not a replacement for E4/EmbracePlus EDA-rich pipeline.
