# Question
What is the prevalence of autism spectrum disorder in Chile among school-age children, and what proportion of autistic children have unmet special education needs?

# Method
- Probabilistic record linkage between 2021 Chilean school registry and 2003–2015 electronic health records.
- Bayesian prevalence estimation projecting from a clinical validation subsample (Araucanía Sur Health Service, 21 municipalities).
- Largest LATAM autism prevalence study to date.

# Findings
- School registry recorded ASD rate: 0.46% (95% CI 0.45–0.47%).
- Electronic health record rate (Araucanía Sur subsample): 1.22% (95% CI 1.16–1.28%).
- Bayesian projected national ASD prevalence: 1.31% (95% CrI 1.25–1.38%) — approximately 1 in 76 children.
- Sex ratio in school registry: 6:1 male-to-female; in clinical-record-linked subset: 4.18:1.
- Unmet special education need: ~64.57% (25,903/40,113) of identified autistic school-age children did not access PIE (Programa de Integración Escolar) inclusion program; some attended unsubsidized private schools with other supports.

# Limitations
- National projection assumes uniform school-vs-clinical record discrepancy across regions — likely false in heterogeneous geography.
- Findings may not transfer to other LATAM countries with different health/education system structures.
- School registry undercounts due to systemic underdiagnosis, not missing data alone.
- Bayesian model is sensitive to validation-subsample assumptions.

# Builder takeaways
- Chile is the only LATAM country with a national-scale (n=3M) autism prevalence estimate.
- 1.31% (1 in 76) projected vs 0.46% recorded in schools shows roughly 3x undercount in administrative data — same pattern likely applies in Peru.
- ~65% unmet special education program access defines the under-served majority — this is the primary addressable LATAM market for ASD-related products outside the formal special-ed system.
- Sex-ratio drop from 6:1 in schools to 4.18:1 with clinical linkage replicates the global "girls under-detected" pattern at population scale in LATAM.
