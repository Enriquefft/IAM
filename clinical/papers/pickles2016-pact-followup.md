# Question
Does the PACT preschool parent-mediated intervention produce sustained improvements in autism symptoms and social communication 6 years after the end of treatment?

# Method
- Long-term follow-up of 152-child Green 2010 PACT RCT.
- 121 of 152 retained (80%) at median 5.75 years post-treatment (mean age 10.5y).
- Blinded outcome assessors maintained.
- Outcomes: ADOS Comparative Severity Score (CSS); Dyadic Communication Assessment Measure (DCMA) child initiations; expressive-receptive language composite.

# Findings
- Autism symptom severity (ADOS CSS) overall effect: 0.55 (95% CI 0.14 to 0.91), p=0.004 — sustained improvement favoring PACT.
- Child initiations (DCMA) at follow-up: 0.29 (95% CI -0.02 to 0.57), p ≈ 0.06; across study period 0.33 (0.11 to 0.57), p=0.004 — sustained.
- Language composite: 0.15 (95% CI -0.23 to 0.53) — NS at follow-up.
- Parent synchrony effect attenuated over time (parents not still using technique).
- First long-term symptom-reduction RCT result in autism early intervention.

# Limitations
- Original cohort selected for core (severe) autism — generalizability to broader spectrum uncertain.
- Adaptive functioning outcomes not strongly improved.
- Long lag between intervention and emergence of symptom benefit complicates causal mechanism narrative.

# Quotes
> "The results are the first to show long-term symptom reduction after a randomised controlled trial of early intervention in autism spectrum disorder."

# Builder takeaways
- Strongest single piece of evidence that early parent-mediated communication therapy produces durable autism-symptom benefit — anchor citation for any product in this space.
- Mechanism: increasing child communication initiation early causes downstream symptom severity reduction (later confirmed by 2023 mediation analysis).
- Implies software should focus on increasing child initiation rate during dyadic interaction, not on decreasing problem behaviors.
