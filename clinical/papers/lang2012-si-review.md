# Question
Does sensory integration therapy (SIT) produce consistent positive effects in the treatment of autism?

# Method
- Systematic review of 25 studies (217 participants).
- Categorized findings by outcome direction: positive, mixed, no benefit.

# Findings
- 3 studies: positive effects (all with suggestive certainty).
- 8 studies: mixed results (6 suggestive, 2 conclusive).
- 14 studies: no benefit (8 suggestive, 1 preponderant, 5 conclusive).
- Authors conclude SIT lacks consistent positive effect.

# Limitations
- Most included studies had serious methodological flaws.
- Many tested single sensory-based procedures (weighted vests, brushing) rather than full Ayres SI protocol — defenders argue category mixing.
- Language restrictions to English.

# Quotes
> "Sensory integration therapy had no consistently positive effect as a treatment for children with autism spectrum disorders."
