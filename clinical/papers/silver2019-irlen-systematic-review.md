# Question
Is there evidence that tinted (Irlen) lenses improve reading, visual stress, or autism symptoms?

# Method
- Systematic review of 44 articles 1989-2018.
- Quality assessment of selection, instruments, and design.

# Findings
- 14 studies weak supportive; 14 not supportive; 5 inconclusive; 4 needed more research.
- "Strong selection bias and dubious instrument validity" across literature.
- WHO, AAO, AAP do not recognize Irlen Syndrome.
- RANZCO (2018): "no scientific evidence that Irlen Syndrome exists" or that lenses help.

# Builder takeaways
- Tinted lenses for autism is functionally unsupported by mainstream evidence.
- Marketing remains active in LATAM Spanish-speaking markets via Irlen practitioners.
- Builder relevance: avoid recommending; address visual sensitivities through screen color/contrast settings instead.
