# Question
What are the demographic, diagnostic, educational, and service-utilization characteristics of children with ASD receiving pediatric rehabilitation at a referral hospital (EsSalud) in Peru?

# Method
- Cross-sectional descriptive study; medical record review.
- Setting: Pediatric Rehabilitation Service, Edgardo Rebagliati Martins National Hospital (HNERM, EsSalud), Lima.
- Period: 1 January – 31 December 2022.
- Sample: 120 children with prior ASD diagnosis.

# Findings
- Sex: 79.2% male (3.8:1 male-to-female ratio).
- Mean age at ASD diagnosis: 3.83 years (SD 1.67) ~ 46 months.
- Diagnosing specialty: Neurology 42.5%, Psychiatry 26.7%, Psychology 21.7%.
- Education: regular education 63.54% (only 9.84% with SAANEE inclusive support); special education 30.21%.
- 78.4% lacked official disability certification.
- 77.5% had incomplete psychological evaluation.
- Intellectual disability evaluated in n=27: mild 48.1%, moderate 37.0%.
- Global developmental delay observed in 84.3%.
- Aggressive behavior in 24.2%.
- Comorbidity-free: 71.8%; ADHD 12.5%; neurological malformations 9.2%; epilepsy 4.2%.
- Median time since last therapy session: physical 3 months (IQR 1–6), occupational 8 months (IQR 1–24), speech 3.5 months (IQR 1–20).
- Medications: antipsychotics 35.8% (risperidone 15.8% leading); methylphenidate 6.7%.

# Limitations
- Single hospital, EsSalud insurance only — over-represents formally insured working families; under-represents MINSA-only and uninsured population.
- Selection bias: only children already in rehabilitation; not population-representative.
- Cross-sectional; no outcomes data.
- Small n (120); intellectual disability subsample n=27.

# Builder takeaways
- In Peru's best-resourced public-insurance hospital, ASD diagnosis happens at ~46 months — same as LATAM regional median (Montiel-Nava 2024).
- 78.4% lack disability certificates → families miss legal protections (tax benefits, school inclusion mandates) under Law 30150. Information product opportunity.
- 8-month gap between OT sessions in best-case EsSalud care defines service scarcity. Wellness/education products that fill this gap have a clear use-case window.
- 35.8% antipsychotic use is high; potential for non-pharmacological supports (sensory, behavioral, parent training) is implicit.
- This is the only published clinical-cohort description of Peruvian autistic children — cite as the canonical Peru clinical reference.
