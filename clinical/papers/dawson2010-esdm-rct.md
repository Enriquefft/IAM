# Question
Does 2 years of intensive Early Start Denver Model (ESDM) intervention starting in toddlerhood improve cognitive, adaptive, and diagnostic outcomes vs community-referred standard care?

# Method
- Single-site RCT, 48 children (24 ESDM, 24 assess-and-monitor [A/M]).
- Ages 18–30 months at enrollment.
- ESDM arm: 20 hr/week therapist-delivered (actual mean 15.2 hr/week) + 16.3 hr/week parent-delivered, home-based, 24 months.
- A/M arm: community referrals (mean 9.1 hr/week individual + 9.3 hr/week group services).
- Outcomes: Mullen Scales of Early Learning (MSEL), Vineland Adaptive Behavior Scales (VABS), ADOS, diagnostic re-evaluation.
- Retention: 100% ESDM, 88% A/M at 2-year follow-up.

# Findings
- IQ (MSEL composite): ESDM +17.6 points vs A/M +7.0 points; F(1,43)=4.31, p=.044.
- Adaptive behavior (VABS composite): ESDM −0.8 vs A/M −11.2; F(1,43)=7.05, p=.011 (A/M lost ground).
- Receptive language: ESDM +18.9 vs A/M +10.2 (p=.048).
- Expressive language: ESDM +12.1 vs A/M +4.0 (p=.033).
- Diagnostic improvement (autism → PDD-NOS): 29.2% ESDM vs 4.8% A/M (p=.041).
- No significant difference on ADOS severity.

# Limitations
- Small sample (n=48), single site.
- Unequal attrition (88% vs 100%).
- Authors of ESDM manual were trial PIs; royalty conflict declared.
- A/M arm received heterogeneous, low-intensity services — comparator floor unclear.
- No blinded ADOS assessment of all participants.

# Builder takeaways
- Anchors the case for intensive, early, naturalistic-developmental intervention but cost (~30 hr/week professional+parent for 2 years) is incompatible with LATAM private-pay reality.
- Parent-delivered share (~52%) of total dose suggests parent coaching is the leverage point software can address.
- IQ + adaptive gains were strongest; autism-symptom-severity effects were weaker — software targeting daily living skills > "curing autism".
