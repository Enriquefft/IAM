# Question
Does PACT-G (combined home + education delivery of PACT) improve autism symptoms vs treatment-as-usual when scaled across home and school contexts?

# Method
- Multisite single-blind RCT, 3 English sites (Manchester, Newcastle, London).
- 248 children (121 PACT-G, 127 TAU), ages 2-11y.
- 79% male, 60% White, mean age 4.0y.
- PACT-G arm: median 10 parent sessions + 8 education sessions (with learning support assistants) over 6 months.
- Video feedback to enhance adult-child synchrony in both home and school contexts.
- Primary outcome: ADOS-2 total at 12 months.

# Findings
- Primary outcome (ADOS-2): effect size 0.04 (95% CI -0.19 to 0.26), p=0.74. NS.
- Improved adult (parent and LSA) synchronous response.
- Benefits emerged for parental wellbeing and child disruptive behavior.
- Authors caution: scaling delivery context does NOT preserve original intervention efficacy.

# Limitations
- ADOS-2 may be insensitive to behavior change at this scale.
- Home + school dilution may undercut intensity of original PACT.
- Older children (up to 11) included — PACT was designed for preschoolers.

# Quotes
> "[We] found no effect on the primary outcome compared with treatment as usual."

# Builder takeaways
- Critical replication failure: original PACT plus school-context generalization did NOT replicate the long-term symptom benefit.
- Implication: parent-mediated interventions may not transport to mixed-context delivery without losing efficacy.
- Software products should pick ONE context (home or school) rather than try to bridge both — concentration matters.
- Disrupted-behavior and parent-wellbeing effects suggest the value proposition is parent-side, not child-side.
