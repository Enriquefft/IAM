# Question
Does telehealth-coached Project ImPACT improve children's expressive language ability via increased parent strategy use and child intentional communication, vs self-directed ImPACT or active control?

# Method
- 3-arm RCT, 64 dyads matched on child age and developmental quotient.
- Conditions: therapist-assisted telehealth ImPACT (6 months), self-directed ImPACT (6 months), active control.
- Assessments at intake, immediate post-treatment, 9-month follow-up.
- Outcomes: parent strategy use, child intentional communication, child expressive language.
- Serial mediation analysis.

# Findings
- No significant total effect of treatment group on child outcomes.
- Therapist-assisted ImPACT had significant indirect effect on follow-up expressive language via parent strategy use → child intentional communication.
- Self-directed ImPACT alone did not produce mediated effects.

# Limitations
- Modest sample for 3-arm design.
- Self-directed arm may have under-implemented strategies.
- Generalizability of telehealth limited to families with internet access.

# Quotes
> "Therapist-assisted ImPACT had an indirect effect on children's expressive language ability at follow-up through their parents' use of the intervention strategies and their intentional communication immediately post-treatment."

# Builder takeaways
- Self-directed parent-mediated software ALONE may not be sufficient — coaching matters.
- Replicates PACT mediation finding: changing parent behavior → changing child initiation → downstream language.
- Telehealth + coach hybrid is feasible — software with human-in-the-loop coaching is the empirically supported design pattern.
- Pure-software autonomous parent training (no coach) lacks evidence; design accordingly.
