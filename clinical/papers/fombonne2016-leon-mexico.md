# Question
What is the prevalence of autism spectrum disorder in 8-year-old children in León, Guanajuato, Mexico — the first community-based ASD prevalence estimate for Mexico?

# Method
- Two-stage stratified survey of 8-year-olds.
- Stratum 1: Children attending regular schools, screened with Social Responsiveness Scale (SRS).
- Stratum 2: Children identified through special education and medical records.
- Confirmed cases via clinical evaluation including ADOS and ADI-R.

# Findings
- Overall ASD prevalence: 0.87% (95% CI 0.62–1.10%).
- 36 confirmed ASD cases identified.
- Sex ratio: 80.6% male (~4:1).
- Intellectual disability: ~33% (one-third) of confirmed cases.
- Non-verbal: 25% of confirmed cases.
- Co-occurring behavioral problems: 69% of confirmed cases.
- Mean age of first parental concern: 23.8 months.
- Mean diagnostic delay: 41.8 months.
- Initial referral source: medical doctor 35.8%, teacher 27.4%, psychologist 17.9%.

# Limitations
- Single city (Leon) — generalizability to rural or Indigenous Mexican populations limited.
- Special education/medical records stratum dependent on existing service contact (undercounts undiagnosed children outside system).
- 2016 study using DSM-IV-TR-era confirmation tools.
- No replication in other Mexican states.

# Builder takeaways
- Mexico's only community-based ASD prevalence estimate is from a single mid-size city (León) in 2016. Anything quoted as "Mexico ASD prevalence" beyond this is extrapolation.
- 0.87% is roughly half the US current rate — consistent with under-detection in a middle-income setting rather than true biological difference.
- Diagnostic delay of ~42 months exceeds US delay (~24 months) — primary product opportunity is reducing delay between concern and confirmation, not adding new screening tools.
- Referral pathway dominated by physicians (36%) and teachers (27%) — distribution channels for any awareness/screening product should mirror this.
