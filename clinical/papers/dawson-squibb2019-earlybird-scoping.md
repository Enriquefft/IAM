# Question
What is the published evidence base for the National Autistic Society EarlyBird and EarlyBird Plus parent education programmes?

# Method
- Scoping review of peer-reviewed publications on EarlyBird/EarlyBird Plus.
- 18 articles identified (16 UK, 2 New Zealand).

# Findings
- Strong parental support for acceptability across studies.
- Evidence of efficacy: low-level (mostly pre-post, parent self-report).
- No RCTs identified.
- No studies in low-resource settings (LATAM, Africa, etc).
- Authors call for RCTs and feasibility evaluation in non-UK contexts.

# Effect sizes
| Outcome | Direction | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Parental knowledge | + | parent self-report |
| Parental coping | + | parent self-report |
| Child outcomes | not robustly assessed | low-level evidence |

# Limitations
- Heavy UK bias.
- Only one cultural context studied.
- Lack of RCT designs.

# Quotes
> "Future research should consider randomised controlled trials. There is no research on EarlyBird/EarlyBird Plus in low-resource settings; therefore, we recommend broader feasibility evaluation of EarlyBird/EarlyBird Plus including accessibility, cultural appropriateness and scalability."

# Builder takeaways
- EarlyBird is the only major parent program in this set with no RCT despite 27,000+ families served in 14 countries — a striking implementation/evidence gap.
- LATAM is on the "no studies" list — opportunity to ship a localized parent-education tool with built-in evaluation infrastructure.
- The model (group-based + home visits) is the structure software can replicate at scale.
