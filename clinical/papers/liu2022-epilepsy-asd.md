# Question
What is the pooled prevalence of epilepsy in autism spectrum disorders, and how does it vary by age, sex, intellectual disability, and country development index?

# Method
- Systematic review and meta-analysis (66 studies).

# Findings
- Pooled prevalence: ~10% (~1 in 10 autistic individuals).
- Prevalence higher in autistic adults than children.
- Adolescents (11–17 years) have significantly higher rates than school-age (7–10).
- Pre-school children (≤6) higher than school-age children (7–10) — bimodal pattern.
- Higher female representation correlates with higher epilepsy prevalence — epilepsy disproportionately co-occurs in autistic females.
- Lower intellectual functioning associated with higher epilepsy rates.
- Human Development Index of country negatively associated with prevalence (LMIC studies report higher rates, partly due to differential ascertainment).
- Clinical samples show higher prevalence than population samples.

# Limitations
- High heterogeneity across studies.
- Diagnostic ascertainment of epilepsy varies (clinical vs records vs caregiver report).
- Not all studies stratify by ID — confounder.

# Quotes
> "Approximately 1 in 10 autistic individuals have comorbid epilepsy." — Results
> "The prevalence of epilepsy in autistic adults was higher than that in autistic children." — Results

# Builder takeaways
- 10% epilepsy comorbidity is a hard safety constraint for any product using flashing visuals, screen flicker, or sensory-overload mechanics — must consider photosensitive epilepsy guidelines (WCAG 2.3.1).
- Adult-targeted autism products carry higher epilepsy prevalence than child-targeted; risk surface scales with age.
- Female + ID subgroup carries the highest comorbid epilepsy load — relevant for accessibility design.
