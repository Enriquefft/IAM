# Question
Does an ambient generative-AI digital scribe (Abridge) reduce documentation time, cognitive burden, and burnout for pediatric providers?

# Method
- 6-month pilot; pretest-posttest with interrupted time series (ITSA).
- 84 pediatric providers.
- Intervention: Abridge — audio-recorded encounters → ASR → LLM-generated note.
- Outcomes: documentation time per appointment, NASA-TLX cognitive load, Mini-Z burnout, caregiver satisfaction.

# Findings
- Documentation time: -2.8 min per appointment (95% CI 2.1–3.5).
- ITSA: 20.9% (95% CI 17.2–24.6) time savings post-adoption.
- Weekly savings: 1.5 hours/provider.
- NASA-TLX (0–20):
  - Mental demand: -6.9 (p<.001).
  - Temporal demand: -8.0 (p<.001).
  - Effort: -7.0 (p<.001).
- Mini-Z burnout: 54.9% → 33.3% (p=.01).
- Caregiver likelihood-to-recommend: 92.3% → 94.3% (p=.01).

# Limitations
- No control arm; pre/post design.
- Single health system; medical pediatric specialties (not SLP/OT therapeutic disciplines).
- Small per-specialty subsamples.
- Caregiver satisfaction increase modest and survey-based.
- 6-month observation; long-term effects unknown.

# Builder takeaways
- This is the strongest pediatric-specific evidence on AI scribes — modest but real time savings, sizable cognitive-load reduction, meaningful burnout drop.
- Pediatric medical providers are NOT pediatric SLP/OT — different note structure, IEP requirements, parent narratives. Do not extrapolate effect sizes blindly.
- The 1.5 hrs/week reclaimed is the unit of value to communicate to clinicians and clinic owners.
- Caregiver satisfaction did move (statistically), suggesting AI-scribe-enabled face-time is parent-perceptible — useful UX justification for any scribe in pediatric autism services.
