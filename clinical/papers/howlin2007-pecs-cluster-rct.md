# Question
Does PECS teacher-training and consultancy improve communication outcomes for children with autism in classroom settings?

# Method
- Pragmatic cluster RCT, 18 schools randomized to 3 arms (immediate, delayed, no treatment).
- 84 children, mean age 6.8y.
- Intervention: 2-day PECS workshop for teachers + 6 half-day school-based consultancy sessions over 5 months.
- Outcomes: classroom observation of communicative initiations, PECS use, speech frequency, ADOS-G communication/social, formal language tests.

# Findings
- Initiation rate: OR 2.72 favoring intervention.
- PECS symbol use: OR 3.90 favoring intervention.
- No increase in spontaneous speech.
- No improvement in ADOS-G or formal language tests.
- Treatment gains NOT maintained after active intervention ceased.

# Limitations
- Short intervention (5 months).
- Maintenance after withdrawal was poor — durable effects require ongoing implementation.
- Cluster design power constraints.

# Quotes
> "Treatment effects were not maintained once active intervention ceased."

# Builder takeaways
- Best PECS RCT to date — confirms PECS produces requesting behavior in trained settings but does NOT produce speech or generalize.
- "Maintenance fails on withdrawal" is the single most important finding for any wellness/education product: ongoing low-friction prompting matters more than intensive bursts.
- Software product implication: design for sustained daily use over years, not intensive 8-week blocks.
