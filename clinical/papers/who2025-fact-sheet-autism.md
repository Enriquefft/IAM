# Question
What is WHO's official position on autism prevalence, characteristics, and care needs as of the 2025 fact-sheet update?

# Method
- Consensus document; not a primary study.
- Cites GBD 2021 (Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation) for prevalence estimates.
- Last updated 17 September 2025.

# Findings
- WHO official global prevalence: about 1 in 127 persons had autism in 2021 (sourced from GBD 2021).
- WHO acknowledges substantial cross-study variation; some well-controlled studies report higher figures.
- Prevalence in many low- and middle-income countries is unknown.
- Autism is described as a group of diverse conditions related to brain development.
- Characteristics may be detected in early childhood; diagnosis often comes much later.
- Abilities and needs vary and evolve over time; some live independently, others require lifelong care and support.
- Evidence-based psychosocial interventions improve communication and social skills.
- Care requires community- and societal-level accessibility and inclusivity actions.
