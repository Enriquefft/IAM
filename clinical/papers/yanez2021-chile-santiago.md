# Question
What is the prevalence of autism spectrum disorder in toddlers attending well-child visits in two urban Santiago communes — first such estimate for Chile?

# Method
- Cross-sectional epidemiological investigation.
- M-CHAT screening; positive screens evaluated by pediatric neurologist using ADOS-2.
- Consecutive sampling.

# Findings
- Estimated ASD prevalence: 1.95% (95% CI 0.81–4.63).
- Sex ratio: 4 boys per 1 girl.
- First ASD prevalence estimate in any Santiago commune.

# Effect sizes
| Outcome | Measure | Effect | 95% CI | p |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ASD prevalence | proportion | 1.95% | 0.81–4.63% | n/r |

# Limitations
- Small sample (n=272); wide CI.
- Two urban communes; not representative of Chile or rural areas.
- Toddler age (18–30 months) — early ages where some ASD presentations may not yet be diagnosable.
- M-CHAT screen-positive rate may inflate apparent prevalence.

# Quotes
> "The first estimate of ASD prevalence in two communes of Santiago, Chile." — Conclusions

# Builder takeaways
- Aligns with Roman-Urrestarazu 2025 Bayesian projection (1.31%) — toddler-screen and EHR-projected national rate are consistent within order of magnitude.
- Confirms Chile is the LATAM country with the most credible primary-data prevalence estimates.
- Toddler-screen prevalence (~2%) supports the case that LATAM under-detection (Peru 0%, Colombia ~0.05%) is sampling, not biology.
