# Question
Does FaceSay (computer-based avatar social-skills training) improve face/emotion recognition and playground social interaction in children with autism vs control?

# Method
- RCT, n=49 children ages 6–15.
- Twice-weekly ~20-min sessions, ≥6 weeks, three FaceSay interactive games (avatar-mediated).
- LFA and HFA subgroups analyzed separately.
- Outcomes: face recognition, emotion recognition, playground social interaction (observed).

# Findings
- LFA group: improvements in emotion recognition and social interactions.
- HFA group: improvements in all three target areas (face recognition, emotion recognition, social interactions).
- Children using FaceSay used more eye contact and better followed playmates' eye gaze on playground (observed).
- This is a pre-LLM-era intervention (avatar-based, scripted, not generative).

# Effect sizes
| Outcome | Measure | Effect | 95% CI | p |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Face recognition (HFA) | Improvement vs control | significant | not extracted | <.05 reported |
| Emotion recognition (LFA & HFA) | Improvement vs control | significant | not extracted | <.05 reported |
| Social interaction (LFA & HFA) | Observed playground | significant | not extracted | <.05 reported |

# Limitations
- Total n=49 modest; subgroup analyses underpowered.
- Pre-LLM technology — avatar with scripted feedback, not conversational AI.
- Short follow-up; no maintenance data.
- Single-site (UAB), US sample.

# Quotes
> Children with HFA improved in all three measured areas (face recognition, emotion recognition, social interactions); LFA improved in emotion recognition and social interactions. — Results

# Builder takeaways
- Pre-LLM-era, but the canonical RCT for avatar-mediated computer-based social skills training. When discussing "chatbots for kids" evidence, this is the comparator — and it's a small effect from a structured non-generative intervention.
- The effective ingredient was structured practice on a constrained task with avatar feedback, not free-form conversation. Mirror this in any LLM-era successor.
- Generalization to playground (real-world) was the key result — measure off-platform behavior, not just in-app metrics.
