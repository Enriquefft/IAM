# Question
What is the prevalence of DSM-5 sensory behaviors in children with autism spectrum disorder vs children with other special educational needs?

# Method
- Multicenter case-control study.
- DSM-5 Criterion B.4 sensory behaviors assessed.

# Findings
- Atypical sensory behavior in 92% of children with ASD vs 67% of SEN-non-ASD children.
- Greater sensory dysfunction associated with higher autism severity (especially restricted/repetitive behaviors) and behavior problems.
- No significant correlation between sensory dysfunction and IQ.

# Limitations
- Single age band (10–14 years).
- ASD vs SEN comparator (not typically developing) — narrows specificity.
- DSM-5 sensory items are a subset of full sensory profiling.

# Quotes
> "Atypical sensory behaviour was reported in 92% of ASD and 67% of SEN children." — Results

# Builder takeaways
- 92% confirms sensory atypicality is near-universal in ASD as DSM-5 framed it.
- Differential of 25 percentage points vs SEN-non-ASD is the discriminative signal — sensory atypicality alone is not autism-specific, but the magnitude is greater.
- Independence from IQ means sensory accommodations apply equally across cognitive levels.
