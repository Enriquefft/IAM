# Question
What is the synthesized evidence across meta-analyses for naturalistic developmental behavioral interventions (NDBIs) in children with ASD under 8?

# Method
- Overview of 5 meta-analyses (6 reports).
- NDBI subtypes covered: ESDM (2 reviews), Pivotal Response Treatment (2 reviews), NDBI broadly (1 review).
- Outcomes: communication/language, cognition, adaptive behavior, autism symptomatology, restricted/repetitive behaviors.

# Findings
- Positive effects on communication/language, cognition, adaptive behavior across reviews.
- Mixed effects on autism symptomatology and restricted/repetitive behaviors.
- Moderators: proximity of outcome to intervention, boundedness of outcome to intervention, study location.

# Effect sizes
| Outcome | Direction | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Language/communication | + | consistently positive across reviews |
| Cognition | + | positive |
| Adaptive behavior | + | positive (smaller) |
| Autism symptoms | mixed | direction inconsistent |
| Repetitive behaviors | mixed | direction inconsistent |

# Limitations
- Overview of reviews — does not produce new pooled estimate.
- Some reviewed meta-analyses included overlapping studies.
- Heterogeneous moderators across reviews.

# Quotes
> "Positive effects of NDBI for young children with ASD are supported by meta-analytic evidence."

# Builder takeaways
- NDBI as a class is the most evidence-supported framework for early autism intervention.
- Effects strongest on proximal, "bounded" outcomes (communication, language) — directly target what you measure.
- Autism symptom severity and stereotypies are less reliable targets — frame products around skills, not symptom reduction.
