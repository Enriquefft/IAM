# Question
What is the journey to autism diagnosis and post-diagnosis support access for families in Ecuador?

# Method
- Cross-sectional survey via Qualtrics.
- 767 families; 651 diagnosed, 116 not yet diagnosed.

# Findings
- Atypical development noticed: peak detection at 12–24 months (47%, n=361); broader range 6–48 months.
- Age at diagnosis: 31% before 36 months; 38% ages 3–4; 12% ages 5–6.
- Most families consulted a professional within 6 months of first concerns (75%).
- Most consulted ≥4 different professionals before confirmation.
- Payment for assessment: 67% private out-of-pocket; 16% national health services.
- 45% received diagnosis from the first professional consulted.
- Persistent barriers cited: lack of information, insufficient services, professional knowledge gaps.

# Limitations
- Online survey; selection bias toward urban, internet-connected, mid-to-high-SES families.
- Self-report only.
- Excludes families that never sought professional consultation.

# Builder takeaways
- Out-of-pocket private payment dominates Ecuadorian autism diagnosis — direct-to-consumer pricing is viable for the diagnostic-adjacent product space.
- The "≥4 professionals before confirmation" pattern defines a clear product opportunity: triage/intake support tools that reduce churn.
- 12–24 month peak first-concern aligns with global literature — early-warning content optimally positioned for parents of toddlers.
- Validates Ecuador as a Peru-similar market (private-pay, urban-concentrated, professional-scarce).
