# Question
Synthesize and meta-analyze the efficacy of social-robot interventions for autistic children/youth across social, emotional, and motor domains.

# Method
- Systematic review with meta-analysis of RCTs and non-RCTs.
- 40 studies met inclusion (17 RCTs, 23 non-RCT).
- Meta-analysis pooled 12 RCTs (n=346); Hedges' g, random-effects.
- Quality assessed; only 17% rated strong, most weak/moderate; 95% lacked blinded outcome assessors.

# Findings
- Combined effect across 12 RCTs: g = 0.33 (95% CI 0.08–0.57), I² = 54.48% — small significant pooled benefit with moderate heterogeneity.
- Social outcomes (k=7): g = 0.35 (95% CI 0.09–0.61), I² = 0.00 — small significant.
- Emotional outcomes (k=2): g = 0.63 (95% CI -1.43 to 2.69), I² = 88.65 — non-significant, very wide CI.
- Motor outcomes (k=3): g = -0.10 (95% CI -1.08 to 0.89), I² = 79.63 — null.
- Robot platforms used: NAO most common (17 studies), then QTrobot, CommU, ACTROID-F, Isobot, Tito, iRobiQ, CARO, Keepon, HUMANE, Probo, Pleo, Zoomer, POL.
- 67% humanoid, 27% animaloid, others robotic arm/plant.
- Age moderator significant (p=.02); younger children showed larger effects (r²=.32).
- Setting moderator: clinic g=0.57 (95% CI 0.16–0.98); home and school showed minimal/negative effects.
- 65% of RCTs and 78% of non-RCTs reported positive direction; selection/publication bias plausible.
- Human therapists controlled robots ("Wizard of Oz") in 80% of interventions; true autonomy not tested.

# Limitations
- 95% of studies lacked blinded outcome assessors.
- Only 17% rated strong methodological quality.
- Median follow-up 10 weeks; minimal long-term data.
- 80% Wizard-of-Oz operation — measured effect is human-via-robot, not autonomous robot.
- Small samples per trial; minimal adverse event reporting.
- Heterogeneity high in emotional/motor outcome pools.

# Builder takeaways
- Pooled small effect (g≈0.35) on social outcomes is the best-supported claim — meaningful but modest, and almost entirely tested in clinic settings with human operators behind the curtain.
- Home and school deployments show null/negative effects; do not assume lab efficacy generalizes to home use.
- Younger children benefit more — if you target a single age band, lean younger.
- True autonomous social robots have not been rigorously tested; "robot helps autistic kid" headlines almost always describe a human therapist remote-operating.
