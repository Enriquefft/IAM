# Question
What is the prevalence and pattern of early identification of autism spectrum disorder among 4- and 8-year-old children in the United States in 2022, by sex, race/ethnicity, and ADDM site?

# Method
- Active surveillance program (ADDM Network) reviewing health and educational records.
- 16 sites across the US plus Puerto Rico (Puerto Rico data reported separately).
- ASD case ascertainment by trained reviewers using DSM-5/DSM-5-TR criteria applied to documented behaviors.
- Outcomes: prevalence per 1,000 children, age at earliest known ASD diagnosis, cumulative incidence of identification by age 48 months, intellectual disability prevalence (IQ ≤70).

# Findings
- Overall ASD prevalence among 8-year-olds: 32.2 per 1,000 (1 in 31; 3.22%).
- Prevalence varied by site from 9.7 per 1,000 (Texas-Laredo) to 53.1 per 1,000 (California).
- Boys: 49.2 per 1,000. Girls: 14.3 per 1,000. Male-to-female ratio: 3.4:1.
- Prevalence by race/ethnicity (per 1,000): Asian/Pacific Islander 38.2; Non-Hispanic Black 36.6; American Indian/Alaska Native 37.5; Hispanic 33.0; Multiracial 31.9; Non-Hispanic White 27.7 (lowest).
- Median age at earliest ASD diagnosis: 47 months; range across sites 36 months (California) to 69.5 months (Texas-Laredo).
- Intellectual disability (IQ ≤70) among children with cognitive data (61.4% of sample): 39.6% overall.
- Intellectual disability by race/ethnicity: Black 52.8%; American Indian/Alaska Native 50.0%; Asian/Pacific Islander 43.9%; Hispanic 38.8%; Multiracial 31.2%; White 32.7%.
- Cumulative incidence of ASD identification by age 48 months in birth cohort 2018 was 1.7× higher than birth cohort 2014.

# Limitations
- Surveillance dependent on quality of school and clinical records — sites with weaker documentation systematically undercount.
- Cognitive data missing for ~38.6% of cases — ID estimate biased.
- Site heterogeneity is real (5.5x range) but partly reflects record-system differences, not true prevalence.
- Cross-sectional snapshot; trend inference requires comparing prior ADDM cycles.
- US-only; not generalizable to LATAM.

# Builder takeaways
- US 1-in-31 figure is the current denominator for sizing US autism markets and for product analogies; not directly transferable to Peru/LATAM.
- Diagnostic timing varies 2x across US sites — diagnostic infrastructure (records, clinician access) is the primary lever, not actual disease.
- 39.6% intellectual disability rate in ASD anchors the "complex needs" subsegment for product accessibility design.
- Sex ratio of 3.4:1 in surveillance, declining from older 4:1 estimates, suggests historically missed girls now being identified — product design must not optimize only for the male-coded autism stereotype.
