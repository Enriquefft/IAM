# Question
What is the effectiveness of Pivotal Response Treatment (PRT) for autistic children, and what predicts treatment response?

# Method
- Umbrella review of 6 systematic reviews + new meta-analysis of 10 RCTs.
- 130 PRT, 122 controls; multiple language and behavior outcomes.

# Findings
- Communication (structured laboratory observation): SMD = 0.45 (95% CI 0.21 to 0.69), sig.
- Expressive language (CDI): SMD = 0.45 (0.16 to 0.74), sig.
- MSEL Expressive Language: SMD = 0.31 (0.04 to 0.58), sig.
- MSEL Receptive Language: SMD = 0.51 (0.23 to 0.80), sig.
- Social interaction (VABS): SMD = 0.10 (-0.16 to 0.37), NS.
- Adaptive functioning, cognitive ability, autism symptom severity: NS.
- Only 4 RCTs examined treatment-response predictors; no consistent pattern emerged.

# Limitations
- Small samples across RCTs.
- Limited long-term follow-up.
- Heterogeneous PRT delivery (parent vs clinician vs school-based).

# Quotes
> "Statistically significant effects of PRT on a range of language and communication skills were identified across a majority of ten RCTs included in this review."

> "Evidence for positive treatment effects of PRT on outcome measures assessing other domains was less robust and specific."

# Builder takeaways
- PRT shows reliable small-to-moderate language gains; null on social interaction and broader autism outcomes.
- PRT's "pivotal" mechanisms — motivation, child choice, response-reinforcer contingency — are software-translatable in language-learning apps.
- Same caveat as ABA broadly: skill-acquisition framing is empirically supported; symptom-reduction framing is not.
