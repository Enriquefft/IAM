# Question
What are the psycholinguistic properties (name agreement, familiarity, image agreement, visual complexity, naming RT, age of acquisition) of high-frequency ARASAAC pictograms in Spanish?

# Method
- 295 high-frequency ARASAAC pictograms presented to ~219 native Spanish university students across multiple tasks.
- Tasks: name production, conceptual familiarity rating, image-agreement rating, visual-complexity rating, tip-of-the-tongue, naming response time.
- Split-half reliability computed.

# Findings
- Name agreement low: only 36% of ARASAAC's labels matched the modal name participants produced.
- Reliability of measures: split-half 0.84–0.93, mean 0.89.
- Naming RT predicted by image agreement, age of acquisition, conceptual familiarity (each significant).
- Authors note prior work (Cabello & Bertola 2015) found ARASAAC had higher transparency/iconicity than PCS and Bliss.

# Limitations
- University student sample, not children with communication disabilities.
- 295 of ~13k+ pictograms only.
- Cultural specificity: Spanish (Spain) Spanish, not LATAM Spanish.
- Naming-task data may not generalize to AAC-user comprehension/recognition.

# Quotes
> "The names that have traditionally been linked to each picture in the ARASAAC database may not be the most suitable." — discussion

# Builder takeaways
- ARASAAC labels should not be treated as ground truth — when localizing for LATAM, expect to re-label for regional Spanish norms (e.g., "computadora" vs "ordenador", "carro" vs "coche").
- Age-of-acquisition data here are useful for sequencing introduction order in a learning product.
- Validates ARASAAC as iconographically strong vs. PCS/Bliss — strong choice when license cost is also a factor.
