# Question
Does Early Intensive Behavioral Intervention (EIBI), based on ABA principles delivered at 20-40 hr/week over multiple years, improve functional outcomes for young children with autism?

# Method
- Cochrane systematic review and meta-analysis.
- 5 included studies (1 RCT, 4 controlled clinical trials), 219 children.
- Outcomes: adaptive behavior, IQ, expressive language, receptive language, autism severity, problem behavior.
- GRADE quality assessment.

# Findings
- Adaptive behavior: MD 9.58 (95% CI 5.57 to 13.60), p<0.001. GRADE: low.
- IQ: MD 15.44 (95% CI 9.29 to 21.59), p<0.001. GRADE: low.
- Expressive language: SMD 0.51 (95% CI 0.12 to 0.90), p=0.01. GRADE: low.
- Receptive language: SMD 0.55 (95% CI 0.23 to 0.87), p=0.001. GRADE: low.
- Autism severity, problem behavior: NS.

# Limitations
- Only 1 RCT; 4 CCTs with allocation by parental preference.
- All effects rated GRADE low.
- Small total sample.
- Study heterogeneity (Lovaas-style EIBI vs newer protocols).

# Quotes
> "There is weak evidence that EIBI may be an effective behavioral treatment for some children with ASD; the strength of the evidence in this review is limited because it mostly comes from small studies that are not of the optimum design."

# Builder takeaways
- Cochrane's verdict: positive directional effects on IQ, adaptive behavior, language; null on autism symptoms and problem behaviors.
- "Weak evidence" qualifier is the most-cited phrase in the EIBI debate — both supporters and critics quote it.
- Clinically meaningful IQ gains (~15 points) are real but only at heroic intensity (20-40 hr/week × multiple years), incompatible with LATAM private pay.
