# Question
Does brief preschool-based JASPER intervention add value over standard 30-hour weekly ABA program for minimally verbal children with autism?

# Method
- Pilot RCT, 15 children (ages 3-5, <10 spontaneous words).
- Both groups received 30 hr/week ABA-based program.
- Treatment group: substitution of 30 min JASPER twice weekly × 12 weeks.
- Control: TAU (continued ABA).
- Outcomes: play diversity, requesting gestures, classroom engagement, joint attention.

# Findings
- Greater play diversity in JASPER group.
- More initiated requesting gestures in JASPER group.
- Less time unengaged in classroom (d = 1.63).
- Joint attention improvements: NS in this small sample.

# Effect sizes

| Joint attention | Various | NS | underpowered |

# Limitations
- Tiny sample (n=15) — pilot.
- Only minimally verbal children — generalization uncertain.
- 12-week duration only.

# Quotes
> "Even brief, targeted interventions on joint attention and play can improve core deficits in minimally verbal children with ASD."

# Builder takeaways
- Adding 1 hr/week of JASPER to existing 30 hr/week ABA produced d=1.63 reduction in classroom disengagement — disproportionate ROI on small added dose.
- Suggests software-delivered targeted JASPER-style prompts could augment any existing therapy regime cheaply.
- Minimally verbal subgroup historically excluded from intervention research — under-served and high-need market.
