# Question
What is the effect of TEACCH-based interventions on social functioning, cognition, motor skills, and autism symptom severity in children with ASD?

# Method
- Meta-analysis of 11 studies (701 individuals).
- Random-effects model, mean differences (MD).
- GRADE quality assessment per outcome.

# Findings
- Socialization: MD = 0.6 (95% CI 0.2 to 1.0), p<0.05. GRADE: high.
- Cognitive Performance Scale: MD = 1.34 (95% CI 0.09 to 2.58), p<0.05. GRADE: moderate.
- Fine motor: MD = 0.7 (95% CI 0.4 to 1.0), p<0.05. GRADE: high.
- Autism Behavior Checklist (ABC, lower = better): MD = -1.57 (95% CI -2.11 to -1.02). GRADE: moderate.
- CARS: MD = -0.7 (95% CI -1.0 to -0.3).
- Communication, imitation, daily living, gross motor: NS. GRADE: low.

# Limitations
- Small included samples; many Chinese-language sources.
- Heterogeneous TEACCH "fidelity" — structured-teaching elements vs full TEACCH program.
- Non-blinded outcome assessors common.

# Quotes
> "TEACCH can improve the social skills, cognitive performance, and fine motor skills of ASD individuals while reducing the severity of the condition."

# Builder takeaways
- Strongest TEACCH evidence is for fine motor and socialization with high GRADE.
- The intervention's signature components — visual schedules, structured environments, work systems — are the most directly software-replicable of any major intervention.
- Communication and daily-living effects NS — software augmentation should focus on schedule/structure not communication.
