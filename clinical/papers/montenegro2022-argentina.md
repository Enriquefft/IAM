# Question
How have age of diagnosis, service access, and rights enforcement for autistic individuals in Argentina changed between 2015 and 2020?

# Method
- Two independent cross-sectional caregiver surveys, 5 years apart.
- Recruitment via PANAACEA and RedEA civil organizations.
- 2015 sample: n=763; 2020 sample: n=422.
- Outcomes: age of first concern, age of diagnosis, perceived service barriers, family work disruption.

# Findings
- Diagnosis before age 3: 66.4% (2015) → 77.2% (2020).
- Diagnosis ages 4–8: 26.7% → 18.3%.
- Reported barriers (2015 → 2020):
  - Wait-list delays: 43.5% → 16.4%.
  - Treatment costs: 33.5% → 12.6%.
  - Limited information: 17.9% → 4.3%.
- Family work disruption (≥1 family member ceased working to care): >34% in both samples — no improvement.
- Caregiver priority shift: "improved implementation of existing rights" 22.1% (2015) → 44.3% (2020).

# Limitations
- Convenience sample via civil organizations — over-represents informed, urban, mid-to-high-SES caregivers.
- 2020 sample smaller than 2015.
- Both samples Argentina-based; not generalizable to other LATAM.
- Self-report only; no record verification.

# Builder takeaways
- Argentina shows the LATAM "best case" trajectory: visible 5-year improvement in diagnostic timing and barrier reduction.
- The 34%+ family-work-disruption rate across both years is the most stable structural finding — wellness/education products that reduce caregiving load have measurable economic value to families.
- Implementation-of-rights demand (rising from 22% to 44%) signals product opportunity around legal/rights navigation tools, not just clinical content.
