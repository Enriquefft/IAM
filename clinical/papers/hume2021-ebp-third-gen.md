# Question
Which focused intervention practices for autistic individuals (birth–22) meet evidence-based-practice criteria as of 2017?

# Method
- Systematic review of 972 articles published 1990–2017.
- 567 added in 2012–2017 update.
- 221 trained external reviewers.
- Quality assessment via published protocol; 90% inter-rater agreement.
- Studies: 17% group designs, 83% single-case designs.

# Findings
- 28 focused intervention practices met EBP criteria.
- Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) listed as an EBP.
- PECS reclassified as a "Manualized Intervention Meeting Criteria (MIMC)" *within* the broader AAC category — i.e., PECS qualifies as an evidence-based manualized AAC protocol.
- AAC evidence base: 44 articles (5 group designs, 39 single-case).
- Visual Supports: separately classified EBP, 65 articles (3 group, 62 single-case).
- Technology-Aided Instruction and Intervention also listed as EBP.

# Limitations
- Lit cutoff 2017; doesn't capture 2018–present app-based AAC trials.
- Demographic gaps: under-representation of non-white, non-US samples; few infants/toddlers, few young adults.
- Quality criteria favor controlled designs; emerging community-based work may be undercounted.
