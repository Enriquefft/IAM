# Question
From parents' perspective, what factors lead to their children rejecting or abandoning AAC systems?

# Method
- Qualitative semi-structured interviews with 12 parents.
- Inclusion: parent of a child with CCN who had rejected or abandoned at least one AAC system.
- Thematic analysis applied.

# Findings
Four themes captured family factors contributing to rejection/abandonment:
- Parents lacked emotional readiness and resilience to implement AAC.
- Implementing AAC was extraneous work for already-stretched parents.
- Children did not use the system to communicate in functional moments.
- Parents were dissatisfied with the AAC system itself (interface, vocabulary, voice, durability).

# Limitations
- 12 parents in one country (Australia).
- Self-selection bias — parents who agreed to discuss rejection.
- Single child-AAC pairings discussed; no tracking across system changes.
- No clinician/child triangulation.

# Quotes
> "Parents lacked the emotional readiness and resilience to implement AAC ... implementing AAC was extraneous work for parents." — themes

# Builder takeaways
- Emotional readiness is a marketable in-product feature — onboarding flows that surface peer-parent stories, validate grief, and provide low-stakes early wins reduce the "I can't do this" abandonment driver.
- "Extraneous work" → ruthless cuts to setup time. Initial 5-minute setup with smart defaults is a competitive moat vs. PCS/Boardmaker which routinely require hours of board design.
- Build for the busy parent: passive-mode features (pre-built routines, low-friction icon updates) over active customization tools.
