# Question
Can features of "pathological demand avoidance" (PDA) be operationalized using items from the Diagnostic Interview for Social and Communication Disorders (DISCO), and what is the prevalence within an ASD-leaning clinical sample?

# Method
- Retrospective DISCO assessment review.
- 11 DISCO items selected to match the proposed PDA profile.

# Findings
- Within the sample, 7.2% (11/153) met "substantial" PDA features; 10.5% (16/153) met "some" PDA features.
- All but one high-PDA case also met ASD criteria.
- Distinguishing features within ASD: apparently manipulative behavior; socially shocking behavior.
- Anxiety and rapid mood changes are common to broader ASD, not PDA-specific.

# Limitations
- Mixed-age clinical sample (DISCO training cases) — not population.
- PDA has no DSM/ICD diagnostic criteria; operationalization is study-specific.
- Small n; case-series evidence level.

# Quotes

# Builder takeaways
- ~7% PDA prevalence within an ASD clinical sample anchors the niche-but-real subpopulation; product copy can acknowledge PDA profile without claiming to address it as a diagnosis.
- "Demand framing" (rephrasing tasks to reduce demand load) is a known PDA-friendly UX pattern; relevant for any task/instruction surface.
- PDA is not in DSM-5-TR or ICD-11 — health-claim adjacent product copy must not state "treats PDA."
