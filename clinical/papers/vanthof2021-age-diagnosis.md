# Question
What is the global average age at autism spectrum disorder diagnosis from studies published 2012–2019?

# Method
- Systematic review and meta-analysis.
- Search of 1,150 articles; 56 included.

# Findings
- Mean global age at ASD diagnosis: 60.48 months (range 30.90–234.57 months).
- Mean for child-only studies (≤10 years): 43.18 months (range 30.90–74.70 months).
- Pre-2012 historical range: 38–120 months.
- 46 of the 56 studies reported on factors associated with diagnostic timing — often conflicting results.
- Authors conclude progress is being made toward earlier diagnosis but it "requires our constant attention."

# Limitations
- Wide range driven by inclusion of adult-diagnosis studies.
- 40 countries, but most studies from high-income settings.
- LATAM and African data sparse.
- Age-at-diagnosis recall by caregivers may be biased.

# Builder takeaways
- 43-month child-only mean is the canonical "early childhood diagnosis age" — products targeting newly-diagnosed families should expect average user age 3.5–5 years at diagnosis.
- Adult-diagnosis distribution (heavy right tail to 234 months ~ 19.5 years) defines the late-diagnosed adult subpopulation — niche but growing.
- LATAM 46-month median (Montiel-Nava 2024) is roughly aligned with global 43-month child-only mean — gap is smaller than expected, but variance and wait-list times are larger.
