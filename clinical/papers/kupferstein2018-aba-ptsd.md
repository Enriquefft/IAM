# Question
Are autistic individuals exposed to ABA in childhood more likely to meet PTSD symptom criteria than those who were not exposed?

# Method
- Online cross-sectional survey, snowball/convenience sample.
- 460 respondents (parents reporting on their children, plus autistic adults).
- Measure: PTSD Checklist (PCL) symptom report; ABA exposure self/parent-reported.
- Comparison: ABA-exposed vs ABA-unexposed.

# Findings
- ABA-exposed group reported PTSD symptoms at higher rates: ABA participants were reported to be 86% more likely to meet PTSD diagnostic criteria threshold than ABA-unexposed peers.
- Nearly half of ABA-exposed respondents met PTSD symptom criteria.

# Limitations
- Convenience sample recruited via autism advocacy networks (anti-ABA selection bias likely).
- Self/parent-report PTSD measure, not clinician diagnosis.
- No control for severity of autism, co-occurring trauma, baseline mental health.
- ABA "exposure" not characterized by hours, era, fidelity, or protocol type (1980s Lovaas vs current NDBI lumped).
- Methodological critiques published (Leaf et al. 2018) arguing causal inference unsupported.
- Published in non-PubMed-indexed journal at time; subsequently widely cited by both critics and defenders.
