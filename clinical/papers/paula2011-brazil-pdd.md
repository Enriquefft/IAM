# Question
What is the prevalence of pervasive developmental disorder (PDD) in a typical Brazilian town — first prevalence estimate for Brazil and South America?

# Method
- Three-phase epidemiological study in a typical southeast Brazilian town (Atibaia).
- Pre-DSM-5 (PDD terminology, including autistic disorder, PDD-NOS, Asperger).

# Findings
- PDD prevalence: 27.2 per 10,000 (95% CI 17.6–36.8) ~ 0.27%.
- Male preponderance.
- More cases of PDD-NOS than autistic disorder (typical of pre-DSM-5 era).
- Half of cases born to older mothers.
- Authors describe estimate as likely an underestimation due to methodological limits.

# Limitations
- Single-town pilot; not nationally representative.
- Pre-DSM-5 (PDD subtypes).
- Authors explicitly state likely underestimation.
- 2010 fieldwork.

# Builder takeaways
- Brazil's only published prevalence study is a 2011 pilot from one small town. National rate is unknown.
- 0.27% is roughly 1/3 of Mexico's rate (Fombonne 2016) and 1/5 of Chile's projected rate (Roman-Urrestarazu 2025) — likely reflects vintage and methodology, not true difference.
- Used by GBD modelers as the only LATAM data input for Brazil — drives Tropical LATAM regional estimate.
