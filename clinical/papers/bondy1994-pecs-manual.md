# Question
What is the structured, manualized protocol for teaching the Picture Exchange Communication System (PECS)?

# Method
- Original PECS Training Manual, Cherry Hill NJ.
- Authors: Andy Bondy (BCBA) and Lori Frost (SLP).
- Published 1994; updated subsequently (2nd ed 2002).
- Defines 6 phases of PECS instruction.

# Findings
- PECS Phase I: how to communicate (single-picture exchange with prompted physical assistance).
- PECS Phase II: distance and persistence (carrying picture to communication partner across distance).
- PECS Phase III: picture discrimination (selecting between two or more pictures).
- PECS Phase IV: sentence structure (using "I want" + picture sentence strip).
- PECS Phase V: responding to "What do you want?" question.
- PECS Phase VI: commenting (responding to "What do you see?", "What do you have?", etc.).
- Theoretical roots: applied behavior analysis (Skinner's verbal behavior) + AAC principles.
- Pyramid Educational Consultants (Bondy & Frost's company) holds training certification authority.

# Limitations
- This is a protocol manual, not an efficacy study — efficacy is documented in subsequent literature ([flippin2010-pecs-meta], [howlin2007-pecs-rct], [charlop2002-pecs-multibaseline]).
- Pyramid charges for official training and certification; "PECS" is trademarked — informal implementations cannot legally use the PECS name.
- Most published research only reaches Phase III–IV; Phase V–VI under-studied.

# Builder takeaways
- "PECS" as a name is trademark-protected; a product can implement the *six-phase protocol* but cannot brand it as PECS without licensing.
- Phase IV (sentence strips) is the under-served gap — meta-analyses ([flippin2010-pecs-meta]) suggest Phase IV is where speech outcomes start emerging, yet few real-world implementations get past Phase III.
- For LATAM education products, "picture exchange protocol" or "intercambio de pictogramas" is fine to use generically; reserve "PECS" for actual licensed/certified content.
- The Pyramid certification model is a reference for how clinical credibility could be packaged (training revenue + product revenue).
