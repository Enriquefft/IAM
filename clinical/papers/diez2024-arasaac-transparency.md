# Question
How transparent (guessable without label) and translucent (rated relationship to label) are the most-used ARASAAC pictograms?

# Method
- 1525 ARASAAC pictograms from the most-frequent subset.
- Transparency task: 248 participants generate words for unlabeled pictograms.
- Translucency task: 253 participants rate label-pictogram fit on 7-point scale.
- Correlations with imageability, concreteness, age of acquisition.

# Findings
- Mean transparency 0.39 (SD 0.34) — relatively low overall.
- Nouns transparency 0.44; verbs and adjectives substantially lower.
- Mean translucency 6.33 / 7 (SD 0.82) — high when label is provided.
- 53.2% of pictograms (713) had a different modal name than the ARASAAC-assigned label.
- Pictograms with label-name match: transparency 0.66 vs 0.08 for mismatched.
- Imageability and concreteness most strongly correlated with transparency/translucency.

# Limitations
- Adult Spanish (Spain) raters; not children, not AAC users, not LATAM Spanish speakers.
- Subset of pictograms only.
- Transparency task vulnerable to free-recall variability.

# Quotes
> "Mean transparency was 0.39 (SD = 0.34), indicating relatively low levels of guessability across the dataset." — results

# Builder takeaways
- Verbs and adjectives are substantially less guessable than nouns — for a product, animate verbs (animations, microvideo) where static pictograms underperform.
- 53% of pictograms have label mismatch — when shipping ARASAAC, do not trust the default name; relabel against an in-product user base or LATAM word-frequency list.
- Imageability and concreteness drive icon recognition — for abstract concepts, supplement with photos, gestures, or short clips rather than relying on a single pictogram.
