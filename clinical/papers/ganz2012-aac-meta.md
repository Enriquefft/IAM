# Question
Across single-case research, what are the effects of aided AAC on communication, social skills, academic skills, and challenging behavior in individuals with ASD, and which AAC modality produces the largest effects?

# Method
- Meta-analysis of 24 single-case experimental design studies.
- Effect sizes computed as Improvement Rate Difference (IRD).
- Compared three AAC categories: PECS, SGDs, other picture-based systems.
- Outcomes coded: communication, social, academic, challenging-behavior.

# Findings
- Aided AAC interventions had *large* effects on targeted behavioral outcomes for individuals with ASD overall.
- Communication outcomes had the largest effects relative to other outcome categories.
- PECS and SGDs produced larger effects than "other picture-based systems."
- No evidence that high-tech necessarily exceeds low-tech within structured comparisons.

# Limitations
- Single-case designs only; limits generalizability.
- IRD is a non-parametric effect index; harder to compare to Cohen's d.
- Study quality varies; few comparisons control for therapist effects.
- Specific numeric IRDs for each comparison not in abstract.

# Builder takeaways
- Across the aided-AAC field, structured systems (PECS, SGD apps) outperform ad-hoc picture boards. A product should ship a *system*, not just a symbol library.
- Communication skills are the strongest outcome domain — anchor product positioning here.
- The PECS/SGD parity (no clear winner) means the choice between "symbol exchange protocol" and "speech-generating tap-to-speak" is a UX choice, not an efficacy one.
