# Question
What is the global prevalence of autism spectrum disorder (and historical subtypes), and how does it vary by region, sex, and intellectual disability comorbidity?

# Method
- Three-level mixed-effects meta-analysis.
- Systematic search of Web of Science, PubMed, EMBASE, PsycINFO through July 2020.
- Subgroup analyses by region, country, diagnostic ascertainment method, study year, sex, ID comorbidity.

# Findings
- Global pooled ASD prevalence: 72 per 10,000 (95% CI 61–85; ~0.72%).
- Subtype prevalence per 10,000: Autistic Disorder 25 (18–33); Asperger Syndrome 13 (7–20); PDD-NOS/Atypical Autism 18 (10–28).
- Regional pooled prevalence: North America 1.01% (highest); Europe 0.73%; Asia 0.41%.
- Country extremes: USA 1.12% (highest); Taiwan 0.11% (lowest).
- Insufficient data for pooled estimates in Africa, Oceania, and (notably) Latin America.
- Male-to-female ratio overall ASD: 4.3 (SD 1.5); Asperger Syndrome: 9.7 (SD 8.0); Atypical Autism/PDD-NOS: 7.0 (SD 3.0).
- Intellectual disability (IQ ≤70) co-occurrence: ASD 34.9% ± 13.5; Autistic Disorder 60.0% ± 23.8; Asperger Syndrome 3.4% ± 4.5.
- Prevalence highest in records-review surveillance studies (1.22%); lowest from health-insurance database studies (0.35%).
- Substantial residual heterogeneity across all subgroups.

# Limitations
- Search closed July 2020 — predates full GBD 2021 redesign.
- LATAM (and African, Oceanian) data sparse; no regional pooled estimate possible.
- Heterogeneity I² high; pooled point estimates mask wide variation in case ascertainment.
- DSM-IV-era subtypes still represented; not directly comparable to DSM-5 unified ASD.
- Survey methodology (records review vs population screening vs insurance claims) drove much of the regional difference.

# Builder takeaways
- Confirms the diagnostic-method bias: surveillance studies always exceed insurance-claim studies by 3-4x. Useful when reading any LATAM "low" prevalence headline.
- M:F ratio of 4.3:1 is the historical average; converging clinical evidence (Shaw 2025: 3.4:1; GBD 2021: 2.1:1) suggests this ratio is shrinking as detection improves in girls.
- ID comorbidity at ~35% is the canonical "one in three" autism+ID figure; product accessibility design should plan for cognitive variability across at least this fraction of users.
- Absence of Latin America from regional pooling is itself a finding — there is no LATAM mean to anchor against.
