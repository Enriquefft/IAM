# Question
Does the Floreo Building Social Connections VR module, delivered as adjunct to ABA therapy over 12 weeks, improve social-communication skills measured by the Autism Impact Measure (AIM)?

# Method
- RCT across Cortica's 17 US autism therapy centers.
- Floreo VR (mobile/Quest) supplementing ABA over 12 weeks.
- Primary outcome: AIM communication subscale.

# Findings
- Significant improvement in communication scores in Floreo arm.
- "Clinically meaningful improvements in targeted social communication behaviors."
- 95.1% of participants in earlier Floreo pivotal cohort mastered ≥1 new skill.
- No serious adverse events reported in Floreo's overall trial program.

# Limitations
- Detailed methods not yet inspected (announcement-level access).
- Industry-sponsored.
- Sample size, control specification, drop-out not yet extracted.
- No LATAM site participation.
- Distinct from the pivotal FDA De Novo submission (April 2026).

# Builder takeaways
- Floreo is the most credibly evidenced autism VR product as of 2026 — FDA Breakthrough Device + TAP Program designation; De Novo submission April 2026.
- US Cortica-network distribution model is not transferable to LATAM markets.
- Underlying mechanism (structured social-scenario practice with predictable cues) is software-replicable in 2D — VR is delivery, not active ingredient.
- Builder caveat: Floreo's evidence will be cited heavily by US payors. LATAM payors will need separate evidence built locally.
