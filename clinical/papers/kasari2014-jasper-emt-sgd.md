# Question
Among minimally verbal school-age children with autism, does adding a speech-generating device (SGD) at the *start* of a blended developmental/behavioral intervention produce more spontaneous communicative utterances than spoken-language intervention alone?

# Method
- Sequential Multiple Assignment Randomized Trial (SMART), 3 sites.
- Stage 1 (12 weeks): random assignment to JASP+EMT (Joint Attention, Symbolic Play, Engagement and Regulation + Enhanced Milieu Teaching) vs. JASP+EMT+SGD.
- Stage 2 (12 weeks): for slow responders, re-randomization to intensify or add SGD.
- 3-month post-treatment follow-up.
- Primary outcome: total spontaneous communicative utterances during a Natural Language Sample.

# Findings
- Adding SGD at start produced significantly more spontaneous communicative utterances than spoken-only intervention.
- Effects also observed for novel words and comments.
- Adaptive trajectory: starting with SGD and intensifying SGD for slow responders gave the best post-treatment outcomes.
- Children who responded slowly to spoken-language intervention benefited from later SGD addition.

# Effect sizes
| Outcome | Measure | Effect | 95% CI | p |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Spontaneous communicative utterances | Stage 1 group difference | favored JASP+EMT+SGD | reported significant | <.05 |

| Comments | Stage 1 difference | favored JASP+EMT+SGD | reported significant | <.05 |

# Limitations
- Modest n=61.
- 24-week active intervention; long-term maintenance not assessed beyond 3 months.
- Therapist-delivered, high fidelity — generalizability to home-only use uncertain.
- SGD device was specific (not generalizable to all tablet apps).

# Quotes
> "Beginning treatment with a speech-generating device ... was associated with greater gains in spontaneous communicative utterances." — abstract

# Builder takeaways
- This is one of the strongest RCTs supporting *early* introduction of high-tech AAC to minimally verbal autistic children — counter-evidence to the "wait-and-see" or "speech-first" stance some clinicians take.
- A wellness/education product should be marketable from the *start* of communication therapy, not as a fallback after spoken-language attempts fail.
- Adaptive intervention design matters: in-app responsiveness ("if user not progressing in 4 weeks, surface SGD/extra modeling features") aligns with evidence.
