# Question
Which wearable solutions exist for physiological and emotional monitoring for people with ASD and their caregivers, and how validated are they?

# Method
- Narrative systematic review of commercial and research-stage wearables.
- Included Empatica E4, Embrace, Reveal (Awake Labs), Pip, Affectiva Q-Sensor, Bodymedia SenseWear, etc.

# Findings
- Most commercial devices not validated against clinical reference standards in ASD populations.
- HRV (PPG-based) is the most-studied signal; EDA second.
- Few devices designed specifically for autism population — most are general-population wearables repurposed.
- Major data-quality gaps in real-world wear (motion artifact, sweat, compliance).

# Limitations
- Includes pre-2018 devices; market has shifted (E4 sunset 2024 → EmbracePlus; Reveal evolved into B2B platform).
- Not autism-specific evidence pooling.

# Quotes
> "few [devices] have been validated and evaluated in terms of their usefulness to consumers"

# Builder takeaways
- Reinforces: wearable autism market is hardware-led, evidence-poor.
- Most peer-reviewed work uses E4 / EmbracePlus (research-grade); consumer wearables (Apple Watch, Garmin, Oura, WHOOP) lack ASD-specific validation.
- For LATAM: only path is BYO smartphone-paired wearable + a software layer that interprets — no LATAM-priced autism-specific wearable exists.
