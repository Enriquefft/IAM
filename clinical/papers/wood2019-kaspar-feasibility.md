# Question
Is a randomized controlled trial of Kaspar humanoid robot for autism social-skills feasible, and does Kaspar improve social skills?

# Method
- Two-year NIHR-funded feasibility RCT.
- 40 children recruited from NHS clinics.
- Kaspar (Univ Hertfordshire's developed minimally expressive humanoid robot) intervention vs control.

# Findings
- ~170 children internationally have interacted with Kaspar across study program.
- Kaspar interactions associated with turn-taking, collaborative skills, tactile social interaction in qualitative observations.
- The wider research literature relies on single case reports; methodological rigor lacking.

# Limitations
- Feasibility design rather than full efficacy trial.
- Reliant on single team's robot platform — generalizability of Kaspar findings to other social robots unclear.

# Quotes
> "the literature regarding the use of social robots in interventions with children with autism is currently reliant on single case reports"

# Builder takeaways
- Kaspar is research-only — not commercially available. Demonstrates that the social-robot evidence base for autism is dominated by single-team programs.
- Kouroupa 2022 meta-analysis aggregates across all platforms; individual-platform RCT evidence remains weak.
