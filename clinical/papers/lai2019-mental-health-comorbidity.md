# Question
What is the pooled prevalence of co-occurring mental health diagnoses (psychiatric comorbidities) in the autism population, and how does it compare to the general population?

# Method
- Systematic review and meta-analysis.
- 96 studies meta-analyzed (100 eligible; 432 full-text reviewed; 9,746 records screened).
- Outcomes: pooled prevalence per psychiatric category with 95% CI.
- Subgroup analyses by clinical vs population vs registry sample, age, sex, country, intellectual functioning.

# Findings
Pooled prevalence (95% CI):
- ADHD: 28% (25–32)
- Anxiety disorders: 20% (17–23)
- Sleep-wake disorders: 13% (9–17)
- Disruptive/impulse-control/conduct disorders: 12% (10–15)
- Depressive disorders: 11% (9–13)
- OCD: 9% (7–10)
- Bipolar disorders: 5% (3–6)
- Schizophrenia spectrum disorders: 4% (3–5)

- Clinical-sample studies report higher prevalence than population/registry studies (selection bias).
- All estimates higher than respective general-population rates.
- Age, gender, intellectual functioning, country contribute heterogeneity.

# Limitations
- High heterogeneity across studies; some not explained by predictors.
- Sleep-wake and "disruptive" categories use heterogeneous case definitions.
- Population-vs-clinical sampling drives much of variation — true population rates likely closer to lower end of ranges.
- Older studies use DSM-IV-era criteria.

# Builder takeaways
- Use Lai 2019 as the canonical citation for any per-category comorbidity figure in autism — it is the most comprehensive synthesis to date.
- The "1 in 4 ASD has ADHD; 1 in 5 has anxiety" framing maps directly to product feature-prioritization.
- Co-occurring sleep, anxiety, and ADHD are the three highest-prevalence non-core conditions — wellness/education product roadmaps that ignore these exclude the majority of the autistic population.
