# Question
What proportion of PACT's long-term symptom-severity benefit is mediated by early changes in child communication initiation?

# Method
- Mediation analysis of 121 children from the PACT 6-year follow-up.
- 59 PACT, 62 TAU; mean age 10.5 years.
- Mediator: child communication initiations at trial midpoint.
- Outcome: ADOS CSS at follow-up; adaptive behavior outcomes.

# Findings
- 73% of overall PACT treatment effect on follow-up CSS was mediated by increased child initiation at trial midpoint.
- Adaptive-behavior mediation effects weaker but present.
- Confirms causal model: early communication initiation drives downstream autism-symptom reduction.

# Effect sizes
| Outcome | Measure | Mediation % | 95% CI | p |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ADOS CSS at follow-up | ADOS | 73% mediated by midpoint initiations | n/r | sig |

# Limitations
- Single trial cohort; replication needed.
- Mediation analysis requires assumption that midpoint mediator was unaffected by other unmeasured variables.

# Quotes
> "Early sustained increase in an autistic child's communication initiation with their caregiver is largely responsible for the long-term effects from PACT therapy on autistic and adaptive behaviour outcomes."

# Builder takeaways
- Most precise mechanistic finding in the autism early-intervention literature: increase initiation rate → long-term symptom reduction.
- Software should measure and increase child initiation rate during parent-child play as primary KPI.
- Justifies scoring/dashboards built around initiation count + initiation diversity vs vague "engagement" metrics.
