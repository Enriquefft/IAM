# Summary
Parent-mediated interventions (PMIs) are the **structurally most LATAM-realistic** delivery model for early autism intervention given therapist scarcity and cost constraints. The strongest evidence cluster: **PACT** (Lancet RCT series, [green2010-pact-rct], [pickles2016-pact-followup], [green2022-pact-g]) — parent synchrony d=1.22 and **long-term autism symptom severity benefit at 6-year follow-up** (the only parent-mediated intervention with this finding). **Hanen More Than Words** ([carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct]) shows reliable parent responsivity gains (Δ=0.71) with child outcomes moderated by baseline object interest. **JASPER** ([kasari2006-jasper-rct], [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd]) targets joint attention and play; consistent gains in proximal targets. **Project ImPACT** has telehealth RCT evidence ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]) with a critical caveat: **self-directed (no coach) delivery did not produce mediated effects**. **EarlyBird** ([dawson-squibb2019-earlybird-scoping]) lacks RCT evidence despite serving 27,000+ families across 14 countries — a striking implementation/evidence gap. The unifying mechanism across all PMIs is **changing parent behavior to change parent-child interaction quality**; the evidence consistently shows reliable parent-behavior change and contingent child-outcome change.

# Programmes covered

## PACT (Preschool Autism Communication Trial / Therapy)
- **Origin**: Aldred, Slonims, Green (UK, 2004 → Lancet 2010). Manchester / King's College London.
- **Mechanism**: SLT/early-interventionist coaches parent through weekly video review of parent-child interaction. Targets: synchrony, sensitive responsiveness, expansion of child communicative bids.
- **Dose**: 12 fortnightly clinic sessions over 6 months + monthly maintenance for 6 months. Daily 30-min home practice.
- **Evidence**: [green2010-pact-rct] n=152 RCT; [pickles2016-pact-followup] 6-year follow-up sustained effect; [green2022-pact-g] PACT-G classroom variant n=248; [carruthers2023-pact-mediation] mediation via early child communication initiation.
- **Effect sizes**: parent synchrony d=1.22, child initiations d=0.41, ADOS-CSS treatment effect at 6yr (Pickles 2016).
- **Licensing**: PACT manual published; certified PACT trainers via Manchester. Implementation requires trained clinicians.

## Hanen More Than Words (HMTW)
- **Origin**: The Hanen Centre (Toronto, founded 1975 by Ayala Manolson). HMTW launched 1995 specifically for autism.
- **Mechanism**: SLT-led group + individual parent coaching. Targets: parent responsivity, child communication function (3 P's: People-, Activity-, Object-Oriented children).
- **Dose**: 8 group sessions × 2.5 hr + 3 home visits with video feedback over ~3.5 months.
- **Evidence**: [carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct] n=62 RCT — parent responsivity Δ=0.71 at 5 mo, 0.50 at 9 mo. Child outcomes NS overall, but moderated by baseline object interest (lower object interest → larger HMTW benefit).
- **Effect sizes**: parent Δ=0.50–0.71; child outcomes contingent.
- **Licensing**: Hanen-certified SLTs only; certification ~CAD $2,500. Strong international network including LATAM (MX, AR, CL, BR, CO).

## JASPER (Joint Attention, Symbolic Play, Engagement and Regulation)
- **Origin**: Connie Kasari, UCLA, 2000s.
- **Mechanism**: NDBI; therapist (or coached parent) follows child's lead in play, embeds joint-attention and symbolic-play targets, scaffolds regulation. Often combined with EMT (Enhanced Milieu Teaching).
- **Dose**: 30-min sessions, 2–3 ×/week × 12 weeks typical for trials.
- **Evidence**: [kasari2006-jasper-rct] n=58 RCT — joint attention and symbolic play gains over ABA-only control; [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd] n=61 SMART trial — adding SGD to JASP+EMT increased socially communicative utterances; [goods2013-jasper-pilot] n=15 pilot.
- **Effect sizes**: joint attention initiations significantly higher; symbolic play diversity increased; spontaneous communicative utterances higher with SGD adjunct.
- **Licensing**: JASPER certification through UCLA Kasari lab; online + in-person training available.

## Project ImPACT (Improving Parents As Communication Teachers)
- **Origin**: Brooke Ingersoll, Michigan State University, 2010s.
- **Mechanism**: parent-mediated NDBI manualized for community use. Teaches parents 6 strategy domains (focus, adjust, create opportunities, teach, model, prompt).
- **Dose**: 12–24 weeks; 1 session/week with coach (clinic, home, or telehealth).
- **Evidence**: [ingersoll2013-impact-pilot] n=8 single-subject; [ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth] n=64 3-arm RCT — therapist-assisted telehealth ImPACT had **indirect effect on expressive language via parent strategy use**; **self-directed ImPACT alone did not produce mediated effects**.
- **Effect sizes**: indirect mediated effect on expressive language; direct effect on child outcomes NS in 3-arm trial.
- **Licensing**: Manual freely available; coach training via MSU.

## EarlyBird and EarlyBird Plus
- **Origin**: National Autistic Society (UK, 1997). EarlyBird for newly diagnosed under-5s; EarlyBird Plus for 4–8s.
- **Mechanism**: 8 group sessions + 3 home visits. Combines didactic education on autism with practical strategy training. Video review of parent-child interaction.
- **Dose**: ~3 months total.
- **Evidence**: [dawson-squibb2019-earlybird-scoping] — scoping review, 18 articles, 16 UK + 2 NZ, **no RCTs**. Parent knowledge and coping reported gains via self-report; child outcomes not robustly assessed.
- **Effect sizes**: not formally synthesized; low-level evidence.
- **Licensing**: NAS-licensed; trainers certified through NAS UK. 27,000+ families served across 14 countries.

# What they share
- Therapist (SLT, psychologist, early interventionist) coaches parent.
- Video review of parent-child interaction is core to PACT, ImPACT, ESDM-parent-coaching, often HMTW.
- Targets parent behavior (responsivity, synchrony, language modeling) as proximal mechanism for child change.
- Lower per-child cost than therapist-direct delivery.
- Higher cultural fit for LATAM family-centered context.

# Evidence summary

## Direction (across the family)
- **Reliable**: parent behavior change (responsivity, synchrony, strategy use). Effect sizes Δ=0.5–1.2 across PACT, HMTW, ImPACT.
- **Contingent**: child outcomes (initiations, expressive language, joint attention). Stronger when:
  - Coach is involved ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]).
  - Child has appropriate baseline profile ([carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct] object-interest moderation).
  - Long-term follow-up is captured ([pickles2016-pact-followup]).
- **Limited**: autism symptom severity. PACT 6-year follow-up is the only parent-mediated trial showing reliable sustained symptom benefit.

## Headline effects
- PACT parent synchrony d=1.22, child initiations d=0.41 ([green2010-pact-rct]).
- PACT 6-year ADOS-CSS sustained benefit ([pickles2016-pact-followup]).
- HMTW parent responsivity Δ=0.71 (5 mo) ([carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct]).
- ImPACT therapist-coached telehealth: indirect effect on expressive language ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]).
- JASPER joint attention + play gains ([kasari2006-jasper-rct]).
- EarlyBird: parent knowledge / coping (self-report); no RCT.

# Controversies
- **Self-directed (no-coach) delivery does not have evidence support** ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]). Pure-software autonomous parent training is not empirically supported.
- **Parent-burden ethical question**: PMIs shift therapeutic labor onto families, predominantly mothers. Critics argue this is invisible workload that intervention research understates.
- **Cultural validity**: most PMI evidence is from US/UK middle-class families. Cross-cultural adaptation studies are limited (some emerging in MX, BR).
- **PMI vs. therapist-direct comparison**: head-to-head trials are scarce. PMIs are usually compared to TAU, not to high-intensity therapist-direct intervention.
- **Hanen MTW null primary outcome** in [carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct] is rarely cited in Hanen marketing — illustrative of practice/evidence asymmetry.

# Cost
- **US**: PACT not commercially licensed; HMTW USD $400–$1,500/family; JASPER variable; ImPACT free manual + coach cost; EarlyBird ~USD $200–$500/family.
- **EU**: Public-system delivery in UK (some areas via NHS), Spain (some autonomous communities), Italy.
- **LATAM**: HMTW most-available via Hanen-certified SLTs (MX, AR, CL, BR, CO). Approximate cost USD $300–$1,000 per family. PACT, JASPER, ImPACT delivery scarce.
- **Peru**: Hanen-certified SLTs limited; CPAL has historical SLT presence; private practitioners variable. PACT / JASPER / ImPACT essentially absent in formal delivery. See [defensoria2023-peru-autism-barriers].

# Availability in Peru / LATAM
- **HMTW** has the strongest LATAM presence via Hanen Centre's international network.
- **PACT** has minimal LATAM presence; UK-Manchester-trained therapists very few outside UK.
- **JASPER** has some LATAM training reach via Kasari lab outreach.
- **ImPACT** has free manual access; coach training via MSU available in English; Spanish-language adaptation work in progress.
- **EarlyBird** licensed to a small number of LATAM organizations; Peru not currently among them.
- **Telehealth** delivery is structurally compatible with LATAM internet access in capital cities; rural/poor connectivity remains a barrier.

# Tech opportunities
- **Coach-in-the-loop telehealth platform** is the empirically supported design pattern. Self-directed parent training is not.
- **Video review tooling** is the highest-leverage component shared across PACT, ImPACT, ESDM-parent-coaching:
  - Parent uploads short (2–5 min) parent-child interaction clip.
  - Coach reviews asynchronously, leaves time-coded annotations.
  - Optional AI-assisted coding (joint attention, parent responsivity, child initiations) to reduce coach workload.
- **Strategy library + prompts**: ImPACT's 6 strategy domains, Hanen's "OWL" (Observe, Wait, Listen), PACT's synchrony cues — software can deliver as JIT prompts during planned routines.
- **Curriculum + goal tracking**: parent-facing what-to-work-on-this-week + child-progress dashboard.
- **Group format support**: HMTW and EarlyBird are group programmes; software can manage group cohort scheduling, between-session homework, peer-parent connection.
- **Adverse event tracking**: parent-reported child distress, dropouts, frustration. Differentiator given the field-wide vacuum ([sandbank2023-project-aim-update]).
- **LATAM-specific adaptation**: Spanish-first, photographic + ARASAAC pictogram support, LATAM family-routine examples, multi-caregiver (often grandmothers, aunts) coordination.
- **Honest scope claim**: "supports parent strategies that have evidence for changing parent-child interaction; child outcomes contingent on multiple factors." Not "treats autism."

# Open questions
- LATAM RCT of any major PMI — outstanding for all major programmes.
- Self-directed (asynchronous) PMI with AI-assisted feedback vs synchronous coach — the central design question for software.
- Optimal coach-load: how often must a human review uploads to retain mediated effect?
- Long-term (5+ year) follow-up of HMTW, ImPACT, JASPER — only PACT has this data.
- Parent burden quantification — minutes per day, mental load, sustainability over months.
- Group-format vs. individual-format PMI cost-effectiveness comparison.

# Builder takeaways
- **PMIs are the LATAM-correct intervention model.** Therapist-direct ESDM at $40k/year is structurally infeasible for most families; coached PMI at $500–$3,000 per programme is.
- **Coach-in-the-loop is non-negotiable for evidence support.** [ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth] is the explicit warning. Pure-software self-directed delivery has no RCT support.
- **Video review is the universal high-leverage primitive** across PACT, ImPACT, ESDM, JASPER, HMTW. Build it once, deploy across multiple programme manuals.
- **Asynchronous coach load** (review uploaded videos in coach's time) is the unit economic that makes software-augmented PMI scalable. Synchronous-only delivery hits the same therapist-scarcity wall as in-person.
- **Honest claim ceiling**: parent strategies, parent-child interaction quality, child initiations, expressive language. Not autism severity (PACT is the only programme with that data).
- **Hanen path of least resistance**: Hanen has the largest existing LATAM SLT network. Building an HMTW-augmenting tool (group cohort management, between-session homework, video review) leverages an existing distribution channel.
- **EarlyBird is an evidence-implementation gap**: 27,000 families served, no RCT. A digitized, Spanish-first, evaluation-instrumented EarlyBird-style programme is a credible academic-partnership pitch (and an obvious wedge for Peru / LATAM where EarlyBird is absent).
- **Adverse event tracking** is a category-wide vacuum and a differentiator.
- **Group + individual hybrid** matches HMTW and EarlyBird structure; software lowers cohort management cost.
