# Summary
TEACCH (Treatment and Education of Autistic and related Communication-handicapped CHildren) is a structured-teaching framework developed at UNC Chapel Hill (Schopler, 1972). Its signature components — **physical structure, visual schedules, work systems, and task organization** — are the most directly software-replicable of any major autism intervention. The 2025 meta-analysis ([shi2025-teacch-meta], 11 studies, n=701) reports significant effects on socialization (MD=0.6, GRADE high), fine motor (MD=0.7, GRADE high), cognition (MD=1.34, GRADE moderate), and autism severity on the ABC (MD=−1.57, GRADE moderate). Communication, daily living, and gross motor outcomes were NS. Project AIM 2020 ([sandbank2020-project-aim]) found TEACCH NS for social communication (g=−0.11). Heterogeneity across what counts as "TEACCH" (full classroom programme vs. structured-teaching components inserted into other settings) is the dominant interpretive issue.

# What it is
- TEACCH is a **whole-life programme** (originally birth–adulthood) emphasizing the "Culture of Autism" — autistic people's strengths in visual processing, attention to detail, and preference for predictability — and structuring the environment to leverage them rather than remediate them.
- Core components:
  - **Physical structure**: clearly demarcated work, play, and transition zones.
  - **Visual schedules**: top-to-bottom or left-to-right sequence of upcoming activities.
  - **Work systems**: "what / how much / when finished / what next" answered visually.
  - **Task organization**: visual cues for sequence and completion within each task.
  - **Routines**: predictable patterns reduce cognitive load.

# Origin
- Eric Schopler, UNC Chapel Hill, 1972 — first US state-funded statewide autism programme.
- Statewide North Carolina implementation across schools, vocational settings, group homes.
- Distinguished from ABA by its **strength-based / accommodation framing** rather than behavior-modification framing.

# Target population
- Originally school-age and adult ASD with intellectual disability.
- Now applied across the spectrum, ages 2–adult, including high-cognition profiles for executive-function support.
- Particularly suited to learners whose visual processing exceeds verbal processing — common in autism.

# Delivery model + intensity
- Embedded in the daily classroom or home environment — not a discrete weekly therapy.
- Practitioner training: TEACCH 5-day certificate (UNC TEACCH Autism Programme). Implementation does not require BCBA supervision.
- Intensity: continuous environmental restructuring + individualized tasks 30–180 minutes per session within structured-teaching blocks.

# Evidence summary

## Headline effects
- [shi2025-teacch-meta] (2025 BMC Pediatrics, 11 studies, n=701):
  - Socialization MD=0.6 (95% CI 0.2–1.0), GRADE high.
  - Fine motor MD=0.7 (0.4–1.0), GRADE high.
  - Cognitive Performance Scale MD=1.34 (0.09–2.58), GRADE moderate.
  - Autism Behavior Checklist MD=−1.57 (−2.11 to −1.02), GRADE moderate.
  - CARS MD=−0.7 (−1.0 to −0.3).
  - Communication, daily living, gross motor: NS, GRADE low.
- [sandbank2020-project-aim] / [sandbank2023-project-aim-update]: TEACCH category in 2020 review showed g=−0.11 for social communication (NS). Quality-adjusted effects do not retain significance.
- [hume2021-ebp-third-gen]: Visual supports (a TEACCH cornerstone) is confirmed as an EBP at the focused-practice level.

## Direction
- Strongest signals on fine motor, social skills, autism severity (ABC, CARS).
- Inconsistent or null on communication and daily living — software augmentation should not over-claim these.
- Heterogeneity issue: many included studies are Chinese-language clinical trials of "TEACCH" that may differ substantially from UNC fidelity.

# Controversies
- **What counts as TEACCH?** Many studies test "structured teaching" components (visual schedules, work systems) without the full TEACCH cultural / lifespan framework. Critics argue category dilution; defenders argue components are the active ingredient.
- **Comparison with ABA**: older head-to-head trials (small, methodologically weak) generally show comparable outcomes; the philosophical difference (accommodate vs. modify) is larger than the outcome difference.
- **Project AIM null** (2020) for TEACCH social communication contrasts with Shi 2025's positive findings — most likely reflects different included-study sets and inclusion criteria.
- **Adoption vs. evidence asymmetry**: TEACCH is one of the most widely adopted classroom frameworks worldwide, with an evidence base smaller than its footprint.

# Cost
- **US**: TEACCH 5-day certificate ~$1,500. School-based implementation cost is integrated into standard SPED budgets — incremental cost low compared to 1:1 ABA.
- **EU**: Variable; UK and Spain have regional TEACCH-trained networks. Spain's "aulas TEA" model in some autonomous communities draws on TEACCH structure.
- **LATAM**: Some private special-education schools in MX, BR, AR, CL, CO advertise TEACCH. UNC offers occasional Spanish-language training; certified trainers are scarce.
- Per-child marginal cost in a TEACCH-structured classroom: USD $5,000–$20,000/year depending on classroom ratios.

# Availability in Peru / LATAM
- **Peru**: Some private special-education centers in Lima reference TEACCH (CASP, CPAL-affiliated, Ann Sullivan Center). No public-system implementation. Certified TEACCH trainers in Peru: extremely few or none currently.
- **LATAM more broadly**: Brazil (USP-affiliated programmes) and Argentina have the strongest TEACCH adoption. Mexico's INSADI and similar private centers use it.
- The structure is publicly documented and freely implementable — no licensing barrier — which makes scope for public-system or NGO adoption.

# Tech opportunities
- **Visual schedules** (TEACCH's most software-replicable component): drag-and-drop scheduler, photo + symbol scheduler, time-display, "first/then" template, transition warnings. Existing apps (Choiceworks, First Then Visual Schedule, Visules) are dated; a Spanish-first redesign with ARASAAC integration is an obvious wedge. See [aac-symbol-systems].
- **Work systems**: digital "what / how much / when finished / what next" templates that families and teachers can populate, with completion tracking → caregiver dashboard.
- **Task organization**: AI-generated step-by-step visual sequences for any task (brushing teeth, getting dressed, lunch routine) — converts a household routine into a TEACCH-style task organizer in seconds.
- **Routines + transition support**: timer overlays, transition warnings, predictable-routine generators.
- **Caregiver coaching**: short videos of a TEACCH-trained practitioner setting up structures in a typical home environment.
- **Augment, do not replace**: TEACCH is environmental — the software is a planning, generation, and tracking layer over physical-world structures.
- **Limitations**: communication and daily-living outcomes were NS in Shi 2025; the product should not over-claim these. The defensible claims are around schedule comprehension, transition tolerance, fine-motor task support, and reduced parent/teacher cognitive load.

# Evidence
- [shi2025-teacch-meta] — 11-study meta: positive on social, fine motor, cognition, ABC; null on communication, daily living, gross motor.
- [sandbank2020-project-aim] — TEACCH g=−0.11 social communication, NS.
- [sandbank2023-project-aim-update] — TEACCH not retained as significant under quality-adjusted analysis.
- [hume2021-ebp-third-gen] — Visual supports confirmed EBP at component level.

# Open questions
- Component-decomposition trial: which TEACCH components (visual schedule alone vs. full work system vs. full programme) carry the effect?
- Long-term outcomes (5+ years) of TEACCH-structured education on adult independent-living outcomes — surprisingly little data given TEACCH is 50+ years old.
- Cultural adaptation in LATAM contexts: do family-routine and educational-system structures interact with TEACCH's middle-class US-classroom assumptions?
- Software-delivered visual schedule vs. paper visual schedule: equivalence not established.
- Effectiveness with high-cognition / verbally-fluent autistic children where executive function (not language) is the deficit.

# Builder takeaways
- **TEACCH is the easiest evidence-anchored intervention to build software for**. Visual schedules, work systems, task organization are textbook UI patterns.
- **Spanish-first visual scheduler with ARASAAC integration** is an obvious LATAM wedge. Existing options (Choiceworks, First Then Visual Schedule) are English-first, dated, and US-priced.
- **Defensible claims**: schedule comprehension, transition support, predictability. Do not claim communication or daily-living gains — Shi 2025 found them NS.
- **AI task-organization generator**: convert any natural-language routine into a step-by-step visual task organizer with TEACCH-style "what / how much / when finished / what next" structure. New capability that paper TEACCH cannot deliver.
- **Caregiver / teacher dashboard**: schedule completion tracking, transition success rates, behavior-during-transitions logging. The data layer is what differentiates a software product from PDF schedules.
- **Public-system path**: TEACCH has no licensing barrier and is widely accepted by educators. Pitching to MINEDU (Peru), SEP (Mexico) special-education divisions is plausible — TEACCH branding has higher institutional acceptance than ABA branding in education contexts.
- **Augment, do not replace**: keep the physical environment central. The software's role is planning, generation, tracking — not substitution for the physical structures.
