# Summary
Relationship Development Intervention (RDI; Steven Gutstein, 2000s) is a parent-led, video-coached programme targeting "experience sharing," dynamic intelligence, and flexible thinking. The evidence base is **essentially a single retrospective uncontrolled study by the developer** ([gutstein2007-rdi-pilot], n=16, no control, severe COI). All 16 children met autism diagnostic criteria pre-treatment; none met criteria at follow-up per the developer's own re-rating — a finding that has not been independently replicated in 20 years. RDI is **not listed as an evidence-based practice** by NCAEP/Hume 2021, Project AIM, or Cochrane, and should not be a target intervention for product positioning despite its commercial dissemination via the RDIconnect network. Acknowledge as a parental-acceptability/relationship-focused option for parents who request it; do not anchor a product on it.

# What it is
- **RDI** = Relationship Development Intervention, a parent-led programme targeting six "core deficits" Gutstein identifies in autism: emotional referencing, social coordination, declarative language, flexible thinking, relational information processing, and foresight/hindsight.
- Delivered through structured parent-child activities ("Objectives") with weekly video review by a Certified RDI Consultant.
- Conceptually adjacent to DIR/Floortime ([dir-floortime]) and PACT ([parent-mediated-interventions]) — all relationship-development models — but with weaker evidence than either.

# Origin
- Steven Gutstein and Rachelle Sheely, Houston TX, late 1990s / early 2000s.
- Foundational text: *Relationship Development Intervention with Young Children* (2002).
- Disseminated through RDIconnect — a private-sector consultant certification + family-subscription model.

# Target population
- Children and adolescents with ASD across the spectrum.
- Marketed across age ranges, no clear age-restriction in protocol.

# Delivery model + intensity
- Parent-delivered through structured "Objective" activities (predictable, varied-with-novelty, scaffolded).
- Weekly video upload to Certified RDI Consultant; consultant reviews and assigns next Objective.
- Recommended dose: variable — typically 1+ hour/day of dedicated RDI activities + integration into daily routines.

# Evidence summary

## Headline effects
- [gutstein2007-rdi-pilot] (n=16, retrospective, uncontrolled, single clinic, developer-authored):
  - 16/16 met autism criteria pre-treatment; 0/16 met criteria at follow-up (per authors' own re-rating).
  - Improvements in ADOS-rated flexibility.
  - More children moved to less restrictive school placements.
  - **No control group, no blinded assessment, severe COI, no independent replication**.
- No subsequent RCT or controlled trial of RDI in 20 years since this publication.
- Not included as an EBP in [hume2021-ebp-third-gen] (NCAEP).
- Not separately analyzed in [sandbank2020-project-aim] / [sandbank2023-project-aim-update].
- No Cochrane review of RDI.

## Direction
- The single existing study is positive — but the design (retrospective, uncontrolled, COI-conflicted, parent-self-selected sample) does not support causal inference.
- The 16/16 → 0/16 diagnostic-loss finding is implausibly large by comparison to all other autism intervention literature; its absence of independent replication in 20 years is itself evidence against the magnitude.

# Controversies
- **Severe COI**: Gutstein is the developer, founder of RDIconnect, and primary commercial beneficiary. He authored the only existing study.
- **No independent replication** in 20 years despite global commercial dissemination.
- **Confirmation bias in measurement**: ADOS scoring at follow-up by same/related raters; no blinded assessment.
- **Diagnostic instability framing**: the "no longer met criteria" finding may reflect diagnostic measurement properties (especially in higher-functioning children) rather than intervention effect. ESDM ([dawson2010-esdm-rct]) and other models show similar but smaller diagnostic-shift findings under controlled conditions.
- **Marketing vs. evidence asymmetry**: RDI is sold as a comprehensive autism treatment despite an evidence base that would not pass any standard EBP criterion.
- **Compared with DIR/Floortime** ([dir-floortime]): RDI shares many principles but has even thinner evidence — DIR has at least a 12-study systematic review.

# Cost
- **US**: RDIconnect family programme USD $200–$500/month for consultant access + materials. Total programme cost USD $3,000–$15,000 over typical 1–3 year duration.
- **EU / LATAM**: Certified RDI Consultants exist in MX, BR, AR, Spain, UK, Australia. Pricing similar.
- **Peru**: No identified active RDIconnect-certified consultants. Some private practitioners reference RDI loosely.

# Availability in Peru / LATAM
- Limited but present. RDIconnect has Spanish-language materials and a small certified-consultant network in MX, BR, AR.
- Peru: minimal formal presence.
- Family demand in LATAM is driven by (a) anti-ABA sentiment, (b) relationship/family-centered framing, (c) parent-led delivery model.

# Tech opportunities
- **Do not anchor a product on RDI specifically**. The evidence base does not support clinical claims and would be a reputational risk.
- The video-upload / consultant-review delivery model is software-tractable — but the same model with stronger evidence is **PACT**, **Project ImPACT**, and **P-ESDM**. Build on those instead.
- Parent-coaching infrastructure (video upload, consultant review queue, Objective-style activity library) is generic — useful for any relationship-development framing, not RDI-specific.
- A neurodiversity-aligned, relationship-focused product can borrow philosophical positioning from RDI without adopting the brand or specific protocol — and ground itself in the broader evidence-based parent-mediated NDBI literature.

# Evidence
- [gutstein2007-rdi-pilot] — Single retrospective uncontrolled study; developer-authored; no replication.
- [hume2021-ebp-third-gen] — RDI **not** listed as an EBP.
- [sandbank2020-project-aim] / [sandbank2023-project-aim-update] — RDI **not** separately retained as evidence-supported.

# Open questions
- Why has there been no independent RCT of RDI in 20 years despite continued commercial dissemination?
- Are RDI's positive parent-acceptability and reported family experiences attributable to the framework specifically or to the parent-coaching delivery model generally?
- How much of RDIconnect's claimed outcomes are diagnostic-instability artifacts vs. real change?

# Builder takeaways
- **RDI is not a viable evidence-anchor** for a wellness/education product. Recommending it to families honestly requires acknowledging the evidence gap.
- **Use RDI as a community-recognition input** — parents know the term, and a respectful framing ("relationship-focused approach with limited formal evidence; consider parent-mediated NDBI alternatives like PACT or Project ImPACT") preserves credibility.
- **Build the same delivery model with stronger evidence**: parent-led activities + video review + coach is exactly PACT and Project ImPACT. Both have RCT evidence; RDI does not.
- **No commercial license obstacle**: RDIconnect protects its certification but not the general framework. A relationship-development-style product can operate legally without RDI branding.
- **Avoid borrowing RDI's outcome claims**. The 16/16 → 0/16 diagnostic-loss figure is not defensible and using it would be dishonest.
- **Honest framing**: list RDI in any "interventions glossary" content, mark its evidence level explicitly low, link to better-evidenced relationship-focused alternatives.
