# Summary
Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Interventions (NDBIs) are a hybrid framework — explicitly named in 2015 (Schreibman, Dawson, Stahmer et al.) — combining **applied behavior analysis principles** (operant learning, prompting, reinforcement, mastery criteria) with **developmental science** (joint attention, social motivation, child-led interaction, naturalistic context). Major NDBIs: **ESDM, JASPER, PRT (Pivotal Response Treatment), Project ImPACT, Reciprocal Imitation Training, Enhanced Milieu Teaching (EMT), Incidental Teaching**. The 2024 NDBI overview ([song2024-ndbi-overview]) synthesizes 5 prior meta-analyses and confirms NDBI as the most-evidence-supported framework for early autism intervention. Project AIM 2023 ([sandbank2023-project-aim-update]) — the most authoritative cross-intervention analysis — finds **NDBI is the only intervention class that retains quality-adjusted significant effects across multiple outcomes** (adaptive behavior g=0.23, autism characteristics g=0.38, language g=0.16, play g=0.19, social communication g=0.35). For under-8 autism intervention, NDBI is the empirical front-runner. Parent-mediated delivery is the structurally most-LATAM-realistic implementation (see [parent-mediated-interventions]).

# What it is
- **NDBI** = Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Interventions. The term was formalized by Schreibman et al. (2015, JADD) to identify a class of interventions that share:
  1. Implementation in natural environments (home, classroom, community).
  2. Child-led shared engagement (follow the child's interest).
  3. Use of natural reinforcers (the activity/object the child is interested in).
  4. Direct teaching of developmentally appropriate skills.
  5. Use of behavioral strategies (prompting, reinforcement, shaping) within the developmental frame.
- **Distinguishes from classical Lovaas-style EIBI/DTT** by naturalistic context and child-led delivery.
- **Distinguishes from purely developmental approaches** (DIR, RDI) by retaining behavioral mechanism.
- The hybrid is the active ingredient — developmental framing maintains motivation and ecological validity; behavioral mechanism maintains learning rigor.

# Programmes in the NDBI umbrella

## Tier 1 (strongest evidence)

- **ESDM (Early Start Denver Model)**: see dedicated topic [esdm]. Toddlers 12–48 months; 15–25 hr/week therapist + parent. Foundational RCT [dawson2010-esdm-rct]; meta [fuller2020-esdm-meta] g=0.36 overall.
- **PRT (Pivotal Response Treatment)**: Koegel lab, UCSB. Targets "pivotal" behaviors (motivation, child choice, response to multiple cues). Umbrella review [uljarevic2022-prt-umbrella] — receptive language SMD=0.51, expressive 0.31; null on adaptive and autism severity.
- **JASPER (Joint Attention, Symbolic Play, Engagement and Regulation)**: Kasari lab, UCLA. Joint-attention and play-focused. [kasari2006-jasper-rct], [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd], [goods2013-jasper-pilot]. Strong evidence on proximal targets.

## Tier 2 (parent-mediated NDBIs)

- **Project ImPACT**: Ingersoll, MSU. Parent-mediated NDBI for community use. [ingersoll2013-impact-pilot], [ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth] — coach-required for mediated effect.
- **PACT (Preschool Autism Communication Trial)**: SLT-coached parent intervention. [green2010-pact-rct], [pickles2016-pact-followup]. PACT-specific framing emphasizes "developmental social-pragmatic" lineage but functions as parent-mediated NDBI.
- **Hanen More Than Words**: parent-mediated SLT-led group. [carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct]. Less explicit NDBI labeling but operationally NDBI-aligned.

## Tier 3 (focused-practice NDBIs)

- **Reciprocal Imitation Training (RIT)**: Ingersoll. Targets imitation as foundational social skill.
- **Incidental Teaching**: Hart & Risley origins; embedded teaching opportunities in daily routines.
- **Enhanced Milieu Teaching (EMT)**: Kaiser. Often combined with JASPER (JASP+EMT in [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd]).

# Origin and target population
- Schreibman et al. 2015 paper formalized the NDBI label; constituent programmes had existed for years/decades.
- Target population: children under 8 with ASD or high autism risk; strongest evidence in 12 mo – 5 yr range.
- Increasing extension to older children and to early-screening high-risk infants.

# Delivery model + intensity
- Therapist-direct (ESDM, JASPER, PRT) typically 5–25 hr/week.
- Parent-mediated (PACT, ImPACT, HMTW, P-ESDM) typically 1–3 hr/week therapist coaching + parent-delivered daily.
- Classroom-embedded (G-ESDM, JASPER classroom variant) — emerging delivery model.
- Telehealth delivery: empirically supported for parent-mediated variants ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]).

# Evidence summary

## Headline effects
- [sandbank2023-project-aim-update] (252 studies, n=13,304): NDBI retained quality-adjusted significant effects on:
  - Adaptive behavior g=0.23
  - Autism characteristics g=0.38
  - Language g=0.16
  - Play g=0.19
  - Social communication g=0.35
  - **Only intervention class to retain significance after restriction to RCTs without high detection-bias or caregiver-report inflation.**
- [song2024-ndbi-overview] (5 prior meta-analyses): NDBI consistently positive on language, communication, cognition; mixed on autism symptoms and stereotypies.
- [sandbank2020-project-aim] (130 samples, n=6,240): NDBI g=−0.01 to 0.35; significant on cognition, language, play, social communication after quality adjustment.
- [hume2021-ebp-third-gen]: Naturalistic intervention confirmed EBP; multiple NDBI components (modeling, prompting, reinforcement, joint-attention intervention) confirmed at component level.
- Programme-specific:
  - ESDM g=0.36 overall ([fuller2020-esdm-meta]).
  - PRT receptive language SMD=0.51 ([uljarevic2022-prt-umbrella]).
  - JASPER proximal-target gains ([kasari2006-jasper-rct]).
  - PACT parent synchrony d=1.22 + 6-year sustained symptom benefit ([pickles2016-pact-followup]).

## Direction
- **Reliable**: language, social communication, play, joint attention. Effects modest (g=0.16–0.41) but quality-adjusted.
- **Less reliable**: autism symptom severity (mixed across NDBI subtypes; PACT shows long-term benefit, ESDM does not at meta-level).
- **Null**: stereotypies, repetitive behaviors.
- **Adverse events**: under-monitored across the field ([sandbank2023-project-aim-update] "poorly monitored, but possibly common").

# Controversies
- **Hybrid framing**: NDBI as a category resists clean definition — exactly which interventions count is contested. Schreibman 2015's criteria are the operational definition.
- **Component decomposition**: which NDBI components (developmental scaffolding, child-led delivery, behavioral mechanism, naturalistic context) carry the effect? Not empirically resolved.
- **NDBI vs. classical ABA**: NDBI proponents argue NDBI is more humane and ecologically valid; ABA traditionalists argue NDBI is "ABA in naturalistic clothing." Operationally both true.
- **Effect-size attenuation in larger replications**: foundational trials (e.g., Dawson 2010) show larger effects than later multi-site replications — typical first-trial pattern.
- **Adverse events vacuum** is the most concerning class-wide finding ([sandbank2023-project-aim-update]).

# Cost
- **Therapist-direct NDBI**: USD $30,000–$70,000/year (US); ESDM and JASPER intensive delivery.
- **Parent-mediated NDBI**: USD $1,000–$5,000 programme cost (US); USD $300–$2,000 LATAM private.
- **Classroom-embedded** (G-ESDM, JASPER classroom): integrated into early-childhood education budgets where available.
- **Peru / LATAM**: parent-mediated delivery is structurally accessible; therapist-direct NDBI scarce. See [defensoria2023-peru-autism-barriers].

# Availability in Peru / LATAM
- **Therapist-direct NDBI**: certified ESDM / JASPER / PRT therapists rare. A handful of centers in MX, BR, AR, CL; minimal presence in PE, EC, BO.
- **Parent-mediated NDBI**: Hanen MTW most-available via certified-SLT network. ImPACT manual freely available; coach training increasingly accessible. PACT minimal LATAM presence.
- **Telehealth delivery** structurally compatible with LATAM internet infrastructure in capital cities.

# Tech opportunities

## High-leverage software wedges

- **Parent coaching platform** for NDBI delivery (covered in detail in [parent-mediated-interventions]):
  - Video upload + asynchronous coach review.
  - Strategy library with situation-specific JIT prompts.
  - Curriculum + goal tracking.
  - Coach dashboard with case-load management.
- **Curriculum tracking software** for therapist-direct NDBI: ESDM has structured curriculum across 7 domains; JASPER has joint-attention/play target hierarchies; PRT has motivation/multiple-cue targets. All currently paper-based.
- **Video-coding / parent-child interaction analysis**: AI-assisted coding of joint attention, child initiations, parent responsivity. Reduces coach workload. Same primitive applies across PACT, ImPACT, ESDM, JASPER.
- **Routine-integrated prompts**: NDBI is delivered through everyday routines (feeding, dressing, play, transitions). App reminders + brief in-routine prompts at planned daily inflection points.
- **Adverse event tracking** as a first-class data type: parent-reported child distress, dropouts, frustration. The class-wide vacuum is a defensible differentiator.
- **Generalization probes**: NDBI emphasizes generalization across people, settings, and materials. Software-tracked probes (parent records same target with different person/setting) operationalize this.

## Limitations

- **Self-directed (no coach) parent NDBI lacks evidence support** ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]). Build for coach-in-the-loop, not coach-replacement.
- **Effect ceiling**: NDBI g=0.16–0.41 is the realistic effect-size magnitude. Software augmentation should not over-claim.
- **Replication / fidelity barrier**: "NDBI-inspired" software without certified-therapist supervision cannot legally claim to deliver ESDM, JASPER, or PRT. Generic NDBI-aligned design is the safer framing.

## Honest scope

- Skill acquisition (language, joint attention, play, communication) — supported.
- Autism symptom reduction — variable; do not over-claim.
- Stereotypies / repetitive behaviors — not supported.
- Long-term outcomes — only PACT has 6-year data; others mostly 1-year follow-up.

# Open questions
- Component-decomposition: which NDBI ingredients (child-led, naturalistic context, behavioral mechanism) carry the effect?
- Minimum effective dose for parent-mediated NDBI variants.
- Long-term (5+ year) outcomes across NDBI subtypes — only PACT has this data.
- LATAM-context efficacy and implementation studies — essentially absent.
- Software-augmented NDBI vs. standard delivery — minimal RCT evidence as of 2026 ([sandbank2020-project-aim] technology-based g=0.05–0.42 NS in RCTs).
- Adverse event characterization across NDBI delivery models.
- Optimal coach-load: how often must a human coach review parent uploads to retain mediated effect?

# Builder takeaways
- **NDBI is the highest-evidence anchor for early autism software products.** Project AIM's quality-adjusted analysis is the strongest case in the literature.
- **Parent-mediated NDBI delivery is the LATAM-correct implementation** — see [parent-mediated-interventions] for the canonical product analysis.
- **Coach-in-the-loop is non-negotiable**. Self-directed software lacks evidence support.
- **Component-based design** lets a single platform serve multiple NDBI manuals (PACT, ImPACT, P-ESDM) — video review, strategy library, curriculum tracking, generalization probes are shared primitives.
- **Honest claim ceiling**: language, social communication, play, adaptive behavior — modest effect sizes (g=0.16–0.41). Not autism symptom reduction.
- **Adverse event tracking** is a category-wide vacuum and a credible differentiator.
- **Avoid programme-name claims** without certification/licensing. "NDBI-aligned" or "based on NDBI principles" is defensible; "delivers ESDM" without UC Davis certification is not.
- **Bridging the technology-effect gap** is the hard product question: Project AIM's technology-based interventions showed g=0.05–0.42 NS in RCTs. New designs (coach-augmented, video-review-native, AI-assisted coding) need to clear this bar with proper evaluation.
- **Public-system / NGO partnerships** are a long-cycle but plausible distribution channel — NDBI's ecological validity and parent-mediated delivery align with public health framing better than ABA branding.
