# Summary
High-tech AAC (tablet apps + dedicated speech-generating devices) is an established evidence-based practice for teaching social-communication to autistic and intellectually-disabled users ([morin2018-hightech-aac], [ganz2012-aac-meta], [hume2021-ebp-third-gen]), but **head-to-head trials show no consistent superiority over low-tech PECS or paper boards** ([gilroy2018-pecs-vs-sgd-rct], [boesch2013-pecs-sgd], [collette2019-proloquo2go-classroom], [morin2018-hightech-aac]). Modality fit is moderated by cognitive profile: SGD-style apps win for ASD-without-IDD, PECS-style exchange wins for ASD+IDD ([ganz2014-aac-participant-interaction]). The dominant clinical problem is not efficacy but **abandonment** — pre-iPad SLP survey put ~60% of AAC systems out of use within one year ([johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment]); broader AT data put baseline abandonment at ~29% ([phillips1993-at-abandonment]); contemporary qualitative work cites 30–50% as the working figure ([yau2024-aac-barriers]). Predictors are stakeholder support, training, system fit, parent emotional readiness, and willingness to maintain/adjust the system over time ([johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment], [moorcroft2019-parent-aac-rejection], [moorcroft2019-slp-acceptance]). Bilingual / multilingual evidence is conceptual rather than empirical: best-practice consensus is to support all the user's home languages ([soto2014-bilingual-aac], [tonsing2020-multilingual-aac-ideology]) but **no controlled trials exist for Spanish-English, Spanish-Portuguese, or Spanish-Quechua AAC**. The "AAC delays speech" myth is the single biggest abandonment driver ([yau2024-aac-barriers]) and the meta-analytic evidence rebuts it cleanly ([millar2006-aac-speech], [schlosser2008-aac-autism-speech]).

# High-tech vs low-tech: what evidence actually says

- [morin2018-hightech-aac] — High-tech AAC meets WWC EBP criteria for teaching social-communication skills in ASD/ID; comparison sub-analysis found *no significant superiority* of high-tech over low-tech AAC.
- [ganz2012-aac-meta] — Across 24 single-case studies, PECS and SGDs both produced large communication-outcome effects; no head-to-head winner.
- [gilroy2018-pecs-vs-sgd-rct] — Pilot RCT n=35, Ireland: a *free open-source* iPad SGD performed equivalently to PECS over 4 months in community classrooms.
- [boesch2013-pecs-sgd] — n=3 alternating-treatment: independent requesting easier under PECS than ProxTalker SGD; no speech gains in either condition.
- [collette2019-proloquo2go-classroom] — n=4 within-subject: [proloquo2go](../../business/research/competitors/proloquo2go.md) on iPad reduced needed adult support vs no-tech but was equivalent to PECS or SMARTBoard.
- [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd] — SMART trial n=61 minimally-verbal: adding a [proloquo2go](../../business/research/competitors/proloquo2go.md) SGD at the *start* of JASP+EMT outperformed JASP+EMT alone on spontaneous communicative utterances. Not a tech-vs-tech comparison; supports early SGD introduction within naturalistic developmental intervention.

**Net.** Treat high-tech AAC as "as good as" PECS, not "better than." The competitive position rests on cost, language coverage, portability, customization, and family fit — not raw efficacy. Confidence: **high** for the no-superiority finding; **medium** for the SMART-trial early-SGD signal (single RCT, n=61).

# Modality fit by user profile

- [ganz2014-aac-participant-interaction] — ASD without comorbid IDD: SGDs most effective. ASD with comorbid IDD: PECS most effective. Preschool: both equally high-yield. Older children: no modality difference. Caveat: small-sample meta-analysis, needs replication.
- [boesch2013-pecs-sgd] — Severe-presentation children acquired requesting more easily under PECS's physical-exchange action than under tap-to-speak SGD. Suggests the *exchange gesture* may be a separable active ingredient — see [pecs-evidence](pecs-evidence.md).

Confidence: **medium** (one meta-analysis + one n=3 case series).

# Core vs fringe vocabulary

- [banajee2003-toddler-core] — 23 function words / verbs / pronouns account for ~96% of typically-developing toddlers' utterances in preschool routines. Almost no nouns in the core. This is the empirical anchor for "core word" AAC pages.
- [laubscher2020-core-vocab-review] — Existing core lists *under-emphasize* common nouns (people, foods, animals) that dominate early expressive vocabulary. Only 1 of 5 reviewed lists used data from children with complex communication needs.
- See [aac-symbol-systems](aac-symbol-systems.md) for symbol-set-level discussion.

**Net.** Core-only boards are misaligned to actual early-symbolic communicators. Best practice is a hybrid: core words for grammar + personally meaningful nouns (family names, favorite foods, pets) from day one, with the noun:function ratio shifting as the user matures. No Spanish-language equivalent of the Banajee 23-word list exists — building one is an open research contribution. Confidence: **medium** (single Banajee study + Light/Laubscher narrative review).

# Abandonment: rates and predictors

| Source | Population | Rate | Window |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [phillips1993-at-abandonment] | Adults, all AT (US, n=227) | 29.3% | lifetime |
| [johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment] | SLP-reported AAC caseload (US, n=275) | ~60% (only 39.35% used >1y) | 1 year |
| [yau2024-aac-barriers] | Stakeholder discussion (AU, n=30) | 30–50% (cited background) | unspecified |

- [phillips1993-at-abandonment] — Predictors of abandonment: lack of user input in selection, easy procurement (no friction = wrong device), poor performance, change in user needs. Two abandonment cliffs: first year, then ~5 years.
- [johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment] — Top success construct: support. Top abandonment construct: failure to maintain/adjust the system. Other modifiable factors: training, attitude, fit.
- [moorcroft2019-parent-aac-rejection] (parents, n=12) — Family abandonment drivers: parent emotional un-readiness; "extraneous work" on already-stretched parents; child not using the system in functional moments; dissatisfaction with interface, vocabulary, voice, durability.
- [moorcroft2019-slp-acceptance] (SLPs, n=16) — Acceptance themes: parent view of child; parent view of AAC; support networks; parent capacity/demands; SLP services; AAC system features. **Novel construct: parent grief and loss as abandonment driver.**
- [yau2024-aac-barriers] — 100% of parents named *user willingness* as a barrier vs. 27–30% of educators/clinicians: parents and professionals systematically disagree about *why* children disengage. The "AAC delays speech" myth persists across stakeholder groups.

**Net.** Pediatric AAC abandonment likely sits 30–50% within the first year and the modifiable levers are (a) family-side support and emotional readiness, (b) per-user system maintenance over time, (c) myth-rebuttal in onboarding. Confidence: **medium** (one US SLP-survey, two Australian qualitative studies, one 1993 cross-AT survey — no contemporary controlled abandonment study).

# Bilingual / multilingual AAC

- [soto2014-bilingual-aac] — Sociocultural framework: child's full linguistic ecology (home, school, community) must shape the AAC system; both/all home languages supported; code-switching allowed.
- [tonsing2020-multilingual-aac-ideology] — Monolingual default in AAC delivery is *ideology*, not just resource constraint. Family preferences are routinely overridden.
- See also [aac-symbol-systems](aac-symbol-systems.md) for the symbol-set language-coverage table; ARASAAC has 30+ languages but Quechua/Aymara coverage is essentially zero.

**Net.** Best-practice is multilingual support from the data layer up. Empirical evidence base for bilingual AAC outcomes is **near-zero RCTs**; everything is conceptual or framework-level. No identified studies on Spanish-Quechua, Spanish-Aymara, or Spanish-Portuguese AAC outcomes. Confidence: **low** (expert opinion only; level-5 evidence).

# Voice output (SGD) vs symbol-only display

Direct head-to-head evidence is sparse:

- [ganz2012-aac-meta], [ganz2014-aac-participant-interaction] — SGDs categorized separately from "other picture-based"; SGDs and PECS both outperform unstructured picture systems. Within structured systems, no consistent SGD advantage.
- [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd] — Adding voice output via [proloquo2go](../../business/research/competitors/proloquo2go.md) at the start of a developmental intervention beats spoken-modeling-alone on spontaneous communicative utterances. The *voice output* is plausibly the active ingredient (auditory model + reinforcement).
- [boesch2013-pecs-sgd] — In severe presentations, voice output did not improve speech outcomes vs silent PECS exchange.

**When voice output likely earns its weight**: minimally-verbal users where adult voice modeling is the intervention; users in inclusion settings where a peer needs to hear the request; users with motor/visual constraints where confirmation feedback matters. **When symbol-only is fine**: early-stage exchange-protocol learners ([pecs-evidence](pecs-evidence.md) phases I–III), users in noisy environments, users who find synthetic voice aversive. Confidence: **medium**.

# Customization burden: who actually programs the boards

The literature is qualitative on this point:

- [moorcroft2019-parent-aac-rejection] — Parents report AAC implementation as "extraneous work" on top of caregiving load; a major theme behind rejection.
- [johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment] — "Failure to maintain/adjust the system" is the #1 SLP-reported abandonment construct. Implies someone needs to keep adding/retiring vocabulary; in practice that someone often does not exist.
- [moorcroft2019-slp-acceptance] — SLPs note parent capacity and support-network depth as decisive — i.e., even when the SLP designs the initial board, ongoing customization devolves to families that may not have the bandwidth.

In the field this means: SLPs build the initial board in the clinic (often using [proloquo2go](../../business/research/competitors/proloquo2go.md) or [td-snap-tobii-dynavox](../../business/research/competitors/td-snap-tobii-dynavox.md) authoring tools); families are expected to maintain it; in practice many do not, and the system stagnates and is abandoned. Open-source tools like [coughdrop](../../business/research/competitors/coughdrop.md), [cboard](../../business/research/competitors/cboard.md), and [asterics-grid](../../business/research/competitors/asterics-grid.md) try to lower the SLP-author barrier; none have published abandonment data showing they solve the maintenance problem.

Confidence: **medium** (qualitative consensus; no quantified field study of who-edits-what).

# Open Board Format (OBF/OBZ) interoperability

- **What it is**: a JSON-based open standard for AAC board content (boards, buttons, images, sounds, navigation) introduced and maintained by the [coughdrop](../../business/research/competitors/coughdrop.md) team via openboardformat.org. `.obf` is a single board; `.obz` is a zip bundle of boards plus media. Specification at openboardformat.org; OpenSymbols and ARASAAC images can be referenced or embedded.
- **What it solves**: portability of *board layouts and vocabulary* across compliant apps. A family can in principle move from one app to another without rebuilding their board. Several open and semi-open AAC tools support OBF/OBZ import/export — [coughdrop](../../business/research/competitors/coughdrop.md), [asterics-grid](../../business/research/competitors/asterics-grid.md), [cboard](../../business/research/competitors/cboard.md).
- **What it does not solve**: (a) **usage history and skill-acquisition data** — no standard schema for "user X uses word Y at frequency Z"; (b) **voice/TTS** — voice quality depends on the destination app; (c) **dominant proprietary apps don't import it** — [proloquo2go](../../business/research/competitors/proloquo2go.md), [touchchat](../../business/research/competitors/touchchat.md), [lamp-words-for-life](../../business/research/competitors/lamp-words-for-life.md), [td-snap-tobii-dynavox](../../business/research/competitors/td-snap-tobii-dynavox.md) use proprietary board formats, so practical lock-in remains; (d) **PECS-style protocol state** (which phase the user is in) is not part of OBF.
- **Evidence base**: No RCT or outcome study evaluates OBF/OBZ adoption; this is a standards-and-tooling fact, not a clinical evidence question.

Confidence: **high** for the standard's existence and scope; **n/a** for clinical effect.

# Parent training as predictor of success

- [johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment] — Training is a top-line modifiable success factor (alongside support, attitude, fit).
- [moorcroft2019-parent-aac-rejection], [moorcroft2019-slp-acceptance] — Parent capacity, emotional readiness, and support networks shape adoption. Training alone is necessary but not sufficient — emotional and grief work matter.
- [barker2013-aac-preschool-language] — Among preschoolers (n=71, longitudinal), *peer use of AAC* was the strongest predictor of receptive (β=0.23) and expressive (β=0.24) language growth; teacher prompting alone showed negative correlations (likely confounded with child severity). Implies that ecology — not just adult instruction — drives outcomes.
- [yau2024-aac-barriers] — Insufficient stakeholder training was named as the #1 barrier theme across parents, educators, and clinicians.
- See [pecs-evidence](pecs-evidence.md) for [howlin2007-pecs-rct] consultancy-trained-teacher results, where in-classroom expert support produced the documented effect.

**Net.** Training the *adult ecology* (parents + teachers + peers), not just delivering the device, is the strongest causal lever in the literature for both acquisition and durability. Confidence: **high** for the parent-training-matters claim; **medium** for the specific dose-response shape (no controlled training-dose trial in AAC).

# Cost, market structure, and access

- [desantos2019-meltzer-aacapps-cost] — Dedicated SGDs (Tobii Dynavox, PRC-Saltillo) USD $4,000–$12,000; iPad + locked AAC app USD $800–$2,500; software-only subscription USD $99–$499/year. US Medicaid is the dominant funder.
- [sennott2009-proloquo2go] — Tablet AAC at ~$200 vs. dedicated SGDs at ~$8000 was a 40× price collapse circa 2009; framework paper, conflict-of-interest noted.
- See competitor files for current pricing on each app: [proloquo2go](../../business/research/competitors/proloquo2go.md), [touchchat](../../business/research/competitors/touchchat.md), [td-snap-tobii-dynavox](../../business/research/competitors/td-snap-tobii-dynavox.md), [lamp-words-for-life](../../business/research/competitors/lamp-words-for-life.md), [coughdrop](../../business/research/competitors/coughdrop.md), [avaz](../../business/research/competitors/avaz.md), [speak-for-yourself](../../business/research/competitors/speak-for-yourself.md), [niki-talk](../../business/research/competitors/niki-talk.md), [araboard](../../business/research/competitors/araboard.md), [arasuite-araword-araboard](../../business/research/competitors/arasuite-araword-araboard.md), [cboard](../../business/research/competitors/cboard.md), [asterics-grid](../../business/research/competitors/asterics-grid.md), [letmetalk](../../business/research/competitors/letmetalk.md), [jabtalk](../../business/research/competitors/jabtalk.md), [lingraphica](../../business/research/competitors/lingraphica.md).

# Open questions

- **Contemporary abandonment rate.** No post-2015 controlled study quantifies AAC-specific (not all-AT) abandonment in the iPad era. The widely-cited 60%-within-1-year figure comes from a 2006 SLP survey ([johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment]) that pre-dates tablet AAC.
- **LATAM-specific evidence.** No identified RCT of any AAC system in Spanish-speaking LATAM. PECS-Spanish materials exist via Pyramid Educational Consultants but lack outcome studies. Quechua, Aymara, Guaraní, Mapudungun AAC: zero published research.
- **Bilingual AAC outcomes.** All current evidence is conceptual ([soto2014-bilingual-aac], [tonsing2020-multilingual-aac-ideology]); no controlled trials of bilingual vs monolingual AAC service delivery.
- **Active ingredient in PECS-style exchange.** Whether the gesture of physically transferring a card to a listener is separable from the symbol-and-prompting-hierarchy effect is unresolved — see [pecs-evidence](pecs-evidence.md).
- **Maintenance/generalization data.** [morin2018-hightech-aac] flagged inconsistent reporting; no AAC product has published 6/12-month maintenance evidence as standard.
- **Customization-burden quantification.** No field study measures how many minutes/week parents actually spend editing boards, or whether AI-assisted board updates change abandonment trajectories.
- **Voice output as active ingredient.** [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd] suggests early SGD beats spoken modeling alone, but the trial does not isolate whether the win is the voice output, the visual symbol, or the tap-to-speak gesture.
- **Peer-AAC distribution.** [barker2013-aac-preschool-language] is the strongest predictor study but is correlational and US-only; no intervention trial has manipulated peer AAC use as the independent variable.
- **Parent grief as a measurable construct.** Surfaced qualitatively by [moorcroft2019-slp-acceptance]; no quantitative instrument validated in AAC families.

# Builder takeaways

Implications for a parent-coached, ARASAAC-based, Spanish-first AAC product targeting LATAM:

- **Position as "as good as PECS" in clinical claims, not "better than".** The head-to-head evidence does not support a superiority claim ([morin2018-hightech-aac], [gilroy2018-pecs-vs-sgd-rct], [collette2019-proloquo2go-classroom]). Compete on cost, language coverage, portability, family-fit — see [aac-symbol-systems](aac-symbol-systems.md).
- **Stratify onboarding by user profile.** ASD-only profile defaults to SGD-style interface; ASD+IDD profile starts with exchange-style scaffolding, transitions to SGD as discrimination is mastered ([ganz2014-aac-participant-interaction], [pecs-evidence](pecs-evidence.md)).
- **Hybrid vocabulary by default.** Ship core (function words, after [banajee2003-toddler-core]) plus 10–20 personally meaningful nouns elicited at onboarding (favorite foods, family names, pets). [laubscher2020-core-vocab-review] argues pure-core boards are mismatched to early symbolic communicators. Build a Spanish equivalent of the Banajee 23-word list as a first-class research deliverable.
- **Multilingual from the data layer.** Spanish + English (US/CA latino families), Spanish + Quechua (Andean families), Spanish + Portuguese (border regions), Spanish + Aymara — architect for *n* languages on every button, allow code-switched sentence strips ([soto2014-bilingual-aac], [tonsing2020-multilingual-aac-ideology]).
- **Address the "AAC delays speech" myth in onboarding.** It is the #1 abandonment driver per [yau2024-aac-barriers]. Cite [millar2006-aac-speech] (89% gain, 0% decrease) and [schlosser2008-aac-autism-speech] directly to caregivers in the first session. This is a marketing problem, not a science problem.
- **Make customization a 5-minute setup, not a 5-hour Boardmaker session.** [moorcroft2019-parent-aac-rejection] flags "extraneous work" as a primary rejection driver. Smart defaults + family-elicited nouns + AI-assisted symbol search compress what currently requires SLP authoring.
- **Anticipate the 1-year and 5-year abandonment cliffs** ([phillips1993-at-abandonment]) with re-onboarding flows, vocabulary refresh prompts, and graceful transition modes (PECS-like → core word → text-based) inside one continuous user account.
- **Surface engagement signals to reconcile parent/professional disagreement** about why a child disengages ([yau2024-aac-barriers]): per-word touch counts, session heatmaps, time-of-day usage. This is the modifiable lever both [johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment] (failure-to-maintain construct) and [moorcroft2019-parent-aac-rejection] (functional-moment usage) point to.
- **Train the ecology, not just the user.** Parent training, peer modeling tools, and SLP collaboration are the strongest causal levers ([barker2013-aac-preschool-language], [johnson2006-aac-success-abandonment], [moorcroft2019-slp-acceptance]). Build supporter accounts (free) for parents/SLPs/teachers — this is what [coughdrop](../../business/research/competitors/coughdrop.md) does and the evidence supports it.
- **Voice output is worth the engineering cost** for minimally-verbal users in inclusion settings ([kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd]). Use offline TTS where licensing permits and provide human-recorded Spanish/Quechua voices for the early-vocabulary subset.
- **Implement OBF/OBZ import/export for the open path.** Solves real lock-in vs [coughdrop](../../business/research/competitors/coughdrop.md), [cboard](../../business/research/competitors/cboard.md), [asterics-grid](../../business/research/competitors/asterics-grid.md). It will not bridge to [proloquo2go](../../business/research/competitors/proloquo2go.md) / [td-snap-tobii-dynavox](../../business/research/competitors/td-snap-tobii-dynavox.md) — those remain proprietary. Extend OBF with a usage-data schema (per-button frequency, last-used timestamp, phase indicator) — no current standard covers this and it is what longitudinal AAC research needs.
- **Address parent grief explicitly.** [moorcroft2019-slp-acceptance] surfaced this as a novel abandonment construct. Onboarding flow that frames AAC as additive (not "giving up on speech"), validated peer-parent stories, and low-stakes early wins reduce grief-driven attrition.
- **Phase tracking as a first-class entity.** OBF doesn't carry it; [pecs-evidence](pecs-evidence.md) shows phase progression matters; build it natively. Surface to caregivers when a user has stalled (e.g., 30 days without phase advancement). Auto-suggest phase-IV sentence strip drill when phase-III discrimination is solid.
- **Run a LATAM RCT.** No published RCT of any AAC modality in Spanish-speaking LATAM — a credible academic-partnership angle (Peru, Mexico, Colombia, Chile) and a hard moat against US/EU competitors who lack regional evidence.
