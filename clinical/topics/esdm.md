# Summary
The Early Start Denver Model (ESDM, Rogers & Dawson, 2010) is the most-studied NDBI for toddlers with ASD. The foundational RCT ([dawson2010-esdm-rct], n=48) reported IQ +10.6 pts, adaptive +10.4 pts, and 29% diagnostic shift to PDD-NOS vs 5% in community comparator after 2 years of ~25 hr/week intervention. The 2020 meta-analysis ([fuller2020-esdm-meta], 12 studies, n=640) reported overall g=0.36 — strongest in cognition (g=0.41) and language (g=0.41), **null on autism symptoms (g=0.07), social communication, adaptive behavior, and stereotypies**. The 2024 NDBI overview ([song2024-ndbi-overview]) confirms ESDM as the most-evidence-supported NDBI but notes effect-size attenuation in larger replications. ESDM's intensity (15–25 hr/week × 2 yr, ~$40–70k/yr in US) is structurally incompatible with LATAM private pay; the **parent-coaching variant (P-ESDM) and the Group ESDM** are the realistic delivery models for non-US contexts.

# What it is
ESDM blends **ABA principles** (operant learning, prompting, reinforcement) with **developmental science** (Greenspan, Mahler — affect, joint engagement, social motivation) within a **play-based, naturalistic** delivery format. Curriculum spans 12–48 months developmental age across communication, social skills, imitation, cognition, play, fine/gross motor, self-care.

# Origin
- Sally Rogers (UC Davis MIND Institute) and Geraldine Dawson (now Duke) — published manual 2010, foundational RCT in *Pediatrics* same year.
- Built on Rogers's earlier Denver Model (1980s) and Dawson's social-motivation theory of autism.

# Target population
- Toddlers 12–48 months with ASD diagnosis or high autism risk.
- Originally tested in children with broad cognitive and language profiles — including with intellectual disability.
- Not designed for school-age children, though P-ESDM principles extend conceptually.

# Delivery model + intensity
- **Standard ESDM**: 15–25 hr/week therapist-delivered + parent component, 2-year duration. 1:1 in clinic or home.
- **P-ESDM (parent-delivered)**: 1 hr/week parent coaching, parent delivers throughout daily routines.
- **G-ESDM (group)**: classroom version for early-childhood centers.
- Certification: Rogers/Dawson run the official ESDM Certification through the UC Davis MIND Institute.

# Evidence summary

## Headline effects
- [dawson2010-esdm-rct] (n=48, 2-year RCT, US):
  - IQ Δ = +10.6 pts ESDM vs A/M (p=.044).
  - Adaptive behavior Δ = +10.4 pts (p=.011) — the comparator group **lost ground** on adaptive measures.
  - Receptive language Δ = +8.7 pts (p=.048).
  - Expressive language Δ = +8.1 pts (p=.033).
  - 29.2% ESDM vs 4.8% A/M shifted from autism to PDD-NOS diagnosis (p=.041).
  - **No significant effect on ADOS severity**.
- [fuller2020-esdm-meta] (12 studies, n=640):
  - Overall g=0.36 (p=0.024).
  - Cognition g=0.41 (p=0.038), Language g=0.41 (p=0.011).
  - Autism symptoms g=0.07 (NS), Social communication g=0.21 (NS), Adaptive g=0.12 (NS), Repetitive behaviors g=−0.02 (NS).
  - Heterogeneity I²=64.8% (moderate). Half of studies non-randomized.
- [song2024-ndbi-overview]: ESDM identified as most-evidence-supported NDBI, but newer multi-site replications show smaller effects than Dawson 2010 (effect-size attenuation pattern typical for first-trial dramatic findings).

## Direction
- Strongest on cognition + language at the trial level; null on autism symptoms / stereotypies / social communication / adaptive at the meta-analytic level.
- Intensity-dependent: parent-only delivery (P-ESDM) shows smaller effects than full therapist-delivered ESDM.
- Earlier start = larger effects (consistent with developmental window arguments).

# Controversies
- **Conflict of interest**: Dawson 2010 trial PIs are ESDM manual authors with royalty interests. Disclosed but baked into the foundational evidence base.
- **Replication attenuation**: early dramatic findings (especially the 29% diagnostic-shift figure) have not replicated at the same magnitude in larger multi-site trials.
- **Cost-prohibitive intensity** for non-US contexts. The ~$40–70k/year cost of full ESDM is functionally unavailable to most LATAM families.
- **"Curing autism" framing**: the diagnostic-shift finding has been used (by clinics, not the developers) to imply ESDM "treats" autism. The meta-analytic data ([fuller2020-esdm-meta] g=0.07 on autism symptoms) does not support this.
- **Comparator floor**: Dawson 2010's "assess and monitor" comparator received heterogeneous low-intensity services; the magnitude of difference is partly attributable to comparator under-dose.

# Cost
- **US**: $40,000–$70,000/year for full therapist-delivered ESDM. Insurance coverage varies; often blended with ABA billing codes.
- **EU**: Limited certified centers (UK, France, Spain, Italy, Germany). Public-system delivery rare; private cost similar to US.
- **LATAM**: Few certified ESDM centers — Brazil (SP), Mexico (CDMX, MTY), Argentina (BA), Chile (Santiago). Private cost USD $30,000–$50,000/year for partial intensity. P-ESDM parent-coaching delivery substantially cheaper (USD $1,000–$3,000 for the full programme).
- **Peru**: No officially certified ESDM provider as of 2026. Some centers reference "denver model" loosely; fidelity unverifiable. See [defensoria2023-peru-autism-barriers].

# Availability in Peru / LATAM
- Certified ESDM therapists in LATAM are scarce. UC Davis MIND Institute runs occasional Spanish-language certification cohorts.
- Public-system ESDM does not exist anywhere in LATAM.
- P-ESDM (parent-coaching) is the realistic delivery model — same evidence base as parent-mediated NDBI generally; see [parent-mediated-interventions].
- G-ESDM (group/classroom) is the structurally most-LATAM-compatible model but requires certified-staff classroom; deployment is rare.

# Tech opportunities
- **Parent coaching delivery (P-ESDM augmentation)** is the highest-leverage software wedge. Coach-in-the-loop telehealth has RCT evidence in the broader NDBI literature ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]); ESDM-specific trials of telehealth P-ESDM are ongoing.
- **Curriculum tracking**: ESDM has a structured 12–48-month developmental curriculum across 7 domains; software can deliver the assessment, target sequencing, mastery criteria, generalization probes that therapists currently track on paper.
- **Video-coding / parent-child interaction analysis**: ESDM coaching sessions involve video review of parent-child play. AI-assisted coding of joint attention, child initiations, parent responsivity reduces coach workload.
- **Play-routine prompts**: ESDM is delivered through everyday routines (feeding, dressing, bath, play). Software can prompt parents at scheduled routine times with the day's targets and example phrases / actions.
- **Caregiver fidelity scoring**: ESDM Fidelity Rating System exists; AI-augmented scoring of parent-uploaded videos is a defensible product feature.
- **Limitations**: pure-software ESDM without coach lacks evidence ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]). Build for coach + parent + child triad, not parent alone.
- **Honest claim ceiling**: cognition and language (g=0.4); not autism symptoms or stereotypies.

# Evidence
- [dawson2010-esdm-rct] — Foundational RCT, IQ + adaptive gains; diagnostic shift caveat.
- [fuller2020-esdm-meta] — 12-study meta-analysis: overall g=0.36, strongest in cognition/language, null on symptoms.
- [song2024-ndbi-overview] — Overview of NDBI meta-analyses; ESDM as anchor.
- [sandbank2020-project-aim] / [sandbank2023-project-aim-update] — NDBI as a class retains quality-adjusted effects.
- [hume2021-ebp-third-gen] — NDBI confirmed EBP.

# Open questions
- Does the diagnostic-shift finding ([dawson2010-esdm-rct]) replicate in LATAM populations or under P-ESDM (parent-only) delivery?
- Long-term (10+ year) outcomes of ESDM vs comparable-intensity ABA — direct head-to-head missing.
- Minimum effective dose: does 5–10 hr/week P-ESDM produce proportional benefit?
- Cultural adaptation of the curriculum for non-US/EU family contexts.
- Telehealth delivery RCTs — early signals positive, multi-site trials in progress.

# Builder takeaways
- **P-ESDM is the LATAM-realistic delivery model**. Therapist + parent + child triad with weekly coaching is structurally feasible; full 25-hr/week therapist delivery is not.
- **Software as the curriculum + coach layer**, not as a substitute for the parent-child interaction. Evidence supports coached telehealth; not autonomous self-directed apps.
- **Honest claim ceiling**: cognition and language gains are the empirical anchor. Frame the product around early language, joint attention, play diversity — not "improvement in autism."
- **Spanish-language ESDM materials gap** is real. UC Davis offers some Spanish content; a complete localized P-ESDM curriculum + parent-app stack is open territory.
- **Coach-in-the-loop is required**. Self-directed parent-only delivery does not have RCT support ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]); design for synchronous or asynchronous coach involvement.
- **Curriculum tracking + video review** are the two highest-leverage software components. Both reduce coach workload, both are currently paper-based, both are universally requested by ESDM clinicians.
- **Earliest start = highest yield**: marketing, partnerships, and onboarding should center the 12–30-month window. Diagnosis-pipeline integration (with pediatricians / developmental clinics) is a distribution wedge.
- **Avoid "early intervention recovery" language**. The 29%-diagnostic-shift figure is a single-trial finding, not a meta-analytic finding, and using it commercially is dishonest given [fuller2020-esdm-meta]'s null on autism symptoms.
