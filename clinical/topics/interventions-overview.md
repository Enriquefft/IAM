# Summary
The most authoritative cross-intervention synthesis as of 2026 is Project AIM ([sandbank2020-project-aim], [sandbank2023-project-aim-update], BMJ 2023, 252 studies / 13,304 children): **only NDBI retains quality-adjusted significant effects** when restricted to RCTs without high detection-bias or caregiver-report inflation. Behavioral (ABA / EIBI), TEACCH, sensory-based, and technology-based categories show positive directional effects in unadjusted analyses but lose significance under quality filtering — chiefly because their evidence is dominated by non-RCT or caregiver-report studies. Adverse events are "poorly monitored, but possibly common" across all intervention classes. Effect sizes are modest (g ~0.16–0.38 for NDBI) and concentrated on **proximal, bounded outcomes** (language, communication, play). No intervention reliably reduces autism symptom severity. The honest framing for any product is **skill acquisition, not symptom reduction**.

# Decision-tree-style guidance

**By age and presentation**:

- **0–24 months, parent newly informed of risk** → Parent-mediated NDBI (Hanen MTW, Project ImPACT, ESDM-parent-coaching). Parent education programme (EarlyBird) for orientation. The strongest evidence base for under-3s is in parent-coached delivery.
- **2–5 years, broad-spectrum diagnosis, family can sustain intensity** → ESDM or PRT first-line. PACT if symptom-severity reduction is the parent's stated goal. JASPER if joint attention is the deficit profile. Behavioral / EIBI is defensible for severe behavior or skill deficits but cost/intensity is rarely realistic in LATAM private pay.
- **2–5 years, minimally verbal** → JASPER + AAC introduction (PECS protocol or tablet SGD). Evidence converges that introducing AAC early does not impede speech ([millar2006-aac-speech], [schlosser2008-aac-autism-speech]).
- **5–12 years, school-attending** → Classroom-embedded TEACCH or SCERTS structure, plus targeted social-skills curriculum for the appropriate developmental level. PECS continuation if AAC is in use.
- **5–18 years, IQ>70, anxiety comorbid** → Adapted CBT (Coping Cat, BIACA, Cool Kids, Exploring Feelings). Strongest mental-health evidence in autism literature.
- **12–25 years, IQ>70, social isolation** → PEERS (UCLA) social skills curriculum.
- **Sensory-driven distress, OT-led** → Ayres SI is a defensible referral if the OT delivers it with fidelity and the goals are individualized (GAS); reject "sensory-based" interventions (brushing, weighted vests) that lack evidence ([lang2012-si-review]).

**By family resource constraint** (LATAM realities):

- **Private pay constrained** → Parent-mediated programmes (Hanen MTW, Project ImPACT, PACT) — lowest cost-per-child-month, evidence anchors parent behavior change. Telehealth-delivered ImPACT has RCT evidence ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]).
- **Public-system access only** → TEACCH-style classroom structures, SCERTS, PECS introduction, and group parent training are the realistic options. EIBI / 30-hr-week ESDM is structurally infeasible.

# Evidence
- [sandbank2020-project-aim] / [sandbank2023-project-aim-update] — Cross-intervention meta-analyses; only NDBI retains quality-adjusted effects.
- [hume2021-ebp-third-gen] — NCAEP third-generation EBP review identifying 28 evidence-based focused intervention practices.
- [reichow2018-eibi-cochrane] — Cochrane EIBI: weak GRADE on positive directional effects.
- [yu2020-aba-meta] — ABA-class meta: only expressive language statistically significant.
- [fuller2020-esdm-meta] — ESDM g=0.36 overall, strongest in cognition + language.
- [shi2025-teacch-meta] — TEACCH effects strongest on fine motor and ABC severity.
- [uljarevic2022-prt-umbrella] — PRT receptive language SMD=0.51.
- [sharma2021-cbt-anxiety-meta] — Adapted CBT g=0.88 clinician-rated anxiety.
- [song2024-ndbi-overview] — NDBI overview of meta-analyses; class-level evidence support.
- [floreo2025-cortica-rct-aim] — VR-augmented ABA RCT, illustrates current tech-augmentation frontier.

# Open questions
- No head-to-head RCTs comparing major intervention models against each other at adequate power.
- Adverse-event monitoring is structurally absent across the autism-intervention literature.
- Almost no LATAM-context efficacy or implementation trials for any of these interventions.
- Software-augmented versions of evidence-based protocols have minimal independent efficacy data ([sandbank2020-project-aim] technology-based g=0.05–0.42 NS in RCTs).

# Builder takeaways
- **NDBI is the highest-evidence anchor** for any developmental autism product targeting young children. ESDM, PRT, JASPER, ImPACT, PACT all sit inside this umbrella ([ndbi]).
- **Parent-mediated delivery is the leverage point**. Therapist scarcity in LATAM makes parent training the only structurally scalable channel; the evidence for parent-mediated NDBIs is the strongest for the under-5 segment.
- **Skill-acquisition framing only**. The literature does not support claims that any intervention "reduces autism severity" reliably. Frame products around language, joint attention, requesting, daily living, anxiety — outcomes the evidence supports.
- **Telehealth + coach is the empirically supported software pattern**. Self-directed parent training without coach lacks RCT evidence ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]); coached telehealth has RCT evidence.
- **Low-cost wedges**: visual schedules (TEACCH), social stories (AI-generated to Gray's criteria), parent video review (PACT), CBT-for-anxiety modules. All software-translatable, all evidence-anchored.
- **Avoid RDI and "sensory-based" interventions** as product targets — neither has sufficient evidence to anchor honest marketing.
- **Adverse events as a product feature**. Project AIM 2023's "poorly monitored, but possibly common" finding is a category-level vacuum. Any product can differentiate by tracking dropouts, distress signals, and parent-reported adverse events as a first-class data type.
