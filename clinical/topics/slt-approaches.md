# Summary
Speech-language therapy (SLT) for autistic children spans a wide modality range with **highly uneven evidence bases**. The strongest evidence is for **parent-mediated NDBI-style approaches** (PACT, Hanen More Than Words, Project ImPACT, ESDM, JASPER) targeting prelinguistic and early language ([green2010-pact-rct], [carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct], [kasari2006-jasper-rct]), with PACT showing parent synchrony d=1.22 and long-term symptom benefit ([pickles2016-pact-followup]). For minimally verbal children, **AAC introduction (PECS or SGD) does not impede speech** and supports communication ([millar2006-aac-speech] 89% gain in speech, [schlosser2008-aac-autism-speech]); see [pecs-evidence]. Traditional articulation/phonology therapy has minimal autism-specific evidence — most autism speech intervention research focuses on **communication function, not articulation**. Roughly **30% of autistic children remain minimally verbal** beyond preschool ([norrelgen2015-minimally-verbal], [tagerflusberg2013-minimally-verbal]); for this population the priority is multimodal communication (AAC + spoken word approximations) rather than articulation training alone.

# Modality landscape

## Naturalistic / developmental SLT (NDBI-aligned)
- **Hanen More Than Words (HMTW)**: parent-mediated SLT-led group programme. RCT evidence ([carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct]) shows parent responsivity Δ=0.71; child outcomes NS overall but moderated by baseline object interest.
- **PACT (Preschool Autism Communication Trial)**: SLT-coached video-review parent intervention. Largest parent-mediated RCT series ([green2010-pact-rct], [pickles2016-pact-followup], [green2022-pact-g], [carruthers2023-pact-mediation]). Parent synchrony d=1.22; long-term symptom severity benefit.
- **JASPER (Joint Attention, Symbolic Play, Engagement and Regulation)**: SLT/early-interventionist joint-attention and play intervention ([kasari2006-jasper-rct], [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd], [goods2013-jasper-pilot]). Strongest evidence for joint attention, play diversity, and (with SGD) socially communicative utterances in minimally verbal children.
- **Enhanced Milieu Teaching (EMT)**: naturalistic milieu-teaching with SLT prompting and incidental teaching. Often paired with JASPER.
- **Project ImPACT**: parent-mediated NDBI delivered by SLT or coach ([ingersoll2013-impact-pilot], [ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]). Telehealth RCT shows mediated effect on expressive language via parent strategy use.
- See [parent-mediated-interventions] for full coverage.

## AAC and minimally verbal approaches
- **PECS**: 6-phase picture-exchange protocol. EBP for functional/requesting communication ([howlin2007-pecs-rct] OR=2.72); see [pecs-evidence].
- **Speech-Generating Devices (SGDs)**: tablet apps (Proloquo2Go, TouchChat, LAMP Words for Life, CoughDrop). Evidence comparable to PECS for ASD-only populations ([ganz2014-aac-participant-interaction], [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd]). See [aac-symbol-systems], [aac-apps-landscape].
- **Aided language modeling**: SLT/parent uses AAC system to model language while talking. Supported within broader AAC literature.
- **Core vocabulary approaches**: focus on high-frequency function words rather than nouns. [banajee2003-toddler-core] (23 core words = 96% of toddler utterances), [laubscher2020-core-vocab-review].

## Articulation and phonology
- Traditional articulation therapy (e.g., motor-based, minimal-pairs) has **minimal autism-specific evidence**.
- Most autism SLT research treats articulation as secondary to communication function.
- Childhood Apraxia of Speech (CAS) in autism is under-diagnosed; PROMPT therapy and DTTC have evidence in CAS broadly but not autism-specific.

## Pragmatic / social communication
- **Social communication interventions** (eye gaze, turn-taking, conversational repair) overlap with social-skills training. See [social-skills-training].
- **Comic Strip Conversations** (Carol Gray): no controlled evidence; included as practice convention.
- **Video modeling for conversation**: NCAEP-confirmed EBP at component level ([hume2021-ebp-third-gen]).

## Echolalia, gestalt language processing
- **Gestalt Language Processing (GLP) framework** (Marge Blanc, derived from Barry Prizant's earlier work on echolalia): treats echolalic / scripted speech as a developmental pathway to generative language rather than as deviance to be eliminated.
- **Evidence base is currently practitioner consensus + qualitative**; no RCTs as of 2026.
- Aligned with neurodiversity framing — has rapidly gained adoption in US/EU SLP community 2020–2026 despite thin formal evidence.

# What it is — common to most SLT approaches
- **Therapist**: Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP, US) / Speech-Language Therapist (SLT, UK/EU/LATAM). Master's-level credential in most jurisdictions.
- **Setting**: clinic, school, home, telehealth.
- **Intensity**: typically 1–3 sessions/week × 30–60 min (private practice); higher in early-intervention programmes.

# Origin and target population
- Modern autism-specific SLT emerged from the social-pragmatic / NDBI tradition (Wetherby, Prizant, Mahoney) in the 1980s–2000s.
- Target population spans full autism spectrum and ages — emphasis varies by modality (NDBI for under-5s; AAC for minimally verbal across ages; pragmatic interventions for verbally fluent autistic children and adolescents).

# Delivery model + intensity
- **Therapist-direct**: 1:1 sessions, 1–3 hr/week typical.
- **Parent-mediated**: SLT coaches parent across weeks/months; parent delivers in daily routines.
- **Classroom-embedded**: SLT consultation model; teachers deliver under SLT guidance.
- **Telehealth**: increasingly common post-2020; evidence base growing ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]).

# Evidence summary

## Headline effects
- [carter2011-hanen-mtw-rct]: HMTW parent responsivity Δ=0.71 (5 mo), 0.50 (9 mo); child outcomes NS overall.
- [green2010-pact-rct]: PACT parent synchrony d=1.22; child initiations d=0.41; primary autism-symptom outcome NS at 13 mo.
- [pickles2016-pact-followup]: PACT long-term ADOS severity benefit at 6-year follow-up — first evidence of sustained autism-symptom effect from a parent-mediated intervention.
- [kasari2014-jasper-emt-sgd]: Adding SGD to JASP+EMT increased socially communicative utterances over JASP+EMT alone in minimally verbal 5–8-year-olds.
- [millar2006-aac-speech]: AAC introduction associated with speech gain in 89% of cases (n=27 best-evidence subset).
- [schlosser2008-aac-autism-speech]: AAC neither impedes nor harms speech in autism; supports introduction at any communication stage.
- [hume2021-ebp-third-gen]: Naturalistic intervention, modeling, prompting, video modeling, AAC, PECS, social-narrative all confirmed EBPs at component level.

## Direction
- Strongest evidence in parent-mediated NDBI for prelinguistic / early language.
- AAC introduction supports rather than harms speech.
- Articulation-focused therapy under-evidenced in autism.
- Approximately **30% of autistic children remain minimally verbal** at school age ([tagerflusberg2013-minimally-verbal]) — multimodal communication is the priority for this subgroup.

# Controversies
- **Articulation vs. communication function**: many LATAM private SLPs default to articulation-focused therapy with autistic clients; the evidence base supports communication-function focus for early intervention.
- **AAC delays speech** myth — explicitly debunked by [millar2006-aac-speech] and [schlosser2008-aac-autism-speech], yet remains the #1 reason families and SLPs delay AAC introduction ([yau2024-aac-barriers]).
- **Gestalt Language Processing**: rapidly adopted framework with thin formal evidence; its growth in clinical practice is outpacing the research literature.
- **Eye-gaze and "look at me" targets**: traditional SLT often included eye-contact prompting; neurodiversity critique has led to dropping these targets in many practices.
- **Echolalia framing**: previously framed as deficit to extinguish (in some ABA traditions); now more often framed as developmental pathway (GLP) or communicative function.

# Cost
- **US**: $100–$250/session SLP private practice. Annual cost USD $5,000–$25,000 typical.
- **EU**: NHS / public coverage varies; private similar to US.
- **LATAM**: USD $30–$80/session in private practice. Annual cost USD $1,500–$5,000.
- **Peru**: Lima SLP private practice USD $40–$100/session. CPAL (Centro Peruano de Audición, Lenguaje y Aprendizaje) is the long-established SLT center. Public-system SLT minimal. See [defensoria2023-peru-autism-barriers], [delacernaluna2024-peru-rebagliati].

# Availability in Peru / LATAM
- SLP/SLT widely available in private practice in capital cities.
- Hanen-certified SLTs present in MX, AR, CL, BR, CO. Peru has limited Hanen presence.
- PECS-Spanish materials distributed by Pyramid Educational Consultants; certified consultants in MX, BR, CL.
- Tablet AAC adoption growing — Proloquo2Go Spanish, JABtalk Spanish, ARASAAC-based apps (LetMeTalk, AraBoard).
- Public-system SLT severely under-resourced across LATAM.

# Tech opportunities
- **Parent-coaching apps for NDBI delivery** (PACT-style video review, ImPACT-style strategy library, Hanen-style family education). Highest evidence anchor; see [parent-mediated-interventions].
- **AAC apps for minimally verbal users** — mature category but Spanish-first / LATAM-cultural-fit gaps remain. See [aac-apps-landscape], [aac-symbol-systems].
- **Communication temptation activities** (planned, recordable, parent-implemented). Software can prompt, scaffold, and capture child responses for SLT review.
- **Video review with AI-assisted coding** (joint attention, child initiations, parent responsivity) — offloads SLT clinical workload.
- **Speech sample logging** — parent records child utterances over a week; software auto-categorizes as requesting / commenting / responding / scripted, exports to SLP report.
- **Telehealth-coached SLT** — RCT-supported delivery model ([ingersoll2024-impact-telehealth]); LATAM internet access constraints noted.
- **Language modeling library** — short videos of SLT modeling aided language input for parents to imitate. Spanish-first, LATAM-context.
- **GLP framework support** — gestalt collection logging, stage-based progression tracking. Practitioner adoption is growing despite thin evidence; ride the wave with explicit evidence-tier labeling.
- **Articulation-only is a weaker product wedge** — limited autism-specific evidence; the leverage is in communication function and parent-mediated delivery.

# Open questions
- Direct head-to-head RCTs among parent-mediated NDBIs (PACT vs HMTW vs ImPACT) — outstanding.
- GLP outcome trials — none as of 2026.
- LATAM adaptation studies — minimal published evidence.
- Articulation-focused autism intervention RCTs — essentially absent.
- Optimal sequencing: when to introduce AAC for late-talking toddlers (currently judgment-based, not evidence-calibrated).

# Builder takeaways
- **Parent-mediated NDBI delivery is the strongest evidence anchor** for autism-specific SLT software. PACT, Hanen MTW, ImPACT all sit in this category.
- **AAC introduction as default for minimally verbal children** — explicit content debunking the "AAC delays speech" myth is a high-leverage parent-education wedge.
- **Communication function over articulation** for early autism intervention. Marketing should emphasize "first words / first requests / first initiations" over "clear speech."
- **Spanish-first language modeling library** is a content-only wedge with minimal technical complexity and high LATAM utility.
- **Coach-in-the-loop telehealth** is the empirically supported delivery pattern.
- **GLP support is a "ride the wave" opportunity** — practitioner adoption growing fast; evidence still thin. Build with explicit evidence-tier labeling.
- **Avoid claiming "treats autism speech delay"**. The defensible claims are: supports parent strategies, increases communication initiations, supports AAC introduction.
- **Distribution channel**: private SLP practices in LATAM are the natural early adopters. Plus parent-direct via app stores. Public-system entry requires longer sales cycle but addressable through MINEDU-style education ministries.
