# Summary
DIR/Floortime (Developmental, Individual differences, Relationship-based; Stanley Greenspan, 1979) is a parent-led, child-directed, affect-driven model emphasizing six functional emotional developmental capacities. The systematic review ([divya2023-dir-floortime-review], 12 studies 2010–2020, **only 1 RCT**) reports significant gains in communication, parent-child interaction quality, and adaptive behavior with no formal pooled effect sizes. Project AIM 2020/2023 grouped DIR under "developmental interventions" — the **developmental category retained quality-adjusted effects** in [sandbank2023-project-aim-update] (g=0.28 on social communication). Confidence in DIR specifically remains **low** because the evidence base is parent-self-report-heavy with minimal independent RCT replication. The model has high parent-acceptability and strong neurodiversity-aligned framing (child-led, affect-driven, no compliance training), making it a popular LATAM private-practice option despite weak research backing.

# What it is
- **DIR** = Developmental, Individual differences, Relationship-based: a clinical framework for understanding child development through six "Functional Emotional Developmental Capacities" (FEDCs):
  1. Self-regulation and shared attention
  2. Engagement and relating
  3. Two-way intentional communication
  4. Complex problem-solving (reciprocal back-and-forth)
  5. Symbolic play / using ideas
  6. Logical / abstract thinking
- **Floortime** = the play-based intervention technique within DIR — the adult literally joins the child on the floor, follows the child's lead, and works to "open and close circles of communication."

# Origin
- Stanley Greenspan (psychiatrist, NIMH then Washington DC) and Serena Wieder, late 1970s.
- ICDL (Interdisciplinary Council on Development and Learning) maintains certification.
- Conceptually adjacent to RDI (Gutstein) and PACT (Aldred/Green) — all relationship-development models.

# Target population
- Originally young children (2–6) with broad developmental concerns including ASD.
- Now applied across the spectrum and into adolescence in some practices.
- Particularly emphasized for children with significant sensory/regulatory differences.

# Delivery model + intensity
- **Parent-delivered**: ICDL recommends 6–10 sessions/day of 20-minute Floortime interactions, distributed across the day.
- **Therapist-delivered**: certified DIR practitioner runs sessions and coaches parents.
- **Multidisciplinary**: OT, SLP, mental-health typically integrated.
- Total recommended dose: ≥20 hours/week of Floortime engagement — though this is rarely measured in studies.

# Evidence summary

## Headline effects
- [divya2023-dir-floortime-review] (12 studies, 2010–2020, only 1 RCT, narrative synthesis):
  - Significant gains in communication and parent-child interaction quality.
  - Enhanced adaptive behavior and self-care in some studies.
  - No adverse events reported.
  - Effect sizes not formally pooled (heterogeneity prevented).
- [sandbank2023-project-aim-update]: "Developmental interventions" category (which includes DIR) retained quality-adjusted effects on social communication g=0.28 — but this is at the category level, not DIR-specific.
- No Cochrane review of DIR specifically as of 2026.

## Direction
- Positive directional signals on communication, parent-child relational quality, and adaptive behavior.
- No reliable autism symptom severity effect.
- Effect-size data essentially absent — DIR's research base remains qualitative and case-study-dominated.

# Controversies
- **Single-RCT problem**: in 12 studies of DIR over a decade, only 1 was randomized. The model is more clinically popular than research-evaluated.
- **Self-report dominance**: most outcome data are parent-completed measures, which Project AIM identifies as a quality-inflation pattern.
- **Greenspan's broader claims**: Greenspan's books extended DIR-like principles to general childhood education (*The Child with Special Needs*, *Engaging Autism*) without strong supporting trial evidence.
- **Floortime vs. ABA debate**: DIR is often framed as the "humanistic alternative to ABA" in parent communities. The actual evidence is too thin to support either superiority claim, but the framing drives adoption.
- **Conflict with NDBI literature**: NDBIs (ESDM, JASPER, PRT) incorporate developmental + behavioral mechanisms with stronger evidence; DIR's pure-developmental positioning is increasingly outflanked by NDBI's hybrid approach.

# Cost
- **US**: ICDL certification ~$3,000–$5,000 for practitioners. Family cost: $80–$200/session for therapist-delivered Floortime + OT/SLP. Annual cost USD $10,000–$30,000 for combined services.
- **EU**: Less common; some adoption in UK, Spain, Italy.
- **LATAM**: **Highly popular in private practice** in Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia. Cost USD $30–$80/session for parent coaching.
- **Peru**: Floortime-affiliated practices in Lima exist (often combined with OT). Certified DIR practitioners scarce; many providers use the term loosely.

# Availability in Peru / LATAM
- One of the more available "non-ABA" interventions in LATAM private practice.
- ICDL Spanish-language training expanded in late 2010s; certified practitioners in MX, BR, AR, CL.
- Peru: a few Lima-based practitioners advertise DIR; certification status varies.
- High parent demand driven by (a) anti-ABA sentiment and (b) DIR's relationship-focused framing being culturally compatible with LATAM family-centered values.

# Tech opportunities
- **Parent coaching layer**: prompts and "follow the child" cue libraries — affect-coding, circle-of-communication tracking, two-way-engagement scaffolds. Low-cost build, high alignment with DIR principles.
- **Video review**: like PACT and ESDM, DIR coaching is video-heavy. AI-assisted coding of parent-child interaction (turn-taking, affect synchrony, circles opened/closed) is software-tractable.
- **FEDC tracking**: the 6-capacity framework is structured enough to convert into a developmental-tracking dashboard (where is the child currently, what are the next-zone targets).
- **Routine integration**: DIR specifies multiple short Floortime sessions per day. A parent-app reminder + brief in-session prompt covers the structural gap.
- **Affect / regulation sensors**: DIR emphasizes regulation as Capacity 1; wearable HRV / EDA data could feed a dyadic-regulation dashboard. See [hardware-landscape] for sensor stack.
- **Limitations**: DIR's evidence base does not support specific outcome claims. Frame the product as "supporting parent-child engagement and communication" — not "improving autism symptoms."
- **Open licensing**: DIR is not a closed protocol with licensing fees. Software can adopt DIR-aligned principles without legal barrier (unlike PEERS, Hanen, ESDM curriculum).
- **Differentiation positioning**: a DIR-aligned, neurodiversity-friendly, relationship-focused product is a credible LATAM positioning **alternative** to ABA-branded products.

# Evidence
- [divya2023-dir-floortime-review] — 12-study systematic review; 1 RCT; positive directional signals; no pooled effect sizes.
- [sandbank2023-project-aim-update] — Developmental category (includes DIR) retains quality-adjusted effects on social communication g=0.28.
- [hume2021-ebp-third-gen] — DIR not explicitly named as an EBP; component practices (modeling, prompting) are.

# Open questions
- High-quality RCT of DIR vs active comparator with assessor-blinded outcomes — outstanding for 40+ years.
- Dose-response: the recommended ≥20 hr/week of Floortime is not empirically calibrated.
- Independent (non-Greenspan-lineage) replication of the model's specific claims about FEDC progression.
- Cultural adaptation: DIR was developed in middle-class US clinical context; LATAM family routines and play patterns differ.
- Effect-size estimation: meta-analyzable data still absent.

# Builder takeaways
- **DIR is high-acceptability, low-evidence**. Software targeting DIR's principles can ride the parent-acceptability wave but should not over-claim outcomes.
- **The real evidence anchor is the broader developmental / NDBI category** ([sandbank2023-project-aim-update] g=0.28 on social communication) — frame the product accordingly.
- **No licensing barrier** makes DIR-aligned design legally cheap. ICDL does not enforce protocol licensing the way Hanen or PEERS does.
- **Relational positioning vs ABA** is the LATAM marketing wedge — neurodiversity values, child-led, no compliance training. Pair with honest evidence framing to maintain credibility.
- **Software components**: parent prompting library, affect/turn-taking video coding, FEDC dashboard, routine reminder system.
- **Pair with stronger-evidence components**: a product can deliver DIR-style parent prompting wrapped around evidence-anchored components (PACT-style video review, JASPER-style joint-attention targets, TEACCH-style visual schedules). The DIR framing is the brand layer; the evidence-anchored mechanics are the product.
- **Be honest about the single-RCT gap**. Internal team should know — and external claims should reflect — that DIR's evidence base is thinner than its market presence suggests.
