# Status

Research file, not legal advice. Chilean counsel required before launch. Chile is in major regulatory transition: Ley 21.719 (2024) replaces the long-standing Ley 19.628 with most provisions taking effect 2026-12-01. Ley TEA (21.545, 2023) is the most comprehensive autism law in LATAM. Several timing/operational items are `Open question` until Reglamento and APDP appointments crystallize.

# Data protection

## Primary law and transition
- **Ley 19.628 sobre Protección de la Vida Privada** (1999) — the legacy law, currently in force.
- **Ley 21.719** — published Diario Oficial 2024-12-13. Substantially modifies Ley 19.628 and creates the **Agencia de Protección de Datos Personales (APDP)**. <https://www.bcn.cl/leychile/navegar?idNorma=141005>.
- Most provisions enter into force **2026-12-01** (24-month deferred start). Reglamento window: 6 months from publication for issuance. Civil Registry to clear specified databases within 20 months.
- Authority transition: APDP is the new independent regulator, replacing the prior fragmented oversight. Real sanctioning power.

## Sensitive data and health
- Ley 21.719 introduces explicit category of `datos sensibles` including health, biometric, and personal beliefs.
- Requires elevated security and processing safeguards for sensitive data.
- Health data — heightened protection regime; exceptions narrow, similar to GDPR Article 9 architecture.

## Children and minors
- Ley 21.719: data of `niños, niñas y adolescentes` is subject to strict rules; **consent given by minors has no legal validity**.
- Chile thus takes a `parental-only` approach for processing minors' data via consent — no GDPR-style digital age threshold (no 13/14/15/16). The legal representative must always provide consent for minors' processing where consent is the basis.
- Open question: Reglamento may define narrow age-based capacity carve-outs; not yet published.

## Penalties
- APDP classifies infractions as leves, graves, muy graves.
- Maximum fines: **up to 20,000 UTM** for muy graves (~CLP 1.4 billion in 2025; ~USD 1.4M). Tripled for recurrence.

# Medical device classification

## Authority and primary instruments
- **ISP** — Instituto de Salud Pública de Chile.
- **Decreto Supremo 825/1998** — Reglamento de Control de Productos y Elementos de Uso Médico. <https://www.bcn.cl/leychile/navegar?idNorma=141005>.
- Código Sanitario art. 102 et seq.
- MINSAL has run consultations to expand the regime to additional devices (2024+); Open question on final scope.

## Risk classes
- DS 825/1998: 4 classes by risk (per ISP):
  - **Clase I** — riesgo muy bajo.
  - **Clase II** — riesgo moderado.
  - **Clase III** — alto potencial de riesgo.
  - **Clase IV** — más críticos.
- Verification of conformity issued by ISP after compliance with quality, safety, performance standards.
- Source: <https://www.ispch.gob.cl/pregunta-frecuente/p-237/>.

## Software as a Medical Device
- DS 825 covers `productos y elementos de uso médico`. Software with medical purpose falls within this scope when intended for diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, or treatment.
- Chile has not published a published, English/Spanish dedicated SaMD guidance equivalent to FDA's General Wellness or MDCG 2019-11. Practice: align with IMDRF.
- Open question: Chile's evolving control regime under MINSAL consultation may explicitly address SaMD.

## Wellness / education boundary in Chile
The line is set by intended purpose. Chile has no published wellness safe-harbor.

### Disqualifying claims
- "Diagnostica TEA" — producto de uso médico.
- "Trata el autismo" — producto de uso médico.
- "Detecta crisis sensoriales con biosignales" — producto de uso médico.
- "Reduce síntomas TEA" — producto de uso médico.
- Screening / tamizaje claims — **especially sensitive in Chile** because Ley 21.545 imposes a State duty to develop tamizaje for TEA (see below). Private claims of tamizaje may be read as offering a regulated service.

### Likely-wellness claims
- "Apoyo educativo y comunicacional" — wellness/education.
- "Documentación pasiva para reflexión del cuidador" — wellness.
- "Apoyo a las rutinas familiares" — wellness/education.

# Disability and autism — substantive rights framework

## Ley 21.545 — Ley TEA Chile (2023)
- Promulgated 2023-03-02. Establece la promoción de la inclusión, la atención integral y la protección de los derechos de las personas con TEA en el ámbito social, de salud y educación. <https://www.diarioconstitucional.cl/2023/03/13/ley-no21-545-que-establece-la-promocion-de-la-inclusion-la-atencion-integral-y-la-proteccion-de-los-derechos-de-las-personas-con-trastorno-del-espectro-autista-en-el-ambito-social-de-salud-y-educac/>.
- Principios: trato digno, autonomía progresiva, perspectiva de género, intersectorialidad, participación, detección temprana, **neurodiversidad**.
- **Health:** MINSAL must develop and promote access to tamizaje / detección de señales de alerta TEA within child/adolescent health services.
- **Education:** State duty to ensure inclusive quality education for all NNAA and adults with TEA — access, participation, permanence, progression.
- **Labor:** parents/guardians of TEA minors get statutory right to attend emergencies in educational settings (Código del Trabajo + estatutos administrativos).

## Ley 20.422 — Igualdad de oportunidades e inclusión social de personas con discapacidad (2010)
- SENADIS — Servicio Nacional de la Discapacidad.
- Art. 3 — accessibility principle.

## Ratificación CRPD — Decreto 201/2008.

## Consent and minors
- Código Civil + Ley 19.620 (adopciones) framework. Minors act through legal representatives; capacity scaled by age.
- Health: Ley 20.584 — Derechos y Deberes en la Atención de Salud — minors' assent and parental consent.
- For data (under Ley 21.719): parental consent always; minor cannot validly consent.

# Accessibility / education

- Ley 20.422 art. 3, 23–25 — accessibility obligations across sectors; State web accessibility per Decreto 41/2017 (Ministerio Secretaría General de la Presidencia) — Norma Técnica que adopta WCAG 2.0 AA for State digital services.
- Open question: extension of WCAG mandate to private commercial apps — not yet binding.
- Education: Ley 20.845 (Inclusión Escolar) + Decreto 83/2015 (diversificación de la enseñanza); Decreto 170/2009 (necesidades educativas especiales).

# Marketing & advertising claims

## Authority
- ISP — health-product advertising.
- SERNAC — Servicio Nacional del Consumidor — under Ley 19.496 (Ley del Consumidor).
- CNTV — Consejo Nacional de Televisión for broadcast.

## Rules
- Ley 19.496 art. 28 — prohibits publicidad falsa o engañosa, with specific reference to health, security, environmental claims.
- Ley 19.733 (libertades de información y opinión) — advertising rules.
- Health-claim regulation: ISP must register dispositivos and approve advertising for regulated products. Unregistered products cannot make device claims.
- Children-targeted advertising — Ley 20.606 (composición nutricional) is illustrative; Convención de los Derechos del Niño governs more generally.

## Concrete rules for the autism wellness/education app
- No diagnostic/therapeutic/screening claims about TEA without ISP registration.
- Be especially careful about `detección` or `tamizaje` framings — Ley 21.545 makes tamizaje a State health-system function, so private claims to perform tamizaje are higher risk.
- Verifiable parental consent for child-image use; from 2026-12-01, parental consent for **all** minor data processing under Ley 21.719.
- SERNAC enforcement on consumer apps has been active; class actions under Ley 21.081 (reform of Ley del Consumidor).
