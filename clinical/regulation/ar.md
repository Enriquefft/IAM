# Status

Research file, not legal advice. Argentine legal counsel required before launch. Argentina has the most mature autism-specific health-coverage law in LATAM (Ley 27.043 + Decreto 777/2019 + PMO inclusion). Several SaMD/wellness boundary questions are open as ANMAT continues to issue guidance.

# Data protection

## Primary law
- **Ley 25.326 — Protección de Datos Personales** (2000). <https://www.oas.org/juridico/pdfs/arg_ley25326.pdf>.
- Decreto Reglamentario 1558/2001.
- Authority: **AAIP** — Agencia de Acceso a la Información Pública (autonomous since 2017). DNPDP — Dirección Nacional de Protección de Datos Personales is the operative arm.
- Argentina holds an EU adequacy decision (since 2003) — relevant for cross-border data flows.

## Sensitive data and health
- Ley 25.326 art. 2: `datos sensibles` = data revealing racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious/philosophical/moral convictions, union affiliation, **information about health or sexual life**.
- Ley 25.326 art. 7: sensitive data may only be collected when (a) circumstances of general interest authorized by law exist, or (b) statistical/scientific purposes with anonymization. **No one can be obligated to provide sensitive data.**
- Art. 8 — health data: public/private health establishments and licensed health professionals may collect/treat health data of patients they have attended, respecting professional secrecy. **Wellness apps are not health establishments and do not benefit.**

## Consent
- Art. 5 — consent must be free, express, informed, and evidenced in writing or by equivalent means depending on circumstances.
- Tacit consent for non-sensitive personal data is allowed in narrow circumstances; **never for sensitive data.**

## Children and minors
- For minors and incapable persons, processing requires documentation of parental authority, guardianship, or representation.
- No statutory digital age threshold equivalent to GDPR art. 8. AAIP has issued guidance treating minors' data as deserving heightened protection but the formal rule is parent-consents.
- Código Civil y Comercial (CCC) art. 26: adolescents 13–16 may consent to non-invasive treatments not endangering health; 16+ treated as adults for autonomous decisions about their body.

## Reform pipeline
- A new draft Personal Data Protection Bill has been in discussion since 2023, aiming for GDPR alignment (digital age threshold, DPO requirement, breach notification, neural data). **As of 2026-05 not yet enacted (Open question).**

## Penalties
- Art. 31: warnings, suspension, fines from ARS 1,000 to ARS 100,000 (figures historically updated by AAIP resolution).
- AAIP has been increasingly active on cross-border data transfer infractions.

# Medical device classification

## Authority and primary instruments
- **ANMAT** — Administración Nacional de Medicamentos, Alimentos y Tecnología Médica.
- INAME / INPM — institutes within ANMAT for medical devices specifically.
- **Disposición ANMAT 2318/2002** (T.O. 2004) — Reglamento Técnico MERCOSUR para el Registro de Productos Médicos. <https://www.argentina.gob.ar/normativa/nacional/disposici%C3%B3n-2318-2002-75867>.
- Disposición ANMAT 727/2013 — products of "uso médico continuo" requirements.
- **Disposición ANMAT 9688/2019** — current registration procedure rules. <https://www.boletinoficial.gob.ar/detalleAviso/primera/222600/20191203>.
- Disposición 2198/2022 and Disposición 8194/2023 — periodic updates.
- Disposición ANMAT 64/2025 — most recent procedural update.

## Risk classes
- Disposición 2318/2002 — 4 classes (I, II, III, IV) per MERCOSUR rules. 18 classification rules.
  - **Clase I** — bajo riesgo. Notificación.
  - **Clase II** — riesgo moderado-bajo.
  - **Clase III** — riesgo moderado-alto.
  - **Clase IV** — alto riesgo.

## Software as a Medical Device
- Disp. 2318/2002 explicitly includes computer-based media (software) used to handle a medical device or that has influence in its utilization. SaMD is treated as a producto médico when it has medical purpose.
- ANMAT issued `Software como Producto Médico (SaMD)` consultation document — see <https://opinionpublica.anmat.gob.ar/proyectos/5293.pdf>. Working toward dedicated SaMD framework; **not yet formalized as binding regulation as of 2026-05 (Open question).**

## Wellness / education boundary in Argentina
The line is the manufacturer-declared finalidad. ANMAT jurisdiction triggers when intended for diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, treatment, alleviation, or compensation of disease/injury/disability.

### Disqualifying claims
- "Diagnóstico de TEA" — producto médico.
- "Tratamiento del autismo" — producto médico.
- "Detección de meltdowns por biosignales" — producto médico.
- "Reduce síntomas del TEA" — producto médico.
- Screening/tamizaje claims — producto médico.

### Likely-wellness claims
- "Apoyo educativo y de comunicación" — wellness/education.
- "Documentación de lo que el usuario muestra" — wellness.
- "Apoyo a las rutinas familiares" — wellness.

### Boundary cases
- Recommending professional consultation upon observed pattern — likely permissible if generic, not clinical-decision-support output (Open question).

# Disability and autism — substantive rights framework

## Ley 27.043 — Abordaje integral e interdisciplinario del TEA
- Sancionada 2014-11-19; promulgada de hecho 2014-12-15. <https://servicios.infoleg.gob.ar/infolegInternet/anexos/240000-244999/240452/norma.htm>.
- Declares of national interest the integral interdisciplinary approach to TEA. Promotes early diagnosis, early intervention, training, and research.
- **Decreto Reglamentario 777/2019** — operationalized after 5-year delay. <https://www.boletinoficial.gob.ar/detalleAviso/primera/221428/20191120>.
- **Mandatory coverage (PMO):** social security, prepaid medicine entities, and other providers must cover screening, early detection, diagnosis, and treatment of TEA. Treatments incorporated into the Programa Médico Obligatorio.

## Ley 26.378 — Convención de la ONU sobre derechos de personas con discapacidad
- Aprobación de la CRPD; rango supralegal vía art. 75.22 CN.
- Ley 27.044 (2014) — confiere jerarquía constitucional a la CRPD. Highest possible status.

## Ley 26.061 — Protección integral de los derechos de las niñas, niños y adolescentes (2005)
- Best-interest principle, autonomía progresiva.

## Ley 22.431 + Ley 24.901 — sistema de prestaciones básicas para personas con discapacidad
- Coverage framework for habilitation and rehabilitation services.

## Consent and minors
- CCC art. 26: minors 13–16 may consent to non-invasive treatments not endangering health; for invasive/risky treatments, parents must consent and the adolescent must provide consent jointly. 16+ treated as adults for body-related decisions.
- For data processing in a minors' wellness app: parental consent + adolescent's informed assent appropriate to maturity.

# Accessibility / education

- **Ley 26.653 — Acceso de la información pública** (2010): mandates accessibility for State web content per ONTI Disposición 6/2019, which adopts WCAG 2.0/2.1.
- Private sector apps not directly bound by web-accessibility law but expected to follow general non-discrimination principles (Ley 23.592).
- Ley 26.378 (CRPD) — substantive accessibility obligation flows through this.
- Education: Ley 26.206 — Ley de Educación Nacional, art. 42–45 — educación inclusiva. Resolución CFE 311/16 — promoción y certificación de estudiantes con discapacidad.

# Marketing & advertising claims

## Authority
- ANMAT — health-product advertising oversight via Decreto 1490/1992 art. 8.
- Secretaría de Comercio (depending on government structure) — consumer protection under Ley 24.240.
- COFEMA — federal consumer-environment matters.

## Health claims
- Resolución 627/2007 (Ministerio de Salud) — standards for advertising of productos médicos and medicamentos.
- Disp. ANMAT 4980/2005 — advertising of producto médico is restricted; non-registered products cannot make device claims.
- Ley 24.240 art. 4 — duty of clear and truthful information; art. 8 bis — dignified treatment.
- Children-targeted advertising — Ley 26.061 + Convención de los Derechos del Niño (constitutional).

## Concrete rules for the autism wellness/education app
- No diagnostic, therapeutic, screening, or detection claims about TEA without ANMAT registration.
- No use of identifiable child imagery without verifiable parental consent.
- Marketing must respect the framing of the app as `educativo` or `de apoyo familiar`, not `clínico` or `terapéutico`.
- Argentina has the deepest autism-specific health regime in LATAM (Ley 27.043) — providers serving patients with TEA who treat the family will need to consider PMO interactions when partnering with social-security/obras sociales/prepaga.
