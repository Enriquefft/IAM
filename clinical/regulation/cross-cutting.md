# Practical guidance — how to be safe in PE first, then in all 8 jurisdictions

## What a Peru-first wellness/education app must do

The Peru-first launch is the easiest jurisdiction to stay on the right side of regulation, because Peru has no published wellness safe-harbor and instead defers to general intended-purpose rules — meaning a clean wellness/education positioning is unproblematic if marketing is disciplined. The risk is sloppy marketing language that triggers DIGEMID + INDECOPI exposure. Concretely, to stay outside DIGEMID jurisdiction:

1. **Claim only educational, documentation, and family-support outcomes.** Use words like `educativo`, `aprendizaje`, `apoyo familiar`, `documentación de lo observado`, `rutinas`, `comunicación familiar`, `bienestar`. Do not use `diagnóstico`, `tamizaje`, `tratamiento`, `terapia clínica`, `intervención clínica`, `detección`, `evaluación clínica`, `cura`, `reduce síntomas`.
2. **Do not produce outputs that look like clinical reasoning.** A passive log of what the user shows the app is wellness; an automated assessment that scores the child against a clinical instrument is SaMD. The line is: does the app interpret data clinically, or does it merely organize it for human reflection?
3. **Do not market screening for autism.** This is unambiguously SaMD across all 8 jurisdictions. Even framings like `detecta señales de alerta TEA` are dangerous.
4. **Always recommend professional consultation generically** if the app surfaces anything about the child. Frame as "Si tienes preocupaciones, consulta a un profesional de salud o educación", not "Tu hijo presenta indicadores de X — consulta a un especialista".
5. **Comply with Reglamento DS 016-2024-JUS:** parental consent for under-14, simple-language consent for 14+, written consent for sensitive data including health, identity verification on online consent, 48-hour breach notification, DPO appointment per phased timeline, secure international data transfers via SCC equivalents.
6. **Comply with Ley 29973 art. 23:** ship with WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility from day one. Do not wait for State enforcement.
7. **Comply with INDECOPI advertising rules:** no health claims without scientific substantiation (Ley 26842 art. 119); no exploitation of minors' image; no testimonials without verifiable parental consent.

## What additional gates open when you expand to other jurisdictions

The Peru-clean positioning is the necessary condition; here are the marginal additions:

- **MX:** Add full LFPDPPP + LGPDPPSO compliance. If selling B2B to public schools or IMSS, LGPDPPSO triggers. Avoid implying any diagnostic/therapeutic function — COFEPRIS will read your marketing.
- **CO:** Build "ser escuchado" age-appropriate disclosure into the consent flow for any minor user. Register the database with RNBD if asset thresholds met.
- **AR:** Strong CCC art. 26 layering — adolescent 13+ may have a voice in their own data processing, even if Ley 25.326 is silent. Argentina has the deepest TEA-coverage regime (Ley 27.043 + PMO) — partner with obras sociales / prepagas if going B2B requires understanding PMO inclusion.
- **EC:** Highest LATAM digital age threshold (15 for direct consent), but for sensitive data **always** parental — even for 15+. Stricter than the rest.
- **CL:** Wait for or design around Ley 21.719 (effective 2026-12-01) — minors' consent is invalid; parental only. Avoid `tamizaje` framing because Ley 21.545 makes State responsible for autism screening — private claims are higher risk. SERNAC active enforcement.
- **US:** COPPA verifiable parental consent for under-13. State-by-state privacy laws (CA, WA, MD, NY, CO, CT priority). Washington My Health My Data Act has private right of action — operationally significant. FDA General Wellness Policy 2026 is the most permissive safe-harbor; design specifically to fit its two-factor test (intended only for wellness + low risk + noninvasive). FTC Health Breach Notification Rule (2024) covers any unauthorized disclosure of PHR data including ad-tech sharing.
- **EU:** Configurable digital age threshold per member state (13/14/15/16). MDCG 2019-11 rev. 1 (2025) is binding interpretive guidance; align language to it. AI Act Art. 5(1)(b) prohibits exploitation of vulnerabilities based on age + disability — implies design-for-trust review for any AI-driven UX targeting autistic children. EAA in force 2025-06-28 — WCAG 2.1 AA / EN 301 549 mandatory for new consumer products. DSA Art. 28 prohibits targeted advertising to minors based on profiling.

## Universal posture (true in all 8 jurisdictions)

- Treat health-related personal data as the most sensitive category. Always require explicit, granular, written/electronic-signature consent.
- Treat parental consent as the safe default for all minor users; layer adolescent-direct-consent only where local law permits AND infrastructure supports identity verification.
- Maintain a documented intended-purpose statement consistent across labelling, marketing, in-app copy, app store listings, ToS, and partner agreements. Inconsistency is what regulators key off.
- Maintain a record of the wellness positioning rationale (what the product does NOT do) for inspection by any of the 8 regulators.
- Plan for accessibility (WCAG 2.2 AA target) regardless of which jurisdiction nominally requires it. EAA will pull the world there by default.
- Engage local legal counsel **before** any public marketing in each jurisdiction. The cost of pre-launch counsel is trivial vs. the cost of unwinding a SaMD or sensitive-data finding.

# Open questions that block full clarity

- **PE:** What identity-verification mechanism for online minor consent does ANPDP accept under DS 016-2024-JUS?
- **PE:** Will DIGEMID issue SaMD-specific guidance analogous to MDCG 2019-11?
- **MX:** Final status of INAI post-2025 constitutional reform; which body inherits enforcement?
- **MX:** When does COFEPRIS publish a binding SaMD guidance document?
- **CO:** Status of legislative proposals for a Ley TEA Colombia.
- **AR:** Status of the new Personal Data Protection Bill (GDPR-aligned).
- **EC:** Final form of the 2025 ARCSA draft replacing Resolución 026-2016.
- **CL:** Reglamento under Ley 21.719 — capacity carve-outs for adolescents, if any.
- **US:** COPPA 2.0 / KOSA enactment.
- **EU:** Application of AI Act high-risk classification to educational autism apps — guidance pending from AI Office.
